\section{Notes on the formalization}
\label{sec:formalization-notes}

This chapter records the wrong turns the formalization of
Katoh--Tanigawa's Theorem~5.5~\cite{katohTanigawa2011} took on the way
to the argument the earlier chapters present: statements whose formal
strength diverged from the source's, decompositions that did not
survive contact with the assembly they were meant to feed, and
obligations left with no artifact tracking them. Three patterns recur
across most of the sections below and are named in the next paragraph.
\Cref{sec:formalization-notes-scaffolding-arc} is a scaffolding
narrative the three-way classification does not apply to at all, and
\cref{sec:formalization-notes-mismodelling-walls} groups two episodes
by a shared symptom instead of by one pattern, each episode there
self-classifying separately. Within a section, an episode narrates
what a check did and did not read at the time a divergence was
introduced, and the check that eventually caught it. Because the
subject here is the formalization itself, Lean declarations, their
types, and short code excerpts appear directly in the prose below,
rather than only as parenthetical citations, as they do in the rest of
this document.

A \emph{genuine mis-factoring} is an internal decomposition --- an
abstraction layer, a splice of two arguments, a case split --- that did
not match the structure the final assembly needed, so the decomposition
was replaced without changing the underlying mathematics. A
\emph{source-faithfulness correction} is a Lean statement whose formal
strength diverged from Katoh--Tanigawa's --- weaker, stronger, or
differently quantified --- found and corrected without invalidating any
proof already built on it. A \emph{process failure} is a decision that
was sound when made but left with no artifact tracking it, or an error
that no check running at the time could have read.

Commits are cited by an eight-character abbreviated SHA linking to the
corresponding commit on GitHub; every cited commit postdates the
project's 2026-05-13 move to a standalone repository.

\subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$d = 3$}{d = 3}-first scaffolding arc}
\label{sec:formalization-notes-scaffolding-arc}

The episode below sits outside the three-way classification above: it is
not a mis-factoring, a source-faithfulness correction, or a process
failure, but a scaffolding choice, refined twice at contact with a build,
that left nothing already proved to redo.

Katoh--Tanigawa present Case~III (KT~\S6.4, Theorem~5.5's hardest case)
twice: concretely at $d = 3$ (\S6.4.1, Lemma~6.10, a fixed dispatch on
four vertices $v, a, b, c$), then at general dimension (\S6.4.2,
Lemma~6.13, a length-$d$ chain $v_0 v_1 \dots v_d$), with the remark
that ``the proof strategy is exactly the same as $d = 3$'' (p.~692). The
formalization took the same order, and made the choice explicit rather
than assumed, at Phase~22c's second design-recon pass
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/6511a01abb7b7dd4ad1b7b077fc2e341d1d635b7}{\texttt{6511a01a}},
2026-06-05): $d = 3$ first, following ``KT's own \S6.4.1-then-\S6.4.2
cut,'' with the general-$d$ lift assigned to its own later phase before
any of the concrete case was built.

The same pass staged the $d = 3$ choice again, one level down, rather
than attempting Lemma~6.10 --- KT's longest proof, roughly twelve
printed pages --- as a single build: it split the lemma into three
difficulty strata (the eq.-(6.12) degenerate placement, giving a rank
one short of full; the redundant-row argument of Claim~6.11, named the
genuinely new research crux; and Claim~6.12's candidate-completion
finish), and committed only the first stratum to the sub-phase then
opening (Phase~22c itself). The three strata were then built in turn
across Phases 22c--22h, entirely inside the concrete carrier --- every
panel normal a literal \texttt{Fin 4} vector, every screw a literal
element of the six-dimensional $\bigwedge^2\R^4$, arithmetic such as
\texttt{screwDim 2} $= 6$ closing by \texttt{decide} --- closing with
the $d = 3$ Case-III node itself reaching green, modulo a small number
of explicitly carried hypotheses later discharged in subsequent
sub-phases
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/b9a45ea909479a5d88e34a7030434141b09ff6e5}{\texttt{b9a45ea9}},
2026-06-11, closing Phase~22h).

Once the $d = 3$ construction reached this state, a design pass drew a
reuse map against it before any general-$d$ Lean was written
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/036804b2a715e8eddc2e5f60d746a7e958028550}{\texttt{036804b2}},
2026-06-07): Claim~6.11's redundant-row mechanism
(\texttt{exists\_redundant\_panelRow\_ab\_of\_finrank\_eq}) and
Lemma~2.1's extensor-span finish were read as reusable verbatim once
re-stated at general grade; the exterior-algebra meet API
(\texttt{complementIso}, \texttt{topEquiv}, \texttt{pairingDualEquiv})
needed only dimension-parametric re-statement; and the fixed
three-candidate dispatch, together with the bespoke $\bigwedge^2\R^4$
duality, were marked for wholesale replacement by a $d$-candidate chain
dispatch and a $\bigwedge^{d-1}(\R^{d+1})$ duality --- the same
one-dimensional top-exterior-power fact at a different grade, not the
general Hodge-star construction Katoh--Tanigawa never invoke. The same
map recorded one further claim: that the realization spine underlying
all three cases --- the genericity device, \texttt{theorem\_55}'s
skeleton, and Cases~I and~II --- was already general and green, the
graph-side combinatorics being dimension-free throughout.

That last claim did not survive Phase~23's own opening design pass
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/6ea8a69147abc813afd33ab1d9d00ba6c2660cb7}{\texttt{6ea8a691}},
2026-06-17). Re-reading every spine declaration against its landed Lean
grade found \texttt{theorem\_55\_d3}, \texttt{case\_III\_realization},
and \texttt{case\_III\_candidate\_dispatch} all carrying
\texttt{ScrewSpace 2} and \texttt{Fin 4} literally: dimension-parametricity
held for the graph-side bookkeeping the earlier map had checked, not for
the panel-geometric spine the induction is actually stated over. The
correction split the remaining work along a fault line the earlier map
had not drawn --- a mechanical lift of the pinned spine to symbolic
\texttt{screwDim k}, kept apart from the genuinely new chain dispatch and
duality --- and scheduled the lift first, as sub-phase 23a: not because
it was mathematically hard, but because the chain dispatch and the
duality both had to be \emph{stated} at general grade, which presupposes
a general-grade spine to state their conclusions over, and because the
symbolic-$k$ arithmetic underlying \texttt{screwDim k} --- concrete and
decidable at $k = 2$, symbolic thereafter --- was the one named risk of a
build-time blocker, best surfaced before new mathematics was built on
top of it.

The same correction repeated at a finer grain the same day. Lifting the
spine's own closing declaration found that four further producers ---
the base, cut-edge, Case-I, and forgetful-map realizations --- were
themselves \texttt{Fin 4}-pinned through the same $\bigwedge^2\R^4$
duality lemma, and so were not liftable by the numeral pass that
generalized the rest of the spine
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/ee1674dfef3789d3fe9d71415be1626e3d2d51e6}{\texttt{ee1674df}},
2026-06-17, closing sub-phase 23a). They were carried forward as four
explicit hypotheses, the project's standing green-modulo idiom, to be
discharged once the general chain-dispatch duality became available.

None of these three revisions --- the $d = 3$-first cut itself, the
reuse map, and its correction at Phase~23's own opening --- reopened
anything already proved: the $d = 3$ construction stayed green
throughout, and Phase~23 closed
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/623846d0f6d545035812d1c6159da4fea309febe}{\texttt{623846d0}},
2026-07-02) with Conjecture~1.2 stated as a theorem, along exactly the
division the corrected map had drawn. Building the hardest case
concretely first did not remove the need to re-scope its
generalization; it moved the re-scoping to a point where a whole worked
case, not a paper reading alone, was there to check the scope against.

\subsection{Statement-faithfulness episodes}
\label{sec:formalization-notes-statement-faithfulness}

The episodes below are source-faithfulness corrections in the sense of
the introduction: a Lean statement's formal strength diverged from
Katoh--Tanigawa's, and the divergence was found and repaired without
invalidating proofs already built against it.

\subsubsection{A realization predicate weaker than its prose}

KT Theorem~5.5 asserts that a minimal $k$-dof graph admits a
panel-hinge realization whose rigidity matrix attains the maximal rank.
In KT's definition (KT~\S5.1) each hinge is a line: a nonzero
$2$-extensor contained in both endpoint panels. The formalization
introduced the realization predicate in June 2026
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/1cd26ccee6d8412a55740f2d9661934b5456701a}{\texttt{1cd26cce}})
as
\begin{alltt}
def HasFullRankRealization (k : \(\N\)) (G : Graph \(\alpha\) \(\beta\)) : Prop :=
  \(\exists\) Q : PanelHingeFramework k \(\alpha\) \(\beta\), Q.graph = G \(\wedge\)
    Q.toBodyHinge.RankHypothesis 0
\end{alltt}
with no condition on the hinge extensors. The omission makes the
predicate satisfiable for every connected graph. The framework
assigning every hinge the degenerate endpoint pair $(a_0, a_0)$ has all
support extensors zero; the constraint at an edge $uv$, that the screw
difference $S_u - S_v$ lie in the span of the edge's extensor, then
reads $S_u = S_v$, so on a connected graph every infinitesimal motion is
constant and the rank is the full $D(|V|-1)$. The realization conjunct
of the formalized Theorem~5.5 was satisfied by this witness
independently of the theorem's content.

The predicate had been introduced as rank bookkeeping for the induction
of KT~\S5 and was not compared against KT's definition of realization,
either at its introduction or at the one later redesign that reworked
it (the two-motive split of
\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/e4693d6170908c480e4f06924f2ea0a95ce9ba3f}{\texttt{e4693d61}}'s
design pass re-read KT on the simple-versus-non-simple axis and did not
pose the question). The blueprint did not expose the divergence: the
definition's dependency-graph node was marked formalized beside prose
at the paper's strength, and none of the per-commit checks compared a
definition's Lean body with its prose --- the name-resolution check
verifies that pinned declarations exist, and the hypothesis audit reads
a theorem's hypotheses, of which a definition has none.

The divergence was found by a pre-build audit of the theorem's
hypothesis feeds
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/e4693d6170908c480e4f06924f2ea0a95ce9ba3f}{\texttt{e4693d61}},
2026-06-11), before any construction was built against the degenerate
witness. It was a weakness of statement selection rather than of proof:
the general-position motive introduced at the two-motive split is at
KT's strength, and the rank arguments beneath the bare conjunct
required no rework. The repair was scheduled at the motive redesign
that the same audit had already forced, so the definition surface was
reshaped once rather than twice, and landed as
(\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/652ea99ffe115bd932f7b9da3324473cd8001d0f}{\texttt{652ea99f}})
\begin{alltt}
def HasPanelRealization (k n : \(\N\)) (G : Graph \(\alpha\) \(\beta\)) : Prop :=
  \(\exists\) (F : BodyHingeFramework k \(\alpha\) \(\beta\)) (normal : \(\alpha\) \(\to\) Fin (k + 2) \(\to\) \(\R\)),
    F.graph = G \(\wedge\)
    (\(\forall\) v \(\in\) V(G), normal v \(\neq\) 0) \(\wedge\)
    (\(\forall\) e u v, G.IsLink e u v \(\to\) F.supportExtensor e \(\neq\) 0 \(\wedge\)
      ExtensorInPanel (F.supportExtensor e) (normal u) \(\wedge\)
      ExtensorInPanel (F.supportExtensor e) (normal v)) \(\wedge\) \dots
\end{alltt}
whose nonzero-normal and nonzero-extensor conjuncts are the
nondegeneracy conditions of KT's definition. The check this produced is
the cheapest-witness audit (\texttt{blueprint/CLAUDE.md}, the
definition-faithfulness gate): when a definition modeling a paper
notion is marked formalized, its blueprint prose is read against the
cheapest satisfying witness of the Lean body, not against the paper.

\subsubsection{Katoh--Tanigawa Lemma 4.1's over-quantified statement}

Katoh--Tanigawa's own argument for the degree-of-freedom bookkeeping a
splitting-off or a vertex removal induces, on the way to Theorem~4.9, is a
forest surgery, \cite[Lemma 4.1]{katohTanigawa2011} (p.~660; the 2009 arXiv
predecessor, Lemma~5.1, p.~11, is essentially verbatim): ``for \emph{any}
independent set $I$ of $M(\tilde G)$ \dots there exists $I'$ \dots with
$|I'| = |I| - D$.'' Formalizing this quantifier literally, rather than
paraphrasing it into the base-restricted form the induction actually needs,
exposed at once that it cannot hold as written: for an independent $I$ with
$|I| < D$ --- $I = \emptyset$ suffices --- the promised $|I'|$ would have to
be negative. The disproof was recorded as a Lean witness rather than quietly
paraphrased away, in the same commit that added the remark distinguishing
the finding's three layers (\cref{rem:kt-lemma-41}) and re-pointed
Theorem~4.9's degree-of-freedom tracking off the forest surgery entirely
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/f0934296677f6e0e535394c5400ca3645db53e34}{\texttt{f0934296}},
2026-06-03). It was introduced as an anonymous \texttt{example}; a later
cleanup pass promoted it to a named lemma so the corresponding blueprint node
could pin it directly, replacing a comment noting it was an anonymous,
unpinnable example
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/bc579c3f753c90d645699863308db2f8ce1d3929}{\texttt{bc579c3f}}):
\begin{alltt}
theorem kt_lemma_41_overquantified (n : \(\N\)) (hD : 1 \(\le\) bodyBarDim n) :
    \(\neg\) \(\exists\) I' : Set (\(\beta\) \(\times\) Fin (bodyHingeMult n)),
      I'.ncard + bodyBarDim n =
        (\(\emptyset\) : Set (\(\beta\) \(\times\) Fin (bodyHingeMult n))).ncard
\end{alltt}
(\texttt{bodyBarDim n} is $D$; \texttt{bodyHingeMult n} the per-edge
multiplicity of $\tilde G$'s edge fibers.)

A second, subtler layer came with the same finding. Katoh--Tanigawa's proof
of the intended, base-quantified reading converts each of the $D$ forests of
a chosen packing by removing one edge, silently assuming the packing is
\emph{balanced} at the degree-2 vertex $v$ under surgery --- every forest
already meets one of $v$'s two incident edges. Neither the 2011 paper nor its
2009 predecessor justifies this, and the finding first recorded it as an open
question. It resolved positively later the same day: a counting bound (a
base of $M(\tilde G)$ meets at least $D$ of $v$'s $2(D-1)$ incident fibers)
together with a redistribution move (a forest avoiding $v$ has $v$ isolated,
so it absorbs a free fiber as a pendant without creating a cycle) and a
finite descent construct a balanced packing for every base and every
$D \ge 2$
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/398ce7acb13ff7949848203ee9f63e03a5ded1e4}{\texttt{398ce7ac}}).
By the time this second layer closed, though, Theorem~4.9's tracking no
longer needed it: the same finding commit had already re-pointed the
induction onto a direct partition-count argument through the
def\,$=$\,corank bridge (\cref{thm:def-eq-corank}, \texttt{rank\_add\_deficiency\_eq},
Phase~19), discharged within the same session, bypassing the forest surgery.
The repaired surgery survives as a sound, off-critical-path formalization of
KT~4.1's splitting-off direction; the removal direction, KT~4.2, stays
deferred, needed by neither route.

This is a divergence in the source's own statement, not in how the Lean
represented it: what the induction actually consumes --- the base-quantified
deficiency inequality --- is exactly Katoh--Tanigawa's intended content, and
stating that content precisely, rather than transcribing the paper's literal
quantifier, was the formalization's task. Full three-layer statement and
repair: \cref{rem:kt-lemma-41}.

\subsubsection{A fixed-candidate disjunction for Claim 6.12's free line choice}

Katoh--Tanigawa's Claim~6.12 \cite[\S6.4.1]{katohTanigawa2011} (eqs.~(6.30)--(6.45))
underlies the $d = 3$ Case-III realization: three candidate placements
$p_1, p_2, p_3$ are built by splitting off along a \emph{freely chosen}
$(d-2)$-affine line --- ``we may choose any $L \subset \Pi(a)$,
$L' \subset \Pi(b)$, $L'' \subset \Pi(c)$'' --- and the claim is that
\emph{for some} choice at least one of the corresponding blocks $M_1, M_2,
M_3$ has full rank. The capstone formalizing this,
\texttt{case\_III\_claim612}, first modeled the claim not at a free line but
at the three \emph{specific} lines the realization producer happens to
build (the split edges $va$, $vb$, $ac$), with conclusion $r(C_1) \ne 0 \lor
r(C_2) \ne 0 \lor r(C_3) \ne 0$ for the three corresponding fixed
extensors, discharged contrapositively through an explicit duality
hypothesis
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/56aef7c487b6e2ad63903f09ece3b487a4efefdb}{\texttt{56aef7c4}},
2026-06-07):
\begin{alltt}
theorem case_III_claim612
    \{r : Module.Dual \(\R\) (ScrewSpace 2)\} (hr : r \(\neq\) 0)
    \{C\(\sb{1}\) C\(\sb{2}\) C\(\sb{3}\) : ScrewSpace 2\}
    \{p : Fin 4 \(\to\) Fin 3 \(\to\) \(\R\)\} (hp : AffineIndependent \(\R\) p)
    (hduality : r C\(\sb{1}\) = 0 \(\to\) r C\(\sb{2}\) = 0 \(\to\) r C\(\sb{3}\) = 0 \(\to\)
      \dots) :
    r C\(\sb{1}\) \(\neq\) 0 \(\vee\) r C\(\sb{2}\) \(\neq\) 0 \(\vee\) r C\(\sb{3}\) \(\neq\) 0
\end{alltt}
Tracing what discharges \texttt{hduality} two days later found the fixed
antecedent mathematically unusable, not merely incomplete: $C_1, C_2, C_3$
are three vectors in the six dimensions of $\bigwedge^2 \R^4$, so
``$r \perp C_1, C_2, C_3$'' can never force $r = 0$, while Katoh--Tanigawa's
own text confirms the three lines are freely chosen, not graph-fixed --- so
Claim~6.12 is a genuine existential over that continuum, not a disjunction
at three fixed points
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/82b3b50af7462aeba2e081608b92a3d43cd31aba}{\texttt{82b3b50a}}).
The repair restated the capstone as the existential it actually is, over the
six pairwise joins of four affinely-independent points, which do span
$\bigwedge^2 \R^4$ (\cref{lem:extensor-independence}, KT Lemma~2.1) even
though three fixed lines cannot
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/d5ff364821581017823283d12f7c15b311f7a9b4}{\texttt{d5ff3648}},
2026-06-09):
\begin{alltt}
theorem case_III_claim612
    \{r : Module.Dual \(\R\) (ScrewSpace 2)\} (hr : r \(\neq\) 0)
    \{p : Fin 4 \(\to\) Fin 3 \(\to\) \(\R\)\} (hp : AffineIndependent \(\R\) p) :
    \(\exists\) q : \{q : Fin 4 \(\times\) Fin 4 // q.1 < q.2\},
      r \(\langle\)omitTwoExtensor (fun i => homogenize (p i)) (ne_of_lt q.2),
        extensor_mem_exteriorPower _\(\rangle\) \(\neq\) 0
\end{alltt}
The realization producer consumes the existential by building its candidate
placement so its hinge line \emph{is} the witness join's line. A later
generalization to arbitrary $d$ subsumed this $d = 3$-specific form into
\texttt{case\_III\_claim612\_gen}, the declaration the blueprint node
\cref{lem:case-III-claim612} pins today.

Unlike Lemma 4.1's divergence, which sits in Katoh--Tanigawa's own text, this
one originated in the Lean's internal decomposition of the claim: modeling a
free line choice as a disjunction over three graph-fixed candidates was a
shape choice, not a mistranscription of KT's statement, and the fix
corrects that shape --- which happened also to be mathematically
undischargeable, not merely a looser reading of the source.

\subsection{Abstraction-layer mis-factorings}
\label{sec:formalization-notes-abstraction-layer}

The episodes below are genuine mis-factorings in the sense of the
introduction: an internal decomposition --- an abstraction layer, a splice of
two arguments --- that did not match the structure the final assembly
needed, so the decomposition was replaced without changing the underlying
mathematics.

\subsubsection{The Claim 6.12 candidate-row producers}

Katoh--Tanigawa's Claim~6.12 \cite[\S6.4.1]{katohTanigawa2011}, introduced above
(\cref{sec:formalization-notes-statement-faithfulness}), supplies the row that completes
Case~III's rank count: the redundant-row argument of Claim~6.11 falls one
row short of the target, and Claim~6.12 supplies the last row from one of
three candidate splittings $p_1, p_2, p_3$. Phase~22e (2026-06-06)
formalized the second and third candidates as abstract row-family
linear-independence theorems, quantified over an arbitrary
\texttt{BodyHingeFramework}, not over any concrete construction:

\begin{alltt}
theorem linearIndependent_sum_p2_candidateRow (F : BodyHingeFramework k \(\alpha\) \(\beta\)) (e : \(\beta\))
    \dots (hr : \(\rho\) (F.supportExtensor e) \(\neq\) 0) :
    LinearIndependent \(\R\)
      (Sum.elim (Sum.elim rn (fun _ : Unit => hingeRow v b \(\rho\))) ro)
\end{alltt}
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/9b8a6c6779d9ee0ebeaac479372deb555ca17e77}{\texttt{9b8a6c67}},
2026-06-06; the symmetric third candidate, split at the other degree-2 body
along $ac$, landed the same day as
\texttt{linearIndependent\_sum\_p3\_candidateRow},
commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/87ea12952bc67dde7aae11968da1061a28b3b40c}{\texttt{87ea1295}}).
Both are graph-free: \texttt{F} is an arbitrary abstract framework, and each
theorem concludes independence of the row family the candidate completion
assembles, packaged for whichever concrete carrier eventually supplies the
surrounding old and new row blocks \texttt{rn}/\texttt{ro}.

A design pass the following day planned to consume these producers
directly: compose the abstract row-family independence with a fixed-framework
device-feed brick, so that discharging the witness condition \texttt{hr}
would close the realization
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/a893639add286e64d176bde5a4fb2431289a8820}{\texttt{a893639a}},
2026-06-07). Four days later, reading Katoh--Tanigawa's own eq.~(6.29) sketch
against that plan found it was not literally transferable: the composed
family has to be consumed at the one-parameter shear family Case~III's
construction actually builds, and the certified family the row-family
producers supply is not of that shape (the candidate row, and part of the
transported bottom family, are not rows of the sheared candidate at $t \neq
0$). The corrected route instead certifies the target rank at the fixed,
unsheared framework $F_0$, converts the count to a rank lower bound,
re-extracts a literal \texttt{panelRow} family from that bound, and
transfers \emph{that} family along the shear
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/5aff13201353fe8a2ebf70ca69b18416f507aea7}{\texttt{5aff1320}},
2026-06-11 --- ``certify-then-rebase''). The rank certification this needs,
\texttt{case\_III\_rank\_certification}, landed the same day
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/f6856538177df6cfe5365a06c63a2feae2fffb72}{\texttt{f6856538}}),
and the three arm closers that consume it ---
\texttt{case\_III\_arm\_realization} ($M_1$),
\texttt{case\_III\_arm\_realization\_M2} ($M_2$,
commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/2981429e19315fe4ed2dc9c3aad60c22719ee2c3}{\texttt{2981429e}}),
and \texttt{case\_III\_arm\_realization\_M3} ($M_3$) --- each read their
target rank bound off the certification, not off
\texttt{linearIndependent\_sum\_p2\_candidateRow} or
\texttt{\_p3\_candidateRow}: their own doc-comments record this explicitly,
factoring the arm closer as the part that depends only on the certified rank
bound and the split/seed data, ``not on how that rank was certified''.

Neither producer, nor their selector packagings, ever gained a caller
outside the file that defines them once the certify-then-rebase route
landed: a later audit during the Phase-26 cleanup round found both
producers and their selectors dead against the live Lean call chain into
the theorem, alongside the $d = 3$ candidate dispatch they were built to
feed
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/fe2f2a73c04e8bd0f557d1b30d0f704c31d54967}{\texttt{fe2f2a73}},
2026-07-07). They were kept rather than deleted --- they ground the worked
$d = 3$ case exposition the blueprint's Case~III chapter presents, even
though the theorem no longer builds through them --- but the abstraction
itself, correct and graph-free as built, never reached the assembly it was
factored to feed. This is a genuine mis-factoring: the row-family layer was
mathematically sound at every stage, and what replaced it is a different
decomposition of the same rank count, not a correction to its content.

\subsubsection{A motion-space splice for Katoh--Tanigawa's block-triangular rank-addition}

Katoh--Tanigawa's Case~I inductive step \cite[eq.~(6.3)]{katohTanigawa2011} adds the ranks of two
separately-placed rigidity matrices: a rigid subgraph $H$ realized at its
own generic placement contributes $D(|V(H)|-1)$ rows, and the contraction
$G/E(H)$ realized at \emph{its own} generic placement contributes the rest,
the two ranks adding because each block occupies disjoint columns once the
other is projected away. No step of KT's argument asks for one placement
simultaneously realizing both legs.

Phase~21b's first Case-I composer nonetheless took two same-placement
rigidity outputs as hypotheses --- the rigid subgraph and the contraction
both rigid on the \emph{same} witness placement, with no Lean lemma
constructing such a placement --- flagged the same day by a
blueprint-honesty audit as premature
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/615638e47acc520ba9c3ca7b0c024b21c44cf3c4}{\texttt{615638e4}},
2026-06-04). The repair that followed built an honest, satisfiable glue
lemma in place of assuming the common placement outright:

\begin{alltt}
theorem isInfinitesimallyRigidOn_of_splice (F : BodyHingeFramework k \(\alpha\) \(\beta\))
    \dots
    (hblock : (F.withGraph GH).IsInfinitesimallyRigidOn sH)
    (hcontract : (F.withGraph Gc).IsInfinitesimallyRigidOn sc) :
    F.IsInfinitesimallyRigidOn t
\end{alltt}
(\texttt{lem:case-I-splice-seed},
commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/5b6f5a6a63a7c14d8137e7987bda6d6a744c7814}{\texttt{5b6f5a6a}},
2026-06-04). This is a locally sound modelling choice --- KT eq.~(6.3) read
as two overlapping rigid pieces glued along their shared body --- and it is
genericity-free, exactly as KT's own splice is. But \texttt{hblock} and
\texttt{hcontract} are both stated of the \emph{same} framework \texttt{F}:
the glue demands one placement simultaneously rigid on both legs, where KT's
block-triangular structure places each leg independently and adds the
resulting ranks.

Phase~22a's realization producer, built on this glue, then had to supply
that common-seed rigidity, and tried to do so across two re-plans on the
same day before the divergence was named, each producing a hypothesis that
type-checked but was not dischargeable. The first composer bundled a
placement-independent
complement-isolation equality --- for every placement $q$, the pinned-motion
dimension at the contraction's surviving body set $s_c$ equals $D \cdot
|s_c^{\complement}|$ --- found generically false the same day: off the full
vertex set, the contraction leg's interior bodies still carry surviving
boundary-edge constraints, so the equality fails
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/c95d7bc09f1a2125ae4cd11eeb33d7c33bcdfefc}{\texttt{c95d7bc0}},
2026-06-05). An asymmetric-coupling repair deleted that equality and fed the
contraction leg's rigidity directly from a universally-quantified
general-position hypothesis --- for every general-position placement $q$,
the leg is rigid at $q$ --- and a second verification pass the same day
found this equally undischargeable: general position is pairwise normal
independence, strictly weaker than the maximal rank the hypothesis demands
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/85f5b0540faad20070bbc2219edcb5fa21721924}{\texttt{85f5b054}},
2026-06-05). Both passes had taken the glue's shape as fixed and tried to
satisfy its obligation; neither questioned the glue itself.

The divergence, once named, was structural rather than a missing
hypothesis: the project's rigidity model is a motion-space predicate ---
every infinitesimal motion constant on a body set --- and the glue had
translated KT's rank-addition into a motion-space statement that needs a
common witness KT's own argument never constructs. The repair replaced the
glue's obligation with a row count read off a \emph{single} framework's
rigidity-row span directly --- independent rows in each block, rather than
rigidity of each leg --- eliminating the common-seed demand by construction
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/f504955c127d13bd7766c7146b3f7601a8be560f}{\texttt{f504955c}},
2026-06-05, the block-triangular reframe; \cref{lem:case-I-splice-seed} and
\cref{lem:case-I-realization} record the corrected form). The reframe's own
first cut restated the surviving block's obligation with the same
universal quantifier over general position again --- the same
over-quantification recurring one level down --- corrected the same day to
a rank-polynomial non-root condition, KT's genuine Zariski-open genericity
locus
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/c9750bdfe3993b8c7fbc6bd69b8a9fffa6de15fb}{\texttt{c9750bdf}},
2026-06-05).

This is a genuine mis-factoring: the motion-space model that carried
Phases~4--16 without incident re-expressed KT's block-triangular
rank-addition as a different argument with a different, harder obligation,
and the tell across both re-plans was the same --- the arithmetic closed,
but each pass needed a fresh hypothesis to bridge a gap Katoh--Tanigawa's
own proof does not have.

\subsection{Walls from mis-modelling}
\label{sec:formalization-notes-mismodelling-walls}

The two episodes below share a symptom rather than one cause: each is a
point at which the formalization's chosen approach turned out to be unable,
in principle, to produce the fact the next step needed --- a wall, in the
sense that no further building along the same route would have closed it
--- and each was diagnosed only once the target quantity was traced
directly through the source construction, rather than by a check of the
surrounding declarations' types. The two do not share one classification
from the introduction's three-way taxonomy; each states its own below.

\subsubsection{The member-mapping wall}

Katoh--Tanigawa's proof of Case~III at general dimension (KT~\S6.4.2,
Lemma~6.13, the length-$d$ chain $v_0 v_1 \dots v_d$) certifies the rank
added at each induction step $i$ by a $D \times D$ corner block $M_i$, whose
rank adds to a base block's to reach the full $D + D(|V|-2) = D(|V|-1)$
(eqs.~(6.64)--(6.65)). The corner's second row is one fixed vector $r \in
\R^D$ at every step $i$ --- held fixed by a degree-two cancellation, not
transported across the induction (eq.~(6.66)) --- and the construction
shows some choice of the corner's first row makes $M_i$ invertible against
$r$, over a free line each step is built from (eq.~(6.67)).

The formalization's first approach to the general-$d$ step (Phase~23b,
opened 2026-06-17) modeled this certification as a transported span
membership instead: hold one witness row's membership in the span of
already-certified rows fixed, and carry it forward across the induction.
Several distinct routes were tried in turn --- a per-step fold along the
chain, a fresh re-derivation of the redundancy at each candidate, a
re-shaping of the base realization consuming Katoh--Tanigawa's column
operation directly --- and each, checked against what that operation
actually does, reached the same obstruction: Katoh--Tanigawa's own column
operation (eq.~(6.62)) relabels which of the base graph's rows plays the
corner's redundant-row role as $i$ advances, rather than fixing one witness
row whose membership a single argument keeps certifying; a route asking for
one witness row's membership to survive past the first step therefore has,
in the text, nothing to derive it from
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/591cf306465c63b0fa13c55fab4b17a38f131cf0}{\texttt{591cf306}},
2026-06-21, the last of these routes to be refuted this way).

Reshaping the certification itself to Katoh--Tanigawa's own block form ---
rather than transporting a membership at all --- was sketched in outline
that same afternoon
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/50fb322968bf7ade9596de46b958a80c1fdde3b7}{\texttt{50fb3229}},
2026-06-21): certify the rank as a block matrix whose corner is $M_i$ and
whose bottom is the base rows with the one redundant row removed, holding
$r$ fixed only as an equation between two vectors in $\R^D$, never as a
transported membership. Confirming that this was the \emph{only} escape
took three more days: Phase~23c, opened that same afternoon, spent them
checking whether the existing certification could instead be fed the
needed fact some other way --- a different base block, a different
re-derivation of the redundancy --- and found every such feed reduced to
the identical obstruction. It closed at what the design notes call the
member-mapping-wall STOP
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/4efcabf5f8611bcc88aceea701a735eca8c45ba1}{\texttt{4efcabf5}},
2026-06-24): the wall was confirmed intrinsic to Katoh--Tanigawa's own row
bookkeeping, not an artifact of one Lean encoding, and the block-form
reshape was confirmed the only route around it.

Turning that reshape into a literal Lean certificate took two more false
starts once building resumed. A bottom block built from the split-off graph
with the reduced vertex removed failed, because that graph's rows are not
literally contained in the candidate's own rigidity-row span (the candidate
graph lacks the corresponding edge); and a construction following the
project's column operation directly failed for the same underlying reason
approached from the other side, zeroing the wrong block and stranding the
fill row the bottom needs in the block the certificate discards. Both were,
on inspection, one obstruction restated: a certificate whose corner and
bottom occupy disjoint row blocks of the candidate graph's own edges cannot
express Katoh--Tanigawa's argument, which shares one row's data between the
corner's $\pm r$ entry and the bottom's redundant-row fill. The shape that
dodges it --- zero in the \emph{other} corner, so a single row operation on
the corner side, not the bottom, produces the needed block-triangular form
--- was found and landed axiom-clean, closing Phase~23e
(commits~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/a02b8c58807768cb967eba57d78d3c3bc61fb2fd}{\texttt{a02b8c58}}
and~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/f4c7ae579969cb3634ec4789daad3cfa3c823c41}{\texttt{f4c7ae57}},
both 2026-06-26):

\begin{alltt}
theorem finrank_span_rigidityRows_ge_of_edge_submatrix_fromBlocks_zero\(\sb{1}\)\(\sb{2}\)
    (F : BodyHingeFramework k \(\alpha\) \(\beta\)) \dots
    (Lrow : Matrix \dots \(\R\)) (hLrow : IsUnit Lrow.det)
    (U : Matrix \dots \(\R\)) (hU : IsUnit U.det)
    (re : m\(\sb{1}\) \(\oplus\) m\(\sb{2}\) \(\to\) \dots)
    (en : (n\(\sb{1}\) \(\oplus\) n\(\sb{2}\)) \(\simeq\) \dots)
    (hblock : (Lrow * F.rigidityMatrixEdge ends hgp * U).submatrix re en
      = Matrix.fromBlocks A 0 C D)
    (hA : LinearIndependent \(\R\) A.row) (hD : LinearIndependent \(\R\) D.row) :
    Fintype.card m\(\sb{1}\) + Fintype.card m\(\sb{2}\)
      \(\le\) Module.finrank \(\R\) (Submodule.span \(\R\) F.rigidityRows)
\end{alltt}

This is a genuine mis-factoring: the transported-membership certificate was
a locally sound internal decomposition of the same rank count
Katoh--Tanigawa's own certificate proves, and it was replaced --- without
changing the target rank or reopening any already-certified step --- by a
decomposition matching the source's own block structure.

\subsubsection{The eq.-(6.12) \texorpdfstring{$+(D-1)$}{+(D-1)} vs \texorpdfstring{$+D$}{+D} shortfall}

Katoh--Tanigawa's Lemma~6.8 (KT~\S6.3, eq.~(6.12)) realizes a $k$-reducible
degree-two vertex $v$ by a degenerate placement: with $(G_v^{ab}, q)$ the
inductive realization, $v$'s hinge to $b$ is placed at the very hinge
$q(ab)$ the split-off edge already occupies. Column operations then exhibit
$R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ as a submatrix of $R(G, p_1)$, with the $vb$-row
reproducing $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$'s $ab$-row, so the rank adds: $(D-1)$ new rows
plus whatever rank $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ already has.

The project's degree-two split producer (Phase~21b, planned under the name
``Case~II'') took this construction as its route to the full rank
$D(|V|-1)$ the theorem's $k=0$ split needs. Several re-plans circled the one
row the construction seemed to be missing without first checking whether
eq.~(6.12) reaches the target at all; two, both on 2026-06-04, are
representative. The first was a row-side attempt to extract that row
directly from $G_v^{ab}$'s own old block, avoiding the split edge
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/524e741c1d7bc9b8b4a51636527666af76136e1d}{\texttt{524e741c}}),
found geometrically impossible ($G - v$ is not itself rigid, so no such row
exists to extract). The second was a motion-side reframing, pinning the
missing freedom directly rather than adding a row
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/7d2057526237e0bafc0df62d4440411d6803f4ea}{\texttt{7d205752}}),
which assumed a motion of $G$ constant off $v$ --- not something a rigid $G$
actually forces, since $G - v$ need not itself be rigid.

A math-first pass against Katoh--Tanigawa's own text, rather than against
either re-plan's declarations, found the target unreachable by eq.~(6.12)
alone at $k=0$ regardless of which row is added: by KT Lemma~4.8(i),
$G_v^{ab}$ is minimal $0$-dof at this split, so $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ is already
full rank, $D(|V \setminus \{v\}|-1)$, and eq.~(6.12) gives rank at least
$(D-1) + D(|V \setminus \{v\}|-1)$, i.e.\ $D(|V|-1) - 1$ --- exactly one row
short of the $D(|V|-1)$ target. Katoh--Tanigawa's own text states the same
shortfall directly, illustrated at $d=3$ ($D=6$) in the sketch introducing
Lemma~6.10 (KT p.~680): ``we only have rank $R(G,p_1) \ge 6(|V|-1)-1 \dots$
which does not complete the proof.'' Lemma~6.8's $+(D-1)$ bound suffices
only for the $k > 0$ split it is stated for; the project's split is the
$k=0$ case, whose missing row is supplied not by Lemma~6.8 but by the
redundant-edge argument of Lemma~6.10/6.13 (\S6.4,
\cref{sec:formalization-notes-scaffolding-arc}) --- Case~III, not Case~II
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/db1bfca589266fd6cbb209932337e41843d1fa72}{\texttt{db1bfca5}},
2026-06-04, closing Phase~21b and re-scoping the producer to
Phases~22--23).

The shortfall was visible in the arithmetic above from the day the
producer was scoped, but sat under these re-plans because each reasoned
about which named construction could supply the missing row rather than
tracing the target count through Katoh--Tanigawa's own construction by
hand; the checks the project runs on a new declaration --- name
resolution, the audit of a theorem's hypotheses --- read types and
hypotheses, not the numeral a rank inequality closes at, so neither
re-plan's declarations tripped anything before the math-first pass traced
the count directly.

This is a process failure: the arithmetic gap was real from the day the
producer was scoped, and no check running at the time --- type-correctness,
hypothesis-honesty --- was positioned to read it; only tracing the target
quantity through the source construction by hand could.

\subsection{Process/tracking failures}
\label{sec:formalization-notes-process-tracking}

The two episodes below are process failures in the introduction's sense:
each is a call that was reasonable given what was read at the time, but
left no artifact for a later check to find, or read a signal that looked
decisive and was not.

\subsubsection{The Case-I dispatch's untracked Lemma-6.5 arm}

Katoh--Tanigawa's Case~I (KT~\S6.2, pp.~673, 676--677) trifurcates on the
simplicity of the contraction $G/E(H)$ of a proper rigid subgraph $H$:
Lemma~6.2 handles a non-simple $G$ directly; Lemma~6.3 handles a simple
$G$ with some simple contraction, adding the rigid block's rank to the
contraction's own inductive rank by KT's block-triangular structure
(eq.~(6.3), \cref{sec:formalization-notes-abstraction-layer}); and
Lemma~6.5 handles what remains --- every contraction non-simple --- via
Claim~6.6, which extracts a degree-two vertex $v$ from a maximal proper
rigid subgraph and re-attaches it after inducting on the smaller graph
$G - v$.

A design recon opening the Case-I sub-phase read this trifurcation
correctly against Katoh--Tanigawa's text and named the right tracking
idiom for its harder leg: carry the Lemma-6.3-versus-6.5 dispatch as an
explicit hypothesis rather than build it outright
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/ac95815277bb846172429337a6919c3ca2c22137}{\texttt{ac958152}},
2026-06-04). The composer that landed the next day discharged only the
Lemma-6.3 leg, taking the case hypothesis \texttt{hcSimple} --- that
$G/E(H)$ is simple --- as a fixed input, and recorded the alternative
directly in its own doc-comment rather than tracking it: ``the
\texttt{hcontract} non-simple branch \dots and full-\texttt{theorem\_55}
dispatch are the coordinator's wiring''
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/c1ef55a0e40a40dba6bf30309713901c52f6d321}{\texttt{c1ef55a0}},
2026-06-05). The blueprint flip that landed the same day minted a red
node for the one hypothesis the composer's own signature carried as
content --- KT Claim~6.4, the surviving block's generic rank
(\cref{lem:claim-6-4}) --- and none for the Lemma-6.3-versus-6.5 dispatch
or the Lemma-6.5 arm
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/8fa5b02a09a2492ad7d68c8239e6801282d3bce5}{\texttt{8fa5b02a}});
the sub-phase's own close then recorded Claim~6.4 as ``the single
deferred obligation''
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/0e10b5ed8209afafa5813038167839f3c21ad15b}{\texttt{0e10b5ed}}).
Neither the dispatch nor the Lemma-6.5 arm appeared on any forward list
from this point.

Both resurfaced five sub-phases later, in the same audit of the
realization layer's hypothesis feeds against the landed Lean that also
found the vacuous bare realization predicate
(\cref{sec:formalization-notes-statement-faithfulness}): tracing what
discharges \texttt{hcontractGP} found no Lemma-6.3-versus-6.5 dispatch
anywhere in the tree, and the Lemma-6.5 arm genuinely missing rather than

(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/e4693d6170908c480e4f06924f2ea0a95ce9ba3f}{\texttt{e4693d61}},
2026-06-11). The dispatch itself was formalized that same day as a
classical case split on whether some proper rigid subgraph has a simple
contraction, with the Lemma-6.5 branch carried forward as a new named
hypothesis \texttt{h65} rather than built
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/06763ffbe96f89ab5d9ea039608c752611fe6c16}{\texttt{06763ffb}}).
Building Claim~6.6 itself --- the vertex-extraction argument the branch
needed --- took five more days, landing as
\texttt{case\_I\_realization\_h65} (\cref{lem:case-I-realization-h65})
and completing the dispatch (\cref{lem:case-I-dispatch}), which now cases
on all three of KT's Lemmas 6.2, 6.3, and 6.5
(commit~\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/commit/0f40c44dfa79c660e03376341bef65baca2c2ecf}{\texttt{0f40c44d}},
2026-06-16).

This is a process failure: the recon that opened the sub-phase read
Katoh--Tanigawa's case split correctly and named the idiom for tracking
its harder leg, but the commit that built the easier leg described the
rest as coordination rather than mathematics instead of using that idiom,
and the blueprint flip that followed took the composer's own carried
hypotheses as the complete list of what remained open. A hypothesis with
no corresponding node is invisible to any check that reads only
\texttt{\textbackslash uses} edges or a lemma's hypothesis list against
its own dependencies; it surfaced only once a scheduled audit read the
Lean's actual case coverage against Katoh--Tanigawa's own case split.

\subsubsection{The \texorpdfstring{$d = 3$}{d = 3} Claim-6.12 ``dead
island'' misread}

Katoh--Tanigawa's Claim~6.12 at general dimension (KT~\S6.4.2,

This is a process failure, in the same audit as the candidate-row episode
of \cref{sec:formalization-notes-abstraction-layer}, but with the opposite

with no grep-visible caller --- agreed on a section that was in fact
load-bearing, because the caller of a general-purpose declaration is a
fact about the Lean call chain, not about either static signal, and a
hedge recorded at one audit read as settled fact by the time a later one
inherited it.
