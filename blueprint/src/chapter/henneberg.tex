\section{Henneberg moves}
\label{sec:henneberg}

Henneberg's theorem~\cite{henneberg1911} characterises Laman graphs as
the graphs obtained from $K_2$ by iterating two combinatorial moves ---
the \emph{Type~I} and \emph{Type~II} extensions defined below. This
section introduces the two moves, shows that each preserves the Laman
property (and that the Type~I move does so in both directions), and
develops the \emph{reverse decomposition theorem}: every Laman graph on
$n \ge 3$ vertices admits a degree-$2$ Type~I reverse or a degree-$3$
Type~II reverse, yielding a Laman graph $G'$ on $n - 1$ vertices
(\cref{thm:isLaman-exists-typeI-or-typeII-reverse}). This reverse
decomposition is the induction step for the $\Leftarrow$ direction of
Laman's theorem in \cref{sec:laman-theorem}.

Throughout, the fresh vertex adjoined by either move is written
$\mathrm{none} : \mathrm{Option}\,V$, and the vertices of $G$ are
embedded into $\mathrm{Option}\,V$ by $\mathrm{some}$.

\subsection{Definitions}

\begin{definition}[Type~I move]
  \label{def:typeI}
  \lean{SimpleGraph.Henneberg.typeI}
  \leanok
  Given $G : \mathrm{SimpleGraph}\,V$ and two vertices $a, b \in V$, the
  \emph{Type~I extension} $\mathrm{typeI}\,G\,a\,b$ is the graph on
  $\mathrm{Option}\,V$ obtained by adding a fresh vertex
  $w = \mathrm{none}$ adjacent to (the images under $\mathrm{some}$ of)
  exactly $a$ and $b$. The other adjacencies are those of $G$.
\end{definition}
\begin{fmlnote}
  Adjoining the fresh vertex as the extra element $\mathrm{none}$ of
  $\mathrm{Option}\,V$ is mathlib's canonical ``add one element''
  construction; it avoids carrying a freshness side-condition on every
  move.
\end{fmlnote}

\begin{definition}[Type~II move]
  \label{def:typeII}
  \lean{SimpleGraph.Henneberg.typeII}
  \leanok
  Given $G : \mathrm{SimpleGraph}\,V$ and three vertices $a, b, c \in V$,
  the \emph{Type~II extension} $\mathrm{typeII}\,G\,a\,b\,c$ is the
  graph on $\mathrm{Option}\,V$ obtained by deleting the edge
  $\{a, b\}$ from $G$ and adding a fresh vertex $w = \mathrm{none}$
  adjacent to $a$, $b$, and $c$.
\end{definition}

In the Type~I extension the fresh vertex has degree~2 when $a \ne b$;
if $a = b$ the two new edges coincide and it has degree~1, so the move
preserves the Laman property only under $a \ne b$. The Type~II
extension is defined for arbitrary $a, b, c$, and preserves the Laman
property only when $a \ne b$, $c \ne a$, $c \ne b$, and
$\{a, b\} \in E(G)$.

\subsection{Edge-set decomposition and counts}

The two moves admit an edge-set decomposition into ``old'' edges (the
image of $E(G)$ under $\mathrm{some}$, with one edge removed in the
Type~II case) plus the two or three new edges incident to
$\mathrm{none}$. The resulting cardinality identities are used in the
tightness half of the preservation theorems below.

\begin{lemma}[Edge set of Type~I]
  \label{lem:typeI-edgeSet}
  \lean{SimpleGraph.Henneberg.typeI_edgeSet}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:typeI}
  \[
    E(\mathrm{typeI}\,G\,a\,b)
    \;=\;
    \mathrm{Sym2.map\;some}\,[E(G)]
    \;\cup\;
    \bigl\{\,
      \{\mathrm{none}, \mathrm{some}\,a\},
      \;
      \{\mathrm{none}, \mathrm{some}\,b\}
    \,\bigr\}.
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  By case analysis on the two endpoints of an edge in
  $\mathrm{Option}\,V$, the only edges incident to $\mathrm{none}$
  are those joining $\mathrm{none}$ to $\mathrm{some}\,a$ or
  $\mathrm{some}\,b$ (by definition of $\mathrm{typeI}$); all other
  edges have both endpoints in $\mathrm{some}\,[V]$ and correspond
  bijectively to edges of $G$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Edge count of Type~I]
  \label{lem:typeI-edgeSet-ncard}
  \lean{SimpleGraph.Henneberg.typeI_edgeSet_ncard}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:typeI-edgeSet}
  If $V$ is finite and $a \ne b$, then
  \[
    |E(\mathrm{typeI}\,G\,a\,b)| \;=\; |E(G)| + 2.
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Use \cref{lem:typeI-edgeSet}: the two summands are disjoint (each new
  edge contains $\mathrm{none}$, which is not in the image of
  $\mathrm{some}$), $\mathrm{Sym2.map\;some}$ is injective, and the
  two new edges are distinct because $a \ne b$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Edge set of Type~II]
  \label{lem:typeII-edgeSet}
  \lean{SimpleGraph.Henneberg.typeII_edgeSet}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:typeII}
  \[
    E(\mathrm{typeII}\,G\,a\,b\,c)
    \;=\;
    \mathrm{Sym2.map\;some}\bigl[E(G) \setminus \{\{a,b\}\}\bigr]
    \;\cup\;
    \bigl\{\,
      \{\mathrm{none}, \mathrm{some}\,a\}, \;
      \{\mathrm{none}, \mathrm{some}\,b\}, \;
      \{\mathrm{none}, \mathrm{some}\,c\}
    \,\bigr\}.
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Case analysis as in \cref{lem:typeI-edgeSet}: edges incident to
  $\mathrm{none}$ are exactly the three new ones, edges with both
  endpoints in $\mathrm{some}\,[V]$ correspond bijectively to edges of
  $G$, and the $\{a, b\}$ edge is explicitly removed by the
  $s(u, v) \ne s(a, b)$ clause in the definition.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Edge count of Type~II]
  \label{lem:typeII-edgeSet-ncard}
  \lean{SimpleGraph.Henneberg.typeII_edgeSet_ncard}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:typeII-edgeSet}
  If $V$ is finite and $a \ne b$, $c \ne a$, $c \ne b$, then
  \[
    |E(\mathrm{typeII}\,G\,a\,b\,c)|
    \;=\;
    |E(G) \setminus \{\{a,b\}\}| + 3.
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  As in \cref{lem:typeI-edgeSet-ncard}, the two summands of
  \cref{lem:typeII-edgeSet} are disjoint and the
  $\mathrm{Sym2.map\;some}$ side is injective; the three new edges
  are pairwise distinct because $a, b, c$ are.
\end{proof}

The identity holds without assuming $\{a, b\} \in E(G)$: the deletion
is recorded on the right-hand side rather than imposed as a hypothesis.

\subsection{Preservation of the Laman property}

Each move preserves the Laman property under the hypotheses noted
above. Tightness follows from the edge counts of the previous
subsection; sparsity is checked by a case analysis on whether the
fresh vertex $\mathrm{none}$ lies in the tested vertex set.

\begin{theorem}[Type~I preserves Laman]
  \label{thm:typeI-isLaman}
  \lean{SimpleGraph.Henneberg.typeI_isLaman}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:typeI, def:isLaman, lem:typeI-edgeSet-ncard}
  If $V$ is finite, $G$ is Laman, and $a \ne b$, then
  $\mathrm{typeI}\,G\,a\,b$ is Laman.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Tightness follows from \cref{lem:typeI-edgeSet-ncard} and
  $|\mathrm{Option}\,V| = |V| + 1$: starting from
  $|E(G)| + 3 = 2|V|$, both sides gain $+2$.

  For sparsity, fix $s \subseteq \mathrm{Option}\,V$ with
  $3 \le 2|s|$ and write $s' := s \cap \mathrm{some}[V]$ for the
  $\mathrm{some}$-preimage. The edge set
  $\edgesIn[\mathrm{typeI}\,G\,a\,b]{s}$ decomposes as the $G$-edges
  on $s'$ plus the subset $T$ of the two new edges
  $\{\mathrm{none}, \mathrm{some}\,a\}$,
  $\{\mathrm{none}, \mathrm{some}\,b\}$ that lie in $s$. Case-split:
  \begin{itemize}
    \item If $\mathrm{none} \notin s$, then $T = \emptyset$ and
      $|s| = |s'|$, so the sparsity of $G$ at $s'$ transfers directly.
    \item If $\mathrm{none} \in s$, then $|s| = |s'| + 1$ and
      $|T| \le 2$. Sub-case on $|s'|$: if $|s'| = 0$ the precondition
      fails; if $|s'| = 1$, say $s' = \{w\}$, then no $G$-edge lies
      in $s'$ and $T \subseteq \{\{\mathrm{none}, \mathrm{some}\,w\}\}$
      so $|T| \le 1$, matching $2|s| - 3 = 0$; if $|s'| \ge 2$, apply
      sparsity of $G$ at $s'$ and use $|T| \le 2$.
  \end{itemize}
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Type~II preserves Laman]
  \label{thm:typeII-isLaman}
  \lean{SimpleGraph.Henneberg.typeII_isLaman}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:typeII, def:isLaman, lem:typeII-edgeSet-ncard}
  If $V$ is finite, $G$ is Laman, the three vertices $a, b, c$ are
  pairwise distinct, and $\{a, b\} \in E(G)$, then
  $\mathrm{typeII}\,G\,a\,b\,c$ is Laman.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Tightness: by \cref{lem:typeII-edgeSet-ncard} and
  $\{a, b\} \in E(G)$, the deletion drops the count by exactly $1$
  and the three new edges add $3$, so $\mathrm{typeII}\,G\,a\,b\,c$
  has $|E(G)| + 2$ edges on $|V| + 1$ vertices. Then
  $\bigl(|E(G)| + 2\bigr) + 3 = 2(|V| + 1)$ follows from
  $|E(G)| + 3 = 2|V|$.

  Sparsity mirrors \cref{thm:typeI-isLaman}. The difference is that
  the deleted edge $\{a, b\}$ interacts with the (at most) three new
  edges. In the subcase $\mathrm{none} \in s$ with $|s'| \ge 2$, the
  net edge gain is again at most $2$: either
  $\{a, b\} \in \edgesIn{s'}$ (the deletion loses $1$ and the three
  new edges contribute at most $3$, for a net gain of at most $2$) or
  $\{a, b\} \notin \edgesIn{s'}$ (the deletion loses $0$; since
  $\{a, b\} \in E(G)$ this forces $a \notin s'$ or $b \notin s'$, so
  the corresponding new edge $\{\mathrm{none}, \mathrm{some}\,a\}$ or
  $\{\mathrm{none}, \mathrm{some}\,b\}$ is also absent, leaving at most
  two new edges in $s$). The remaining sub-cases
  ($|s'| \in \{0, 1\}$ and $\mathrm{none} \notin s$) match
  \cref{thm:typeI-isLaman}.
\end{proof}

For the Type~I move the implication reverses: $\mathrm{typeI}\,G\,a\,b$
is Laman if and only if $G$ is. This reverse direction is used in the
reduction of \cref{thm:isLaman-exists-typeI-or-typeII-reverse} to the
sparse reverse decomposition: after
\cref{thm:isSparse-exists-typeI-or-typeII-reverse} produces a
$(2, 3)$-sparse $G' = G[\{w \ne v\}]$, the equivalence combined with
$G$'s global edge count raises $G'$ from sparse to Laman.

\begin{theorem}[Type~I reflects Laman]
  \label{thm:typeI-reverse-isLaman}
  \lean{SimpleGraph.Henneberg.typeI_reverse_isLaman}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:typeI, def:isLaman, lem:typeI-edgeSet-ncard}
  If $V$ is finite, $a \ne b$, and $\mathrm{typeI}\,G\,a\,b$ is Laman,
  then $G$ is Laman.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Dual to \cref{thm:typeI-isLaman}: lift an arbitrary
  $s' \subseteq V$ to $s := \mathrm{some}[s'] \subseteq \mathrm{Option}\,V$
  and apply sparsity of $\mathrm{typeI}\,G\,a\,b$ at $s$. With
  $\mathrm{none} \notin s$ the fresh-edge contribution collapses to
  zero, so the $\mathrm{typeI}$-sparsity bound at $s$ collapses to the
  $G$-sparsity bound at $s'$. Tightness transports via
  \cref{lem:typeI-edgeSet-ncard} run backwards.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Type~I is Laman iff its base is]
  \label{thm:typeI-isLaman-iff}
  \lean{SimpleGraph.Henneberg.typeI_isLaman_iff}
  \leanok
  \uses{thm:typeI-isLaman, thm:typeI-reverse-isLaman}
  If $V$ is finite and $a \ne b$, then
  $(\mathrm{typeI}\,G\,a\,b).\mathrm{IsLaman}
   \iff G.\mathrm{IsLaman}$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Combine \cref{thm:typeI-isLaman} and
  \cref{thm:typeI-reverse-isLaman}.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Reverse decomposition}
\label{sec:henneberg-decomp}

Every Laman graph on $n \ge 3$ vertices admits a Type~I reverse (at
some degree-$2$ vertex) or a Type~II reverse (at some degree-$3$ vertex
with a suitable non-adjacent neighbor pair), yielding a Laman graph
$G'$ on $n - 1$ vertices. This reverse decomposition is the
combinatorial input to the $\Leftarrow$ direction of Laman's theorem in
\cref{sec:laman-theorem}.

The proof reduces the Laman statement to the sparse-graph reverse
decomposition (\cref{thm:isSparse-exists-typeI-or-typeII-reverse})
together with an edge-count step raising the resulting sparse graph to
Laman. The combinatorial work --- the per-pair tight-blocker argument
that selects the non-adjacent neighbor pair in the Type~II branch ---
is carried out in \cref{thm:isSparse-exists-typeI-or-typeII-reverse},
which uses the tight-blocker witness
(\cref{lem:isSparse-typeII-reverse-blocker}) and the union-closure of
tight subsets (\cref{thm:isTightOn-union-inter}).

\begin{theorem}[Laman reverse decomposition]
  \label{thm:isLaman-exists-typeI-or-typeII-reverse}
  \lean{SimpleGraph.Henneberg.IsLaman.exists_typeI_or_typeII_reverse}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:isLaman, def:typeI, def:typeII,
    thm:isSparse-exists-typeI-or-typeII-reverse,
    thm:typeI-isLaman-iff,
    lem:typeII-edgeSet-ncard,
    lem:isLaman-two-le-degree}
  Let $G$ be Laman on a finite vertex type $V$ with $|V| \ge 3$. Then
  there exists $v \in V$ such that either
  \begin{enumerate}
    \item $\deg_G(v) = 2$, the two neighbors $a, b$ of $v$ are
      distinct, and the induced subgraph $G' := G[\{w \ne v\}]$ is
      Laman; or
    \item $\deg_G(v) = 3$ with three pairwise distinct neighbors
      $\{a, b, c\}$, the pair $\{a, b\}$ is non-adjacent in $G$, and
      the bridge-augmented subgraph
      $G' := G[\{w \ne v\}] \cup \{\{a, b\}\}$ is Laman.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Apply \cref{thm:isSparse-exists-typeI-or-typeII-reverse} to the
  sparsity of $G$ (a Laman graph is $(2, 3)$-sparse with
  $|E(G)| = 2|V| - 3 \ge 1$). Every vertex of a Laman graph on
  $|V| \ge 3$ vertices has degree at least $2$
  (\cref{lem:isLaman-two-le-degree}), so the pendant branch
  $\deg_G(v) = 1$ of the sparse decomposition cannot occur.

  In the Type~I branch the sparse decomposition already gives
  $\deg_G(v) = 2$ and its two neighbors; \cref{thm:typeI-isLaman-iff},
  applied through the canonical Type~I isomorphism, raises $G'$ from
  $(2, 3)$-sparse to Laman.

  In the Type~II branch the Type~II isomorphism transports the Laman
  property of $G$ to $\mathrm{typeII}\,G'\,a\,b\,c$. Combining
  \cref{lem:typeII-edgeSet-ncard} with the tight global count
  $|E(G)| + 3 = 2|V|$ and the presence of the bridge in $G'$ gives
  $|E(G')| + 3 = 2\,|\{w \ne v\}|$, raising $G'$ from $(2, 3)$-sparse
  to tight, hence Laman.
\end{proof}
\begin{fmlnote}
  The theorem is stated in \emph{flat} form: $G'$ is described by its
  edges --- the subgraph of $G$ induced on the vertices other than $v$,
  augmented with the bridge $\{a, b\}$ in the Type~II case --- rather
  than as the image of a Henneberg constructor, and the chosen vertex's
  neighborhood data is provided alongside. From that data the
  isomorphism $G \simeq \mathrm{typeI}\,G'\,a\,b$ (or its Type~II
  analogue) required by the operation-form preservation theorems is
  reconstructed through the project's isomorphism constructors. See the
  statement-form aside in \cref{sec:rigidity-matroid-lifts} for why
  reverse decompositions take this form.
\end{fmlnote}

\subsection{Worked example: \texorpdfstring{$K_4 \setminus e$}{K4 minus e}}

As an illustration of the forward moves, we certify a small Laman graph
by building it from the base case $K_2$.

\begin{theorem}[$K_4 \setminus e$ is Laman]
  \label{thm:top-fin-four-minus-edge-isLaman}
  \lean{SimpleGraph.top_fin_four_minus_edge_isLaman}
  \leanok
  \uses{thm:typeI-isLaman, thm:top-fin-two-isLaman, lem:isLaman-iso}
  The graph $K_4$ on the vertex set $\mathrm{Fin}\,4$, minus the single
  edge $\{2, 3\}$, is Laman.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Apply \cref{thm:typeI-isLaman} twice, starting from $K_2$
  (\cref{thm:top-fin-two-isLaman}): one Type~I move yields a Laman
  graph on three vertices (a copy of $K_3$), and a second yields a
  Laman graph on four vertices. This graph, on
  $\mathrm{Option}\,(\mathrm{Option}\,(\mathrm{Fin}\,2))$, is
  isomorphic to $K_4 \setminus \{2, 3\}$ on $\mathrm{Fin}\,4$ via an
  explicit bijection, and \cref{lem:isLaman-iso} transports the Laman
  property along it.
\end{proof}
