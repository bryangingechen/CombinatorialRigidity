\section{The algebraic induction: KT Theorem 5.5 and its three cases}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction}

This chapter proves KT Theorem~5.5 \cite[\S5--\S6]{katohTanigawa2011}, which
turns the combinatorial reduction of \cref{sec:molecular-induction} into a
statement about rigidity-matrix rank. That chapter reduced every minimal
$0$-dof-graph to the two-vertex double edge by combinatorial induction (KT
Theorem~4.9, \cref{thm:minimal-kdof-reduction}). Here each step of the
reduction is realized geometrically: every minimal $k$-dof-graph $G$ with
$|V| \ge 2$ carries a panel-hinge realization $(G,p)$ whose rigidity matrix
attains the rank $D(|V|-1) - k$ (\cref{thm:theorem-55}, at every dimension
$n \ge 3$, i.e.\ $D = \binom{n+1}{2} \ge 6$; the $d = 3$ instance is
\cref{thm:theorem-55-d3-instance}). Two consequences follow. The first is the
generic-rank reconciliation of Tay--Whiteley's body-bar count with the
rigidity-matrix rank (KT Proposition~1.1, \cref{prop:rigidity-matrix-prop11}).
The second extends the realization from minimal $0$-dof graphs to arbitrary
deficiency (KT Theorem~5.6, \cref{thm:theorem-55-6}, with $d = 3$ instance
\cref{thm:theorem-55-6-d3}). Together they give the molecular conjecture
itself (KT Conjecture~1.2, \cref{thm:molecular-conjecture}).

Throughout, $D = \binom{n+1}{2}$ is the body-bar dimension, $\tilde G =
(D-1)\cdot G$ is the multiplied graph of \cref{sec:body-hinge}, $R(G,p)$ is
the panel-hinge rigidity matrix of \cref{def:rigidity-matrix}, and
$\operatorname{def}(\tilde G)$ is the $D$-deficiency of
\cref{def:D-deficiency}. The rank of $R(G,p)$ counts only the rigidity rows
coming from the edges of $G$, so it depends on $G$ alone and is unchanged by
ambient bodies outside $V(G)$. This is what lets a realization compose
through an induction that removes one vertex at a time: an inductive subgraph
does not span the ambient body type, so its full null space always carries
the free motions of the omitted bodies, and only a rank statement is stable
under the induction (\cref{def:rank-hypothesis},
\cref{rem:rank-hypothesis-relative}).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{The argument.} \Cref{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-panel}
introduces the panel-hinge frameworks: KT's hinge-coplanar frameworks,
presented in panel-normal form so that transversality and coplanarity are
computed in the extensor algebra rather than through affine-subspace
intersections. \Cref{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-prop11} proves the
analytic half of KT Proposition~1.1, identifying the graphs a generic panel
realization can make rigid. KT Theorem~5.5 is then proved by induction on
$|V|$, using KT Theorem~4.9 (\cref{thm:minimal-kdof-reduction}): every minimal
$k$-dof-graph on at least three vertices has a proper rigid subgraph
(Case~I), a cut edge or a positive-deficiency splittable vertex (Case~II), or
a zero-deficiency splittable vertex (Case~III), and each of the moves yields a
realization at full rank.
\begin{itemize}
  \item \emph{Case~I} (\cref{lem:case-I}, \cref{lem:case-I-realization}), the
        contraction of a proper rigid subgraph, is KT's block-triangular
        rank-addition (KT eq.~(6.3)): the rigid-block rows and the surviving
        rows are made jointly independent by an exterior-column projection,
        reaching full rank.
  \item \emph{Case~II} (\cref{lem:case-II}, \cref{lem:case-II-realization})
        handles the cut edge and the positive-deficiency splitting-off. It is
        the panel-hinge analogue of the bar-joint $1$-extension, whose
        preservation of infinitesimal rigidity is due to
        Whiteley~\cite[Theorem~2.2.2]{whiteley1996}.
  \item \emph{Case~III} (\cref{lem:case-III}), the zero-deficiency
        splitting-off with no proper rigid subgraph, is the most difficult of
        the three. KT's degenerate placement (KT eq.~(6.12)) falls one row
        short of full rank, and the missing row is supplied by a redundant-edge
        argument (KT Lemma~6.10, developed in
        \cref{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-caseIII}).
\end{itemize}

\noindent The shared analytic input is the genericity argument (KT
Claim~6.4, \cref{lem:genericity-device}), which selects the coordinates
attaining the generic rank; the cycle-realization lemma (KT Lemma~5.4,
\cref{lem:cycle-realization}; Crapo--Whiteley~\cite{crapoWhiteley1982},
Whiteley~\cite{whiteley1999}) is used in Case~III. The base of the induction is
the two-vertex double edge (\cref{lem:theorem-55-base}). At $d = 3$ the three
cases assemble into \cref{thm:theorem-55-d3-instance}; the general-dimension
assembly (KT Lemma~6.13) gives \cref{thm:theorem-55}.
