\section{The algebraic induction: Theorem 5.5 and its three cases}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction}

This chapter is the dep-graph of the
\emph{algebraic} half of Katoh--Tanigawa's proof of the molecular
conjecture --- \cite[\S5, \S6]{katohTanigawa2011}, the fifth and
final proof stratum (phases~21--26). Where \cref{sec:molecular-induction} reduced every
minimal $0$-dof-graph to the two-vertex double edge by a combinatorial
induction (Theorem~4.9, \cref{thm:minimal-kdof-reduction}), this chapter
\emph{realizes} that reduction at the rigidity-matrix rank: it states
Katoh--Tanigawa's Theorem~5.5 --- every minimal $k$-dof-graph $G$ with
$|V| \ge 2$ has a panel-hinge realization $(G,p)$ with
$\operatorname{rank} R(G,p) = D(|V|-1) - k$ --- and discharges its base
case and all three of its inductive cases at $d = 3$, including the crux
Case~III ($k=0$, no proper rigid subgraph); the formalized $d = 3$ instance
(\cref{thm:theorem-55-d3-instance}) is complete, and from it the generic-rank
reconciliation (\cref{prop:rigidity-matrix-prop11}) and Theorem~5.6 at $d = 3$
(\cref{thm:theorem-55-6-d3}) follow. General $d$ is Phase~23.
Throughout $D = \binom{d+1}{2} = \delta$ is the body-bar dimension,
$\tilde G = (D-1)\cdot G$ is the multiplied graph of
\cref{sec:body-hinge}, $R(G,p)$ is the panel-hinge rigidity matrix of
\cref{def:rigidity-matrix}, and $\operatorname{def}(\tilde G)$ is the
$D$-deficiency of \cref{def:D-deficiency}.

\medskip
\noindent The realization motive is carried in its \emph{$V(G)$-relative
rank form} $\operatorname{rank} R(G,p) = D(|V(G)|-1)$
(\cref{def:rank-hypothesis}) --- equivalently ``every infinitesimal motion
is constant on $V(G)$''. This is essential: the \emph{absolute} null-space
equality $\dim Z(G,p) = D$ over the whole screw-assignment space
$\R^{D|V_\alpha|}$ of the fixed body type $\alpha$ equals infinitesimal
rigidity only when $G$ \emph{spans} $\alpha$ (a body in
$\alpha \setminus V(G)$ carries no hinge constraint, so it is a free
non-trivial motion). The induction of Theorem~4.9
(\cref{thm:minimal-kdof-reduction}) reduces to graphs with strictly fewer
vertices on the same $\alpha$, which therefore do \emph{not} span it, so
the absolute motive is unsatisfiable for every inductive subgraph (the
two-vertex base case already needs ``every body is one of the two''). The
$V(G)$-relative form is $\alpha$-independent (isolated bodies contribute no
rigidity rows), composes through the induction, and makes the
block-triangular gluing of Case~I satisfiable.

The chapter opens with the generic-rank reconciliation (Tay--Whiteley's
Proposition~1.1, relocated forward from Phase~18), states the induction
skeleton, then discharges the base case (via the parallel-hinges Lemma
\cref{lem:rank-parallel-full}), Case~I (proper rigid subgraph, via
rigid-subgraph contraction \cref{lem:reduction-step} and the pin-a-body
Lemma~\cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex}), and Case~II ($k>0$ splitting, the
panel-hinge analogue of Whiteley's bar-joint 1-extension). The shared
analytic device --- the genericity argument (Claim~6.4/6.9,
\cref{lem:genericity-device}) --- is green; the cycle-realization Lemma~5.4
(\cref{lem:cycle-realization}; Crapo--Whiteley~\cite{crapoWhiteley1982},
Whiteley~\cite{whiteley1999}) enters as an input. The relativized motive,
the base case (\cref{lem:theorem-55-base}, the two-vertex case
unconditionally), the two accounting iffs \cref{lem:case-I} /
\cref{lem:case-II} (genericity-free $\alpha$-independent rigidity bridges,
with the device entering only in the producers), the panel-coordinate row
coordinatization \cref{lem:rows-polynomial-in-normals} (the keystone, B0),
and the $V(G)$-relative count bridge (\cref{lem:relative-screw-split},
\cref{lem:relative-device-count}, \cref{lem:isInfRigidOn-of-relative-count})
that converts the device's absolute screw-count to the $V(G)$-relative motive
are all green.

The two realization \emph{producers} carry the geometric content:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \emph{The Case~II producer (\cref{lem:case-II-realization}) is KT
        Case~III.} The project's $\mathtt{hsplit}$ branch is the $k=0$
        reducible-vertex split (\cref{thm:theorem-55}, $\mathrm{IsMinimalKDof}\,n\,0$,
        no proper rigid subgraph). By KT Lemma~4.8(i) $G_v^{ab}$ is then minimal
        $0$-dof, and KT's eq.~(6.12) degenerate placement (the $vb$-hinge placed
        at $q(ab)$, reproducing the $e_0$ row) yields only
        $\mathrm{rank}\,R(G,p_1) \ge (D-1) + D(|V|-2) = D(|V|-1)-1$ --- \emph{one
        row short} of full rank (KT p.~680). The missing row is the Case~III
        redundant-edge row (Lemma~6.10/6.13), built in
        \cref{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-caseIII}. KT's Lemma~6.8 (where
        $+(D-1)$ suffices) is $k>0$ \emph{only}.
  \item \emph{The Case~I producer (\cref{lem:case-I-realization}) is full-rank
        (KT~\S6.2), reaching full $D(|V|-1)$ with \emph{no}
        shortfall.} It is KT eq.~(6.3)'s block-triangular rank-addition over a
        single common framework --- the rigid-block rows $E(H)$ $\sqcup$ the
        surviving-edge rows $E(G) \setminus E(H)$, made jointly independent by
        the exterior-column projection (KT's top-right $0$), fed to the device's
        row-count closure, with KT Claim~6.4 (\cref{lem:claim-6-4}, the
        surviving block's exterior-projected rank, eq.~(6.9)) as the analytic
        input and the graph$\leftrightarrow$matroid bridge
        \cref{lem:rigidContract-isMinimalKDof} on the combinatorial side.
\end{itemize}
The reusable row infra (the row sub-nodes N7b-0/1/2/3, the device-closure glue
\cref{lem:realization-of-independent-rows}, the count bridge) feeds those
producers. At $d = 3$ all three case producers are formalized and the instance
\cref{thm:theorem-55-d3-instance} assembles them with no carried hypotheses,
discharging the $d = 3$ spine \cref{thm:theorem-55}; the full reconciliation
\cref{prop:rigidity-matrix-prop11} and Theorem~5.6 at $d = 3$
(\cref{thm:theorem-55-6-d3}) follow. The general-$d$ argument (KT Lemma~6.13)
stays open for Phase~23.
