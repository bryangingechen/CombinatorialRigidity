\section{The algebraic induction: Theorem 5.5 base, Cases I \& II}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction}

This chapter is the \textbf{Phase~21} dep-graph: the opening of the
\emph{algebraic} half of Katoh--Tanigawa's proof of the molecular
conjecture --- \cite[\S5, \S6.1--6.3]{katohTanigawa2011}, the fifth and
final proof stratum. Where \cref{sec:molecular-induction} reduced every
minimal $0$-dof-graph to the two-vertex double edge by a combinatorial
induction (Theorem~4.9, \cref{thm:minimal-kdof-reduction}), this chapter
\emph{realizes} that reduction at the rigidity-matrix rank: it states
Katoh--Tanigawa's Theorem~5.5 --- every minimal $k$-dof-graph $G$ with
$|V| \ge 2$ has a panel-hinge realization $(G,p)$ with
$\operatorname{rank} R(G,p) = D(|V|-1) - k$ --- and discharges its base
case and the first two of its three inductive cases. The crux Case~III
($k=0$, no proper rigid subgraph) is deferred to Phases~22--23.
Throughout $D = \binom{d+1}{2} = \delta$ is the body-bar dimension,
$\tilde G = (D-1)\cdot G$ is the multiplied graph of
\cref{sec:body-hinge}, $R(G,p)$ is the panel-hinge rigidity matrix of
\cref{def:rigidity-matrix}, and $\operatorname{def}(\tilde G)$ is the
$D$-deficiency of \cref{def:D-deficiency}.

\subsection{The panel layer}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-panel}

Katoh--Tanigawa's \emph{panel-hinge} framework is a
\emph{hinge-coplanar} body-hinge framework: at each body all incident
hinges lie in a common hyperplane \cite[p.\,647]{katohTanigawa2011}. The
molecular conjecture's content is that this coplanarity can be met
without losing rigidity; the Phase-18 body-hinge framework
(\cref{def:hinge-constraint}) carries free hinges with no coplanarity,
so the realization-existence nodes below need a \emph{panel layer} on
top. We take the panel-data form: a panel realization assigns each body
$v$ a hyperplane normal $n_v \in \R^{k+2}$, and the hinge at an edge
$e = uv$ is the codimension-2 intersection $\mathrm{panel}(u) \cap
\mathrm{panel}(v)$. Its supporting $k$-extensor --- the screw-space
element of \cref{def:hinge-constraint} the rigidity matrix constrains ---
is the Grassmann--Cayley meet of the two panels, equivalently the
complement iso of the join of the two normals. Coplanarity (both hinges
at $v$ lie in $\mathrm{panel}(v)$ by construction) and transversality
both live in the extensor algebra, so no affine-subspace-intersection
plumbing is needed.

\begin{definition}[Panel support extensor]
  \label{def:panel-support-extensor}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.panelSupportExtensor,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.normalsJoin,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.panelSupportExtensor_ne_zero_iff}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:join, def:meet-complement-iso, def:hinge-constraint}
  For two panel normals $n_1, n_2 \in \R^{k+2}$, the \emph{panel support
  extensor} is the supporting $k$-extensor $C(p(e)) \in \Lambda^k
  \R^{k+2}$ of the codimension-2 intersection of the two panels, given as
  the complement iso (\cref{def:meet-complement-iso}) of the grade-2 join
  $n_1 \wedge n_2 \in \Lambda^2 \R^{k+2}$ (\cref{def:join}). Since
  $k+2-2 = k$ is the screw-space grade, the result lands in the screw
  space $\Lambda^k \R^{k+2}$ of \cref{def:hinge-constraint} and feeds the
  hinge constraint verbatim. It is nonzero exactly when the two normals
  are linearly independent --- the algebraic form of the two panels
  meeting transversally in a genuine hinge --- the only general-position
  condition the panel layer needs.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Panel-hinge framework and its body-hinge interpretation]
  \label{def:panel-hinge-framework}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.toBodyHinge,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.IsHingeCoplanar,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.isHingeCoplanar_toBodyHinge}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:panel-support-extensor, def:hinge-constraint}
  A \emph{panel-hinge framework} is a multigraph $G = (V,E)$ together with a
  per-body panel normal $n_v \in \R^{k+2}$ (the pole of body $v$'s
  hyperplane $\mathrm{panel}(v)$). The hinge at an edge $e = uv$ is the
  codimension-2 intersection $\mathrm{panel}(u) \cap \mathrm{panel}(v)$,
  with supporting $k$-extensor the meet $C(p(e)) =
  \mathrm{panelSupportExtensor}(n_u, n_v)$
  (\cref{def:panel-support-extensor}). Its \emph{body-hinge interpretation}
  is the body-hinge framework (\cref{def:hinge-constraint}) on $G$ carrying
  exactly these supporting extensors, so the entire rigidity-matrix rank
  theory of \cref{sec:molecular-rigidity-matrix} applies verbatim. Every
  hinge incident to $v$ has $n_v$ as a join factor, hence lies in the single
  panel $\mathrm{panel}(v)$: the framework is \emph{hinge-coplanar} by
  construction. A body-hinge framework is hinge-coplanar
  ($\mathrm{IsHingeCoplanar}$) when it arises this way; this is the
  molecular-model constraint Katoh--Tanigawa's panel-hinge frameworks carry
  beyond the free-hinge body-hinge model, and the conjecture's content is
  that it can be met without dropping rigidity.
\end{definition}

\subsection{The generic-rank reconciliation (Proposition 1.1, analytic half)}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-prop11}

The matroidal half of Tay--Whiteley's Proposition~1.1 --- the bridge
$\operatorname{def}(\tilde G) = \operatorname{corank} M(\tilde G)$ ---
landed green in Phase~19 (\cref{thm:def-eq-corank}). The analytic half
relocated forward to this phase: it wires the rigidity-matrix rank
$R(G,p)$ of \cref{sec:molecular-rigidity-matrix} to the matroid corank,
and depends on the generic-rank argument (Claim~6.4) that drives the
whole induction.

\begin{proposition}[Generic rank equals $D(|V|-1) - \operatorname{def}$; KT Proposition 1.1, analytic half]
  \label{prop:rigidity-matrix-prop11}
  \uses{def:rigidity-matrix, def:D-deficiency, thm:def-eq-corank, def:dof-generic}
  For a generic panel-hinge realization $(G,p)$,
  \[
    \operatorname{rank} R(G,p) = D(|V|-1) - \operatorname{def}(\tilde G).
  \]
  Equivalently $G$ has a rigid panel-hinge realization iff
  $\tilde G = (D-1)\cdot G$ contains $D$ edge-disjoint spanning trees,
  reconciling the honest rank form of
  \cref{sec:molecular-rigidity-matrix} with the body-hinge existence
  form (\cref{thm:body-hinge-tay}) of Phase~16. This is
  Jackson--Jord\'an's Theorem~6.1, geometric side
  \cite{jacksonJordan2009}.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
  \uses{thm:theorem-55}
  The deficiency is the corank of $M(\tilde G)$
  (\cref{thm:def-eq-corank}), so the claim is that a generic realization
  attains the maximum rank $D(|V|-1) - \operatorname{corank}$. The
  lower-bound (generic max-rank) direction is exactly the content of
  Theorem~5.5 (\cref{thm:theorem-55}), pushed from minimal $k$-dof-graphs
  to all multigraphs by deleting down to a minimal $k$-dof spanning
  subgraph and observing that re-adding edges only grows the rank; the
  upper bound is the codimension form
  \cref{lem:trivial-motions-rank-bound} together with the deficiency
  count. Lands with the Claim~6.4 generic-rank argument.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The induction skeleton and base case}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-base}

\begin{definition}[Generic-rank hypothesis (6.1)]
  \label{def:rank-hypothesis}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.RankHypothesis}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:rigidity-matrix, def:k-dof}
  For a minimal $k$-dof-graph $G$ with $|V| \ge 2$, the
  \emph{realization hypothesis} $(6.1)$ asserts the existence of a
  panel-hinge realization $(G,p)$ with
  $\operatorname{rank} R(G,p) = D(|V|-1) - k$, and --- when $G$ is simple
  --- with no two hinges parallel. This is the predicate Theorem~5.5
  establishes by induction on $|V|$. In the codimension form of
  \cref{sec:molecular-rigidity-matrix} (the column space is the
  screw-assignment space of dimension $D|V|$ and
  $\operatorname{rank} R(G,p) = D|V| - \dim Z(G,p)$), the target rank is
  equivalently the null-space dimension $\dim Z(G,p) = D + k$, the form
  the Lean carries.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Realization at the target rank; KT Theorem 5.5]
  \label{thm:theorem-55}
  \uses{def:rank-hypothesis, thm:minimal-kdof-reduction, lem:case-I,
        lem:case-II, lem:case-III, lem:theorem-55-base}
  Every minimal $k$-dof-graph $G$ with $|V| \ge 2$ has a panel-hinge
  realization $(G,p)$ with $\operatorname{rank} R(G,p) = D(|V|-1) - k$,
  nonparallel if $G$ is simple. (\cref{def:rank-hypothesis}.)
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  \uses{thm:minimal-kdof-reduction, lem:theorem-55-base, lem:case-I,
        lem:case-II, lem:case-III}
  Induction on $|V|$, driven by the combinatorial reduction
  Theorem~4.9 (\cref{thm:minimal-kdof-reduction}). The base case
  $|V| = 2$ is \cref{lem:theorem-55-base}. The inductive step splits
  three ways on the reduction dichotomy: Case~I (a proper rigid
  subgraph, \cref{lem:case-I}), Case~II ($k > 0$, splitting off a
  reducible degree-2 vertex, \cref{lem:case-II}), and Case~III ($k = 0$,
  no proper rigid subgraph, \cref{lem:case-III}). Cases~I and~II land in
  this phase; Case~III --- the crux --- is Phases~22--23.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Theorem 5.5 base case ($|V| = 2$)]
  \label{lem:theorem-55-base}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.theorem_55_base,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.rankHypothesis_zero_iff}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:rank-hypothesis, lem:rank-parallel-full}
  The two-vertex double edge has a panel-hinge realization of rank
  $D(|V|-1) - k = D - k$. For the minimal $0$-dof case (the double edge,
  $k = 0$) this is the full rank $D$, attained by two non-parallel hinges
  via the parallel-hinges-full Lemma~5.3
  (\cref{lem:rank-parallel-full}). At $k = 0$ the realization hypothesis
  is exactly infinitesimal rigidity, the null space being the
  $D$-dimensional trivial-motion space.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:rank-parallel-full}
  Two bodies joined by hinges with linearly independent supporting
  extensors realize the full screw-difference constraint: the
  parallel-hinges-full Lemma~5.3 (\cref{lem:rank-parallel-full}) forces
  $S(u) = S(v)$, so every infinitesimal motion is trivial and the null
  space is the $D$-dimensional trivial-motion space, i.e.
  $\dim Z(G,p) = D$, matching $D(2-1) - 0$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{The framework-construction op}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-withgraph}

Both inductive cases build a realization of $G$ from a realization of a
\emph{different} graph: Case~I from the contraction $G/E(H)$, Case~II
from the splitting-off $G_v^{ab}$. The shared, citation-free primitive
both need is the ability to keep the hinge assignment fixed while
changing the underlying multigraph --- the supporting extensors $C(p(e))$
and hinge-row blocks depend only on the hinge data, not on which edges
$G$ carries. The one fact this phase needs from the op is the
graph-monotonicity of the motion space: deleting edges (passing to a
subgraph) can only enlarge the null space $Z(G,p)$, the direction
Cases~I and~II travel toward the smaller inductive graph.

\begin{definition}[Framework on a new graph]
  \label{def:framework-with-graph}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.withGraph}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:rigidity-matrix}
  For a body-hinge framework $F = (G, p)$ and any multigraph $G'$ on the
  same bodies, $F[G']$ is the framework with underlying multigraph $G'$
  and the same hinge assignment $p$. Every supporting extensor $C(p(e))$,
  hinge-row block, and per-edge constraint is untouched; only the set of
  edges imposing a constraint changes.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Edge deletion enlarges the motion space]
  \label{lem:motions-mono-of-graph-le}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.infinitesimalMotions_le_withGraph_of_le,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.finrank_infinitesimalMotions_le_of_graph_le}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:framework-with-graph}
  If $G' \le G$ then $Z(G,p) \subseteq Z(G', p)$, equivalently
  $\dim Z(G,p) \le \dim Z(G', p)$ --- i.e.
  $\operatorname{rank} R(G', p) \le \operatorname{rank} R(G, p)$. A
  supergraph imposes more hinge constraints, so a motion of $G$ is a
  fortiori a motion of any subgraph $G'$. This is the
  ``re-adding edges only grows the rank'' monotonicity that lifts a
  realization of a minimal $k$-dof spanning subgraph to one of the whole
  multigraph (the step \cref{prop:rigidity-matrix-prop11} uses), and the
  companion to the fixed-graph span-monotonicity Lemma~5.2.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:framework-with-graph}
  Each link of $G'$ is a link of $G$ (\texttt{Graph.IsLink.mono}), and
  the matching supporting extensors agree, so every infinitesimal motion
  of $G$ meets every constraint of $G'$: $Z(G,p) \subseteq Z(G', p)$.
  Taking dimensions (\texttt{Submodule.finrank\_mono}) gives the rank
  form.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Case I: a proper rigid subgraph}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-caseI}

\begin{lemma}[Cycle realization, short-cycle base]
  \label{lem:cycle-realization-base}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.toBodyHinge_rankHypothesis_zero}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:panel-hinge-framework, lem:theorem-55-base}
  The two-body base of \cref{lem:cycle-realization}: a panel-hinge
  framework $P$ on a two-body cover, with two edges $e_1, e_2$ joining
  the two bodies $u \ne v$ whose panel support extensors $C(p(e_1)),
  C(p(e_2))$ are linearly independent, has an infinitesimally rigid
  body-hinge interpretation $P^\flat$ --- it realizes the rank
  hypothesis at $k' = 0$ (\cref{def:rank-hypothesis}), the full rank
  $D = D(2-1) - 0$. This is the panel analogue of the base case
  \cref{lem:theorem-55-base}, lifted through the body-hinge
  interpretation, and is the brick the general panel cycle is built
  from.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:theorem-55-base}
  The body-hinge interpretation $P^\flat$ carries exactly the two
  independent supporting extensors as its hinge data, so the
  parallel-hinges-full base case \cref{lem:theorem-55-base} applies
  to $P^\flat$ verbatim.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Rigid cycle dimension bound ($|V| \le D$)]
  \label{lem:cycle-realization-dim-bound}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.card_le_screwDim_of_linearIndependent,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.card_le_screwDim_of_supportExtensor_linearIndependent}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:panel-hinge-framework, def:rigidity-matrix}
  If the supporting extensors of $m$ edges of a panel-hinge framework are linearly
  independent in the screw space $\Lambda^k \R^{k+2}$, then $m \le D = \binom{k+2}{2}$.
  This is the upper half $|V| \le D$ of the cycle hypothesis $3 \le |V| \le D$ in
  \cref{lem:cycle-realization}: a cycle of $m$ panels and $m$ hinges is infinitesimally
  rigid exactly when its $m$ supporting extensors are independent, which the
  $D$-dimensionality of the screw space (\cref{def:rigidity-matrix}) caps at $m \le D$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  A linearly independent family in a $D$-dimensional space has at most $D$ members
  ($\dim \Lambda^k \R^{k+2} = D$, the screw-space dimension of
  \cref{def:rigidity-matrix}).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Panel independence reduces to grade-2 join independence]
  \label{lem:panel-support-extensor-independence}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.panelSupportExtensor_linearIndependent_iff}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:panel-support-extensor, def:meet-complement-iso}
  A family of $m$ panel support extensors $i \mapsto C(p(e_i)) =
  \mathrm{panelSupportExtensor}(n_{u_i}, n_{v_i})$ is linearly independent in the screw
  space $\Lambda^k \R^{k+2}$ iff the family of grade-$2$ joins
  $i \mapsto n_{u_i} \wedge n_{v_i}$ (\cref{def:panel-support-extensor}) is independent in
  $\Lambda^2 \R^{k+2}$. This is the reduction at the heart of the generic-panel independence
  argument (Claim~6.4/6.9): it turns the screw-space independence of the supporting extensors
  --- what \cref{lem:cycle-realization} needs --- into an independence question on the joins of
  the panel normals, a concrete exterior-power statement a basis choice on $\Lambda^2$
  discharges (bottoming on the extensor-independence Lemma~2.1,
  \cref{lem:extensor-independence}).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  The complement isomorphism $\mathrm{complementIso} : \Lambda^2 V \simeq \Lambda^k V$
  (\cref{def:meet-complement-iso}, with $k + 2 - 2 = k$) is a linear \emph{equivalence}, and
  $\mathrm{panelSupportExtensor} = \mathrm{complementIso} \circ (\text{grade-}2\text{ join})$
  by definition. A linear equivalence both preserves and reflects linear independence, so the
  two families are independent together.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Existence of an independent panel-extensor family for $m \le D$ panels]
  \label{lem:exists-independent-panel-extensor}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.exists_independent_panelSupportExtensor,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.exists_independent_normalsJoin}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:panel-support-extensor, lem:panel-support-extensor-independence,
        lem:extensor-independence}
  For every $m \le D = \binom{k+2}{2}$ there are $m$ pairs of panel normals
  $(n_{u_i}, n_{v_i})$ whose supporting extensors $i \mapsto
  \mathrm{panelSupportExtensor}(n_{u_i}, n_{v_i})$ are linearly independent in the screw
  space $\Lambda^k \R^{k+2}$, equivalently whose grade-$2$ joins $i \mapsto n_{u_i} \wedge
  n_{v_i}$ are independent in $\Lambda^2 \R^{k+2}$. This is the existence half of the
  generic-panel independence argument (Claim~6.4/6.9): the independent supporting extensors
  \cref{lem:cycle-realization} needs for a rigid panel-cycle realization, capped from above by
  the dimension bound \cref{lem:cycle-realization-dim-bound}.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  By \cref{lem:panel-support-extensor-independence} it suffices to produce $m$ independent
  grade-$2$ joins. The witness is a basis choice rather than a perturbation: the index set of
  $2$-element subsets of $\{1, \dots, k+2\}$ has cardinality $\binom{k+2}{2} = D \ge m$, so pick
  $m$ distinct $2$-subsets and take the corresponding pairs of standard basis vectors. Each join
  $e_a \wedge e_b$ is then a member of the basis-indexed exterior-power family, which is linearly
  independent (the $\Lambda^2$-basis fact bottoming on the extensor-independence Lemma~2.1,
  \cref{lem:extensor-independence}); the chosen subfamily inherits independence via the injection
  of indices.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Rigidity of a cycle with independent extensors]
  \label{lem:cycle-realization-rigid}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.toBodyHinge_rankHypothesis_zero_cycle,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.rankHypothesis_zero_of_cycle,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.isTrivialMotion_of_isInfinitesimalMotion_cycle}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:panel-hinge-framework, def:rank-hypothesis, lem:cycle-realization-base}
  The rigidity half of \cref{lem:cycle-realization}: a panel-hinge framework on the cycle of
  $m$ bodies $0, 1, \dots, m-1$ --- the $i$-th hinge joining body $i$ to body $i+1$ (cyclically)
  --- whose $m$ supporting extensors are linearly independent in the screw space $\Lambda^k
  \R^{k+2}$ is infinitesimally rigid, realizing the rank hypothesis at $k' = 0$
  (\cref{def:rank-hypothesis}), the full rank $D(|V|-1)$ of the minimal $0$-dof case. This is the
  $m$-body generalization of the two-body short-cycle base \cref{lem:cycle-realization-base}.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:rank-parallel-full}
  Each hinge constraint puts the relative screw $S(i) - S(i+1)$ of an infinitesimal motion $S$ in
  the one-dimensional span of its supporting extensor, and the $m$ differences telescope around
  the cycle to $\sum_i (S(i) - S(i+1)) = 0$ (the shift $i \mapsto i+1$ is a bijection of the cycle
  $\Z/m$). Independence of the $m$ extensors then forces every difference to vanish --- the
  $m$-edge generalization of the parallel-hinges-full Lemma~5.3 (\cref{lem:rank-parallel-full},
  the $m=2$ case) --- so $S$ is constant around the connected cycle, hence a trivial motion. By
  \cref{def:rank-hypothesis} the framework realizes the rank hypothesis at $k'=0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Cycle realizations; KT Lemma 5.4]
  \label{lem:cycle-realization}
  \uses{def:rigidity-matrix, lem:cycle-realization-base, lem:cycle-realization-dim-bound,
        lem:cycle-realization-rigid,
        lem:panel-support-extensor-independence, lem:exists-independent-panel-extensor}
  A cycle graph $G = (V, E)$ with $3 \le |V| \le D$ has an
  infinitesimally rigid, nonparallel panel-hinge realization $(G,p)$
  --- equivalently a realization at the full rank $D(|V|-1)$, the
  target rank of the minimal $0$-dof case
  (\cref{def:rank-hypothesis}). This is Katoh--Tanigawa's Lemma~5.4,
  whose geometric content is the cycle-realization result of
  Crapo--Whiteley~\cite[Proposition~3.4]{crapoWhiteley1982} (the
  $3$-dimensional, $D = 6$ case) and
  Whiteley~\cite[Proposition~3]{whiteley1999}, the technique extending
  to general $d$ without modification. All three pieces are now formalized: the two-body base is
  \cref{lem:cycle-realization-base}, the rigidity of an $m$-body cycle whose supporting extensors
  are independent is \cref{lem:cycle-realization-rigid}, the upper bound $|V| \le D$ on the
  hypothesis is \cref{lem:cycle-realization-dim-bound}, and the generic-panel independence
  argument supplying the independent supporting extensors (bottoming on the extensor-independence
  Lemma~2.1, \cref{lem:extensor-independence}) is \cref{lem:exists-independent-panel-extensor}.
  What remains a cited rather than formalized step is only the projective assembly of these into
  a single realization on a concrete cycle graph (the genericity device, Claim~6.4/6.9, shared
  with Cases~I and~II).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \uses{lem:cycle-realization-base, lem:cycle-realization-rigid,
        lem:exists-independent-panel-extensor}
  The two-body base case is \cref{lem:cycle-realization-base}. For the
  general cycle, a single cycle of $m$ panels and $m$ hinges is infinitesimally rigid
  iff the $m$ hinge lines are independent in the Grassmann line
  geometry (\cref{lem:cycle-realization-rigid}) --- the dimension-$D$ extensor space supports at
  most $D$ independent lines, so a cycle of length $3 \le |V| \le D$ admits a
  nonparallel realization whose supporting extensors are independent
  (\cref{lem:exists-independent-panel-extensor}), hence rigid
  (\cite[Prop.~3.4]{crapoWhiteley1982}, \cite[Prop.~3]{whiteley1999}). The detailed projective
  assembly is an external geometric input, cited rather than reproved
  (KT~\cite[Lemma~5.4]{katohTanigawa2011} likewise defers it to these sources).
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Block-pinning a set of bodies]
  \label{def:pinned-motions-on}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.pinnedMotionsOn,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.pinnedMotionsOn_singleton,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.pinnedMotionsOn_eq_iInf}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:rigidity-matrix, lem:rank-delete-vertex}
  For a body-hinge framework $F = (G,p)$ and a set of bodies
  $s \subseteq V$, the \emph{block pin} $Z_s(G,p)$ is the subspace of
  infinitesimal motions vanishing on every body of $s$:
  $\{S \in Z(G,p) : S|_s = 0\}$. It is the set-level analogue of the
  pin-a-body subspace $\operatorname{pinned}(v)$
  (\cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex}), recovering it at a singleton
  ($Z_{\{v\}}(G,p) = \operatorname{pinned}(v)$), and for a nonempty
  block equals the intersection of the single-body pins,
  $Z_s(G,p) = \bigsqcap_{v \in s} \operatorname{pinned}(v)$. Fixing a
  whole block of bodies to the zero screw is the algebraic effect of
  contracting them to one pinned body --- the move Case~I makes on a
  rigid subgraph $H$ (pin all of $V(H)$), so $Z_{V(H)}(G,p)$ is the
  framework-side carrier of the rigid-block contraction.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Block-pinning drops at least the $D$ trivial-motion dimensions]
  \label{lem:pinned-motions-on-rank-bound}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.trivialMotions_inf_pinnedMotionsOn_eq_bot,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.screwDim_add_finrank_pinnedMotionsOn_le}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:pinned-motions-on, lem:rank-delete-vertex, lem:trivial-motions-rank-bound}
  For a nonempty block of bodies $s \subseteq V$,
  \[
    D + \dim Z_s(G,p) \;\le\; \dim Z(G,p),
  \]
  i.e. block-pinning $s$ cuts the motion space by at least the $D$
  trivial-motion dimensions. The $D$-dimensional trivial motions
  (\cref{lem:trivial-motions-rank-bound}) and the block pin $Z_s(G,p)$
  (\cref{def:pinned-motions-on}) are disjoint submodules of $Z(G,p)$, so their
  dimensions add to at most $\dim Z(G,p)$. This is the inequality form of the
  single-body pin-a-body equality \cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex} (an exact $+D$
  direct-sum split); pinning a rigid block $V(H)$ collapses it to one effective
  body, the residual constraints making the bound an inequality whose slack the
  contraction's inductive rank fills. The lower-bound brick of Case~I's
  block-triangular gluing.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:rank-delete-vertex}
  Pinning the whole block in particular pins any one body $v \in s$, so
  $Z_s(G,p) \subseteq \operatorname{pinned}(v)$; the latter meets the trivial
  motions only at $0$ (\cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex}), hence so does $Z_s(G,p)$.
  Both sit inside $Z(G,p)$, so $\dim(\text{trivial}) + \dim Z_s(G,p) \le \dim
  Z(G,p)$, and $\dim(\text{trivial}) = D$ (\cref{lem:trivial-motions-rank-bound}).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Case I: rigid-subgraph contraction realizes the rank; KT Lemmas 6.2, 6.3, 6.5]
  \label{lem:case-I}
  \uses{thm:minimal-kdof-reduction, lem:contraction-minimality,
        lem:rank-delete-vertex, lem:cycle-realization, def:pinned-motions-on,
        lem:pinned-motions-on-rank-bound}
  Let $G$ be a minimal $k$-dof-graph with a proper rigid subgraph $H$.
  Then $G$ has a realization at rank $D(|V|-1) - k$, built from a
  realization of the contraction $G/E(H)$ (a smaller minimal
  $k$-dof-graph by \cref{lem:contraction-minimality}, supplied the
  induction hypothesis) and a rigid realization of $H$ (the
  pin-a-body Lemma~5.1, \cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex}), glued
  block-triangularly. KT split this into the non-simple case (6.2),
  the simple case with simple contraction (6.3, via the Claim~6.4
  genericity argument), and the remaining simple case (6.5).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \uses{lem:rank-delete-vertex}
  Pin the rigid block $H$ (\cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex}) and place the
  contraction $G/E(H)$ at its inductive rank; the combined rigidity
  matrix is block-triangular, so the ranks add to $D(|V|-1) - k$. The
  genericity step (the entries are polynomials in algebraically
  independent panel coordinates, so a generic point attains the maximum
  rank over the parametrized family --- Claim~6.4) lifts a single
  good realization to a generic one.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Case II: \texorpdfstring{$k > 0$}{k > 0} splitting (1-extension)}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-caseII}

\begin{lemma}[Case II rank-lift accounting; the $+D$ core of KT Lemma 6.8]
  \label{lem:case-II-rank-lift}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.rankHypothesis_iff_finrank_pinnedMotions}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:rank-hypothesis, lem:rank-delete-vertex}
  In the codimension form of \cref{sec:molecular-rigidity-matrix},
  re-inserting (equivalently, pinning) a body $v$ shifts the realization
  count by exactly $D$. A framework $F$ realizes the target
  rank at $k'$ ($\dim Z(G,p) = D + k'$, \cref{def:rank-hypothesis})
  precisely when its body-$v$-pinned motion subspace has dimension $k'$.
  This is the algebraic $+D$ core of the panel-hinge $1$-extension: the
  pinned subspace is the null space of the rigidity matrix with $v$'s $D$
  columns deleted (the smaller graph $G - v$), and $\dim(\text{pinned at
  } v) + D = \dim Z(G,p)$ by the pin-a-body Lemma~5.1
  (\cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex}).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:rank-delete-vertex}
  Immediate from the pin-a-body identity
  $\dim(\text{pinned at } v) + D = \dim Z(G,p)$
  (\cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex}): substitute it into the definition of
  the rank hypothesis $\dim Z(G,p) = D + k'$ and cancel the $D$ term.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Case II: splitting off a reducible vertex; KT Lemmas 6.7, 6.8]
  \label{lem:case-II}
  \uses{thm:minimal-kdof-reduction, lem:reduction-step,
        lem:rank-delete-vertex, lem:case-II-rank-lift, lem:cycle-realization}
  Let $G$ be a minimal $k$-dof-graph with $k > 0$ and a reducible
  degree-2 vertex $v$ (KT~4.6), and let $G_v^{ab}$ be the splitting-off
  at $v$ (a smaller minimal $k$-dof-graph by \cref{lem:reduction-step}).
  A realization of $G_v^{ab}$ at its inductive rank extends to a
  realization of $G$ at rank $D(|V|-1) - k$. This is the panel-hinge
  analogue of Whiteley's bar-joint $1$-extension: re-inserting $v$ with
  its two hinges lifts the rank by exactly $D$ (Lemma~6.8, via the
  Claim~6.9 genericity argument).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \uses{lem:rank-delete-vertex, lem:case-II-rank-lift}
  Place $G_v^{ab}$ at its inductive realization; re-insert $v$ joined to
  $a$ and $b$ by hinges chosen generically. The rank-lift accounting
  \cref{lem:case-II-rank-lift} (via the pin-a-body Lemma~5.1,
  \cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex}) accounts for $v$'s screw freedoms, and
  the two new hinge-row blocks raise the rank by exactly $D$, giving
  $D(|V|-1) - k$. Genericity (Claim~6.9) lifts to a generic realization.
\end{proof}
