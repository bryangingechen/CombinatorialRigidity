\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

This blueprint accompanies the
\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity}{\texttt{CombinatorialRigidity}}
Lean~4 / mathlib4 formalization. The goal is a proof of \textbf{Laman's
theorem}~\cite{laman1970}: for $n \ge 2$, a graph $G$ on $n$ vertices is generically
rigid in the plane if and only if it contains a spanning subgraph $H$ with
$|E(H)| = 2n - 3$ such that every subgraph $H[S]$ on $k \ge 2$ vertices
satisfies $|E(H[S])| \le 2k - 3$. Such an $H$ is called a \emph{Laman
graph}, or \emph{minimally rigid graph in the plane}.

The Lean development follows the \emph{Henneberg-construction} route: we
characterize Laman graphs inductively via two combinatorial moves (Type~I
adds a vertex of degree~2; Type~II splits an edge), prove the moves
preserve generic rigidity, and combine these facts to deduce the ``Laman
implies generically rigid'' direction. This $\Leftarrow$ direction is the
classical strategy of Tay and Whiteley~\cite{tayWhiteley1985}, in the
modern presentation of Jord\'an~\cite{jordan2016}~\S 2.2. The converse
direction goes through the rigidity matroid. For background on
combinatorial rigidity, see
\cite{graverServatiusServatius1993,jordan2016}.

\subsection*{Phase plan}

The Lean code is organized into eleven phases, tracked in
\href{https://github.com/bryangingechen/CombinatorialRigidity/blob/master/ROADMAP.md}{\texttt{ROADMAP.md}}.
Phases~1--11 are complete. The first six phases formalize Laman's
theorem, Phase~7 packages the planar rigidity matroid as a mathlib
\texttt{Matroid} via the Lov\'asz--Yemini matroid identification,
Phase~8 reframes it as a linear matroid via \texttt{Matroid.ofFun}
at a uniformly generic placement (Lov\'asz--Yemini, linear-matroid
form), Phase~9 ships the basic $(k, \ell)$-pebble game of
Lee--Streinu~\cite{leeStreinu2008} in the matroidal regime
$\ell < 2k$ with a certificate-form correctness theorem and the
matroidal-independence corollary against the Phase~7 count matroid,
on top of a verified-DFS warmup, and Phase~10 bridges Phase~9's
\texttt{noncomputable} \texttt{runPebbleGame} to an actually-runnable
decision procedure: a computable wrapper
\texttt{runPebbleGameExec} under $[\mathrm{LinearOrder}\,V]$,
\texttt{Decidable} instances for $\mathrm{IsSparse}$ /
$\mathrm{IsTight}$ / $\mathrm{IsLaman}$, and a
\texttt{lake exe pebble-game} CLI binary. Phase~11 reshapes
Phase~9/10's $\mathrm{Option}$-shaped pebble-game algorithms
(workhorses, math/exec wrappers, and \texttt{Decidable} instances)
to return a verdict-bearing inductive
$\mathrm{PebbleGameResult}\,G\,k\,\ell$ whose constructors carry
inline witnesses --- the blocking subset $V'$ on the
$\mathrm{NOT\_SPARSE}$ branch, the partial orientation $D$ on the
accept branches --- and bumps the CLI to print those witness lines
alongside the trichotomy label, making the CLI's classification
externally checkable. As a structural-edit phase, Phase~11's
blueprint reshape lands in-place in \cref{sec:dfs},
\cref{sec:pebble-game}, and \cref{sec:executable} alongside each
Layer's Lean, with no new chapter opened.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Sparsity.} Basic $(k,\ell)$-sparsity and $(k,\ell)$-tightness
        for arbitrary $k, \ell$. (Files: \texttt{EdgesIn.lean},
        \texttt{Sparsity.lean}.)
  \item \textbf{Laman graphs.} The $(2,3)$-tight specialization, the
        $K_2$ base case, and degree facts feeding the Henneberg induction.
        (File: \texttt{Laman.lean}.)
  \item \textbf{Henneberg moves.} Type~I and Type~II moves, Laman-preservation
        in both directions, and the existence of an inverse move at every
        non-base Laman graph. (File: \texttt{Henneberg.lean}.)
  \item \textbf{Frameworks.} Frameworks, the rigidity map, infinitesimal
        rigidity, generic rigidity. (File: \texttt{Framework.lean}.)
  \item \textbf{Laman's theorem ($\Leftarrow$).} Per-move rigidity
        preservation and the induction. (Files: \texttt{HennebergRigidity.lean},
        \texttt{LamanTheorem.lean}.)
  \item \textbf{Laman's theorem ($\Rightarrow$).} The companion direction
        via the planar rigidity matroid (Lov\'asz--Yemini's easy
        identification of row-independence with $(2,3)$-sparsity).
        (Files: \texttt{RigidityMatroid.lean}, \texttt{LamanTheorem.lean}.)
  \item \textbf{Lov\'asz--Yemini matroid identification.} The harder
        converse: $(2,3)$-sparse $\Rightarrow$ row-independent at some
        placement; together with Phase~6 this gives the
        Lov\'asz--Yemini~\cite{lovaszYemini1982} row-LI $\Leftrightarrow$
        $(2,3)$-sparse identification. Packaged as a mathlib
        \texttt{Matroid} via the general $(k, \ell)$-count matroid in
        the matroidal regime $\ell < 2k$
        (Whiteley~\cite{whiteley1996}, Lee--Streinu~\cite{leeStreinu2008}).
        (Files: \texttt{CountMatroid.lean}, \texttt{MatroidIdentification.lean}.)
  \item \textbf{Linear-matroid framing.} The planar rigidity matroid
        as a linear matroid via \texttt{Matroid.ofFun} (from the
        \texttt{apnelson1/Matroid} library) of the rigidity-row
        function at a uniformly generic placement, identified with the
        combinatorial $(2,3)$-count matroid of Phase~7
        (Lov\'asz--Yemini, linear-matroid form).
        (File: \texttt{LinearRigidityMatroid.lean}.)
  \item \textbf{Pebble game.} The basic $(k, \ell)$-pebble game of
        Lee--Streinu~\cite{leeStreinu2008} (generalising the original
        $(2, 3)$ algorithm of Jacobs--Hendrickson~\cite{jacobsHendrickson1997})
        and its correctness theorem in certificate form, in the
        matroidal regime $\ell < 2k$ matching Phase~7. Preceded by a
        verified-DFS warmup. (Files:
        \texttt{Search/DFS.lean}, \texttt{PebbleGame/\{Basic,Algorithm,Correctness\}.lean}.)
  \item \textbf{Executable pebble game.} A computable wrapper
        \texttt{runPebbleGameExec} that plugs
        $\mathrm{Finset.sort}$-based list views into Phase~9's
        computable \texttt{-With} workhorses, lifting the
        certificate-form correctness theorem to a non-\texttt{noncomputable}
        body; \texttt{Decidable} instances for $\mathrm{IsSparse}\,k\,\ell$
        / $\mathrm{IsTight}\,k\,\ell$ / $\mathrm{IsLaman}$ in the
        matroidal regime $\ell < 2k$, backed by it; \texttt{\#eval}-able
        worked examples on $\mathrm{Fin}\,n$ graphs; a
        \texttt{lake exe pebble-game} CLI binary. (Files:
        \texttt{PebbleGame/\{Exec,Examples\}.lean}, \texttt{Main.lean}.)
  \item \textbf{Witness extraction.} Reshape Phase~9/10's
        $\mathrm{Option}$-shaped pebble-game algorithms to return a
        verdict-bearing inductive
        $\mathrm{PebbleGameResult}\,G\,k\,\ell$ carrying inline
        witnesses on every branch. The maximal reshape folds the
        deferred Phase~10 witness-extraction work into the canonical
        algorithm: Phase~9's existence-style failure-witness
        theorems are absorbed into the reshaped
        \texttt{tryAddEdgeWith}'s recursion, the certificate-form
        correctness theorem collapses into the verdict's type, and
        Phase~10's \texttt{Decidable} instances re-route through
        the verdict's \texttt{.isAccept} projection. Prerequisite:
        a verified-iterative computable
        $\mathrm{reachClosureComputable}$ in
        \texttt{Search/DFS.lean} (sibling of Phase~9's
        $\mathrm{reachableFinding}$), through which
        $\mathrm{PartialOrientation.reach}$ is redefined,
        eliminating the previous \texttt{Classical.decPred}
        dependency that blocked computable witness extraction.
        Layer~5 bumps the CLI surface to pattern-match on the
        verdict and emit $\mathrm{ARCS}\,u\,v$ /
        $\mathrm{BLOCKING}\,n$ + $\mathrm{VERTEX}\,w$ lines
        alongside the trichotomy label.
        (Files: \texttt{Search/DFS.lean},
        \texttt{PebbleGame/\{Basic,Algorithm,Correctness,Exec\}.lean},
        \texttt{Main.lean}.)
\end{enumerate}
