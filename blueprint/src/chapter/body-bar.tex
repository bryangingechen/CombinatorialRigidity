\section{Body-bar Tay theorem}
\label{sec:body-bar}

This chapter is the forward-mode authoritative dep-graph and lemma
index for Phases~13--15 of the formalization (tree-packing, the
$k$-frame matroid, and the body-bar frameworks / Tay's theorem); the
abstract matroid-union prerequisite is developed separately in
\cref{sec:matroid-union} (Phase~12). Its nodes are currently
\emph{red} (stated, not yet formalized): the dependency structure
below is the to-do list. Each node gains its
\texttt{\textbackslash lean\{\}} pointer and turns green as the
matching Lean lands.

The phase target is \textbf{Tay's theorem}~\cite{tay1984}: for
$n \ge 2$ and $d = n(n+1)/2$, a multigraph $G$ on $b$ bodies
is the underlying graph of an infinitesimally rigid independent
body-bar framework in $\mathbb{R}^n$ if and only if
$|E| = d(b - 1)$ and every $m$-body sub-multigraph has at most
$d(m - 1)$ edges; equivalently, by the
Tutte--Nash-Williams~\cite{tutte1961,nashWilliams1961} tree-packing
theorem, $G$ is the edge-disjoint union of $d$ spanning trees.

The proof route follows Whiteley's matroid-union
framing~\cite{whiteley1988} (a streamlining of Tay's original
induction-based proof~\cite{tay1984}, surveyed in modern form
in~\cite{whiteley1996}). Three ingredients:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The generic $k$-frame matroid on a multigraph is the
        $k$-fold union of the cycle matroid (Whiteley's
        Theorem~1).
  \item Edmonds' matroid-partition theorem~\cite{edmonds1965}
        characterizes independence in this union by the count
        $|E'| \le k|V'| - k$ on every non-empty subset.
        Tutte--Nash-Williams~\cite{tutte1961,nashWilliams1961}
        follows as a corollary.
  \item Body-bar realizations are obtained by specializing
        two-extensor row coefficients to standard-basis
        Pl\"ucker coordinates (Whiteley's witness
        construction).
\end{enumerate}

Whiteley's full irreducible-variety lift (Proposition~6
in~\cite{whiteley1988}, lifting independence from the
indeterminate setting to ``almost all'' body-bar realizations) is
\emph{deferred} out of Phase~15. The phase ships the
existence-of-realization form of Tay's theorem only: there exists
a body-bar realization in $\mathbb{R}^n$ that is infinitesimally
rigid (resp. independent) iff the count holds, witnessed by the
standard-basis specialization.

\subsection{Matroid-union machinery (prerequisite)}
\label{sec:body-bar-matroid-union}

The first ingredient is the matroid-union operation and Edmonds'
matroid-partition theorem~\cite{nashWilliams1966,edmonds1965}. These
abstract-matroid prerequisites are developed in their own chapter,
\cref{sec:matroid-union} (Phase~12, formalized locally from Peter
Nelson's \texttt{apnelson1/Matroid} package); the body-bar route
consumes them as the matroid union $\bigcup_{j} M_{j}$
(\cref{def:matroid-union}), its decomposition characterization of
independence (\cref{lem:union-indep-iff}), and the partition rank
formula (\cref{thm:matroid-partition-rank}).

\subsection{The $k$-frame matroid as a union of cycle matroids}
\label{sec:body-bar-k-frame}

The carrier throughout is mathlib's core multigraph
$\mathtt{Graph}\,\alpha\,\beta$: bodies are vertices (type $\alpha$,
$G.\mathrm{vertexSet}$), bars are edges (type $\beta$,
$G.\mathrm{edgeSet}$). The graphic matroid is
$G.\mathrm{cycleMatroid} : \mathtt{Matroid}\,\beta$ from the
\texttt{apnelson1/Matroid} package, whose bases are the forests of
$G$ and whose rank on $E'$ is $|V'| - c(E')$ (vertices spanned minus
connected components).

\begin{definition}[Generic $k$-frame matroid]
  \label{def:k-frame-matroid}
  \lean{Graph.kFrameMatroid}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:matroid-union}
  For a multigraph $G$ and $k \ge 1$, the generic \emph{$k$-frame
  matroid} $F(G, X)$ on the edge type $\beta$ is the linear matroid
  (via \texttt{Matroid.ofFun}) of the formal $k \cdot |V|$-column
  matrix whose row for edge $e = (u, v)$ carries indeterminate
  coefficients across the $k$ vertex blocks, placing the generic
  body-bar incidence pattern in each block. The coefficients are
  realized over the indeterminate field
  $K := \mathrm{Frac}\,\mathbb{Q}[X_{e, j} : (e, j) \in \beta \times
  \mathrm{Fin}\,k]$: the row for bar $e$ in block $j$ is
  $X_{e, j}$ times the signed graph-incidence row of $e$ (the row
  vector representing $G.\mathrm{cycleMatroid}$), placing $k$
  independent indeterminate-scaled copies of the incidence pattern.
\end{definition}

The identification with the cycle-matroid union is proved by matching
the two matroids' independent sets (\cref{lem:k-frame-indep-iff-count}):
both have ground set $E(G)$, so by matroid extensionality it suffices to
show their independence predicates agree. The cycle-matroid-union side is
already characterized by the $(k,k)$-sparsity count
(\cref{thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse}), so the work is to give the
generic side the \emph{same} characterization. That is Whiteley's
column-reorder / nonzero-monomial argument~\cite{whiteley1988} \S2.1,
which we factor into its two directions
(\cref{lem:k-frame-nonzero-monomial-forest},
\cref{lem:k-frame-specialize-forest}) — the genericity machinery over the
indeterminate field $K = \mathrm{Frac}\,\mathbb{Q}[X_{e,j}]$ — and a
characterization lemma that packages them against the union-side count.

The forward direction (generic independence $\Rightarrow$ the count) is
formalized by a \emph{rank-counting} route rather than Whiteley's original
nonzero-monomial expansion: the two arguments give the same bound, but the
rank count avoids the $\mathrm{MvPolynomial}$ determinant machinery. Each
$k$-frame row $\mathrm{kFrameRow}\,e$ places, in every block $j$, the scalar
multiple $X_{e,j} \cdot (\text{signed incidence row of } e)$, so it lives in
the $k$-fold product subspace $\mathrm{blockPiSpan}$ whose $j$-th block is the
$K$-span of \emph{all} signed incidence rows of $G$. Hence the whole
$k$-frame row space is contained in $\mathrm{blockPiSpan}$
(\cref{lem:k-frame-span-le-pi}). The dimension of $\mathrm{blockPiSpan}$
is $k$ times the dimension of the incidence-row span, and that span dimension
is exactly the cycle-matroid rank (the cycle-matroid representation,
\cref{def:k-frame-matroid}'s reused $\mathrm{signedIncMatrix}$); so linear
independence of $|E'|$ rows forces $|E'| \le k \cdot r_{G.\mathrm{cycleMatroid}}(E')$,
and the same applies to every $Y \subseteq E'$.

\begin{lemma}[$k$-frame row space lies in the block-diagonal product span]
  \label{lem:k-frame-span-le-pi}
  \lean{Graph.span_kFrameRow_le_blockPiSpan, Graph.kFrameRow_mem_blockPiSpan,
    Graph.blockPiSpan}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-frame-matroid}
  Let $\mathrm{blockPiSpan}$ be the $\mathrm{Fin}\,k$-fold product subspace of
  $\mathrm{Fin}\,k \to \alpha \to K$ whose $j$-th block is the $K$-span of the
  signed incidence rows $\{\mathrm{signedIncMatrix}\,e\}_{e}$ of $G$. Then
  every $k$-frame row $\mathrm{kFrameRow}\,e$ lies in $\mathrm{blockPiSpan}$,
  and consequently the span of all $k$-frame rows is contained in
  $\mathrm{blockPiSpan}$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  In block $j$ the $k$-frame row is $X_{e,j} \cdot (\text{signed incidence
  row of } e)$, a scalar multiple of an incidence row, hence in the block's
  span; so the row lies in the product subspace, and the span of all rows
  follows by \texttt{span\_le}.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Dimension of the constant block-product subspace]
  \label{lem:k-frame-pi-finrank}
  \lean{Graph.finrank_constPiSpan, Graph.constPiSpanEquiv}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:k-frame-span-le-pi}
  For a finite-dimensional block submodule $W$, the constant $\mathrm{Fin}\,k$-fold
  product subspace $\mathrm{Submodule.pi}\,\mathrm{univ}\,(\_\mapsto W)$ has
  $K$-dimension $k \cdot \dim_K W$. (It is linearly equivalent to the plain
  product $\mathrm{Fin}\,k \to W$, whose dimension is the $k$-fold sum.)
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  The forward map projects each of the $k$ blocks and the inverse re-bundles, a
  linear equivalence onto $\mathrm{Fin}\,k \to W$; its dimension is $\sum_{j} \dim_K W
  = k \cdot \dim_K W$ by the finite-product dimension formula.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Incidence-span dimension is the cycle-matroid rank]
  \label{lem:k-frame-incidence-finrank-rk}
  \lean{Matroid.Rep.finrank_span_image_eq_rk,
    Graph.finrank_span_signedIncMatrix_eq_cycleMatroid_rk}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-frame-matroid}
  For any finite-rank matroid $M$ with a representation $v$, the $K$-dimension of the
  span of $v(Y)$ equals the matroid rank $r_M(Y)$. Specialized to the cycle-matroid
  representation by the signed incidence rows (\cref{def:k-frame-matroid}'s reused
  $\mathrm{signedIncMatrix}$):
  $\dim_K \mathrm{span}_K\{\mathrm{signedIncMatrix}\,e : e \in Y\}
  = r_{G.\mathrm{cycleMatroid}}(Y)$ — piece~(2) of the forward rank count.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  A basis $I$ of $Y$ in $M$ has $v(Y) \subseteq \mathrm{span}_K v(I)$ and $v$ linearly
  independent on $I$, so the two spans agree; the independent image has dimension
  $|v(I)| = |I| = r_M(Y)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Generic independence $\Rightarrow$ forest count]
  \label{lem:k-frame-nonzero-monomial-forest}
  \lean{Graph.forest_count_of_linearIndepOn_kFrameRow,
    Graph.blockPiSpanOn, Graph.span_kFrameRowOn_le_blockPiSpanOn,
    Graph.finrank_blockPiSpanOn}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-frame-matroid, lem:k-frame-span-le-pi, lem:k-frame-pi-finrank,
    lem:k-frame-incidence-finrank-rk}
  Let $E' \subseteq E(G)$ be a bar set whose generic $k$-frame rows
  $\{X_{e,j} \cdot (\text{signed incidence row of } e)\}_{e \in E'}$ are
  linearly independent over $K$. Then $E'$ satisfies the count
  $|Y| \le k \cdot r_{G.\mathrm{cycleMatroid}}(Y)$ for every $Y \subseteq E'$
  (equivalently, $E'$ decomposes into $k$ forests of $G$).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-frame-matroid, lem:k-frame-span-le-pi, lem:k-frame-pi-finrank,
    lem:k-frame-incidence-finrank-rk}
  The forward half of Whiteley~\cite{whiteley1988} \S2.1, by the rank count.
  Restricting to any $Y \subseteq E'$, the $|Y|$ generic rows remain linearly
  independent, so $|Y| = \dim_K \mathrm{span}_K\{\mathrm{kFrameRow}\,e :
  e \in Y\}$. That span sits inside the block-diagonal product subspace
  (\cref{lem:k-frame-span-le-pi}) restricted to $Y$, whose dimension is
  $k \cdot \dim_K \mathrm{span}_K\{\mathrm{signedIncMatrix}\,e : e \in Y\}
  = k \cdot r_{G.\mathrm{cycleMatroid}}(Y)$
  (\cref{lem:k-frame-pi-finrank}, \cref{lem:k-frame-incidence-finrank-rk}).
  Hence $|Y| \le k \cdot r_{G.\mathrm{cycleMatroid}}(Y)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Block-diagonal specialization of the rows on a forest packing]
  \label{lem:k-frame-specialize-li}
  \lean{Graph.specRow_linearIndependent}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-frame-matroid}
  Let $\mathrm{Fs} : \mathrm{Fin}\,k \to \mathcal P(\beta)$ be a $k$-tuple of acyclic
  bar sets (a forest packing). The rows obtained by placing, for a bar $e \in
  \mathrm{Fs}_j$, the signed incidence row $\mathrm{signedIncMatrix}\,e$ in block
  $j$ and $0$ in every other block — i.e. $\mathrm{Pi.single}\,j\,
  (\mathrm{signedIncMatrix}\,e)$ — are linearly independent over $K$, indexed by
  the disjoint union $\bigsqcup_j \mathrm{Fs}_j$. This is the
  specialized-to-full-rank matrix of the reverse half: setting the block-$j$
  variable $X_{e,j}$ to $1$ on $\mathrm{Fs}_j$ and $0$ elsewhere turns each generic
  $k$-frame row into exactly this block-single row.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Each block $j$ is the signed-incidence matrix of the forest $\mathrm{Fs}_j$,
  whose rows are linearly independent over any field (the cycle-matroid
  representation restricted to an acyclic set,
  $\mathtt{Graph.orientation.isAcyclicSet\_linearIndepOn}$). A family supported on
  disjoint blocks with each block independent is independent
  ($\mathtt{Pi.linearIndependent\_single}$).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Generic independence reduces to independence over the polynomial ring]
  \label{lem:k-frame-li-over-poly-ring}
  \lean{Graph.linearIndepOn_kFrameRow_iff_over_polyRing}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-frame-matroid}
  The coefficient field $K = \mathrm{Frac}(R)$ is the field of fractions of the
  polynomial ring $R = \mathbb{Q}[X_{e,j}]$. For any bar set $E' \subseteq E(G)$,
  the generic $k$-frame rows of $E'$ are linearly independent over $K$ if and
  only if they are linearly independent over $R$. This moves the remaining
  nonzero-minor / specialization argument off the fraction field (where no
  general ring homomorphism extends) and onto the integral domain $R$, where the
  indeterminate-coefficient minors are honest polynomials.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Immediate from \texttt{LinearIndependent.iff\_fractionRing} applied to the fixed
  row family in the $K$-module $\mathrm{Fin}\,k \to \alpha \to K$, which is also an
  $R$-module via the algebra map, with the scalar tower inherited from the product
  structure.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[A $(k,k)$-sparse bar set is a $k$-forest packing]
  \label{lem:k-frame-forest-packing-of-sparse}
  \lean{Graph.exists_forestPacking_cover_of_isSparse_restrict}
  \leanok
  \uses{thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse}
  If the edge-restriction $G \restriction E'$ is $(k,k)$-sparse, then there is a
  $k$-tuple $\mathrm{Fs} : \mathrm{Fin}\,k \to \mathcal P(\beta)$ of acyclic bar
  sets (forests of $G$) whose union is exactly $E'$. This is the family on which
  \cref{lem:k-frame-specialize-li} places its block-diagonal full-rank
  specialization: each $e \in E'$ lies in some $\mathrm{Fs}_j$, so setting
  $X_{e,j} \mapsto 1$ on $\mathrm{Fs}_j$ (and $0$ elsewhere) turns the generic
  $k$-frame row of $e$ into the block-single row of \cref{lem:k-frame-specialize-li}.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  By \cref{thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse}, $(k,k)$-sparsity of
  $G \restriction E'$ is independence of $E'$ in the $k$-fold cycle-matroid union,
  and \texttt{Matroid.union\_indep\_iff} unpacks that into a per-copy acyclic cover
  (via the acyclic-set characterization of independence in the cycle matroid).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Specialization sends the generic rows to the block-single rows]
  \label{lem:k-frame-specialize-identity}
  \lean{Graph.forestEval_kFrameRowR_eq_single, Graph.kFrameRowR,
    Graph.kFrameRow_eq_map_kFrameRowR, Graph.forestEval, Graph.signedIncMatrix_map}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-frame-matroid, lem:k-frame-specialize-li}
  Write the $k$-frame rows over the polynomial ring $R = \mathbb{Q}[X_{e,j}]$ rather
  than over $K = \mathrm{Frac}(R)$ — the generic row $\mathrm{kFrameRow}\,e$ is the
  image of the $R$-valued row $\mathrm{kFrameRowR}\,e =
  (X_{e,j} \cdot \mathrm{signedIncMatrix}\,e)_j$ under the algebra map $R \to K$
  applied entrywise. For a \emph{disjoint} $k$-forest packing $\mathrm{Fs}$ and a
  bar $e \in \mathrm{Fs}_{j_0}$, the evaluation homomorphism $\varphi : R \to
  \mathbb{Q}$ sending $X_{e,j} \mapsto 1$ if $e \in \mathrm{Fs}_j$ and $0$ otherwise
  carries $\mathrm{kFrameRowR}\,e$ to exactly $\mathrm{Pi.single}\,j_0\,
  (\mathrm{signedIncMatrix}\,e)$ — the block-single row of
  \cref{lem:k-frame-specialize-li}.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Each indeterminate scales the incidence row, which has entries in $\{0, \pm 1\}$
  and so commutes with any ring homomorphism (the naturality lemma
  $\mathtt{signedIncMatrix\_map}$). In block $j_0$ the coefficient $X_{e,j_0}$
  evaluates to $1$ (as $e \in \mathrm{Fs}_{j_0}$), leaving the real signed-incidence
  row; in every other block $j \ne j_0$ disjointness gives $e \notin \mathrm{Fs}_j$,
  so $X_{e,j}$ evaluates to $0$ and the block vanishes.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Forest decomposition $\Rightarrow$ generic independence]
  \label{lem:k-frame-specialize-forest}
  \lean{Graph.linearIndepOn_kFrameRow_of_isSparse_restrict}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-frame-matroid, lem:k-frame-specialize-li, lem:k-frame-li-over-poly-ring,
    lem:k-frame-forest-packing-of-sparse, lem:k-frame-specialize-identity}
  Conversely, suppose $E' \subseteq E(G)$ decomposes as
  $E' = \bigsqcup_{j} E'_j$ with each $G \restriction E'_j$ a forest
  (equivalently, the union-side count holds on $E'$). Setting the block-$j$
  variables $X_{e,j}$ to $1$ on $E'_j$ and $0$ elsewhere specializes the
  generic $k$-frame rows of $E'$ to a block-diagonal matrix whose $j$-th
  block is the real signed-incidence matrix of the forest $E'_j$, which has
  full row rank. The specialized rows are therefore linearly independent
  over $\mathbb{Q}$, and a specialization that makes the rows independent
  witnesses linear independence of the generic rows over $K$ (a nonzero
  minor specializing to a nonzero value cannot be the zero polynomial).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-frame-matroid}
  The reverse half of Whiteley~\cite{whiteley1988} \S2.1. Each forest
  $E'_j$ has linearly independent incidence rows (the cycle-matroid
  representation, \cref{def:k-frame-matroid}'s reused $\mathrm{signedIncMatrix}$),
  and the block-diagonal arrangement keeps the blocks independent. Lifting
  back: by \cref{lem:k-frame-li-over-poly-ring} it suffices to argue over the
  polynomial ring $R$, where a matrix with a specialization of full rank has full
  generic rank (a nonsingular maximal minor of the specialized block-diagonal
  matrix is the image of a minor that is therefore a nonzero polynomial), so the
  unspecialized indeterminate rows are independent over $R$ and hence over $K$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Generic $k$-frame independence by the count]
  \label{lem:k-frame-indep-iff-count}
  \lean{Graph.kFrameMatroid_indep_iff_isSparse_restrict}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-frame-matroid, lem:k-frame-nonzero-monomial-forest,
    lem:k-frame-specialize-forest, thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse}
  A bar set $E' \subseteq E(G)$ is independent in the generic $k$-frame
  matroid $F(G, X)$ if and only if $G \restriction E'$ is $(k,k)$-sparse —
  equivalently, $|Y| \le k \cdot r_{G.\mathrm{cycleMatroid}}(Y)$ for every
  $Y \subseteq E'$. This is precisely the count that
  \cref{thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse} attaches to independence in
  the $k$-fold cycle-matroid union.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:k-frame-nonzero-monomial-forest, lem:k-frame-specialize-forest}
  Independence in $F(G, X)$ is linear independence of the generic rows
  (the \texttt{Matroid.ofFun} independence predicate). The forward
  implication is \cref{lem:k-frame-nonzero-monomial-forest}; the reverse is
  \cref{lem:k-frame-specialize-forest}. The resulting count is the one
  \cref{thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse} reads off $(k,k)$-sparsity.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[$k$-frame matroid is the $k$-fold cycle-matroid union;
  Whiteley Theorem~1]
  \label{thm:k-frame-union-cycle}
  \uses{def:k-frame-matroid, def:matroid-union,
    lem:k-frame-indep-iff-count, thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse}
  For every multigraph $G$ and every $k \ge 1$,
  \[
    F(G, X) \;=\; \bigcup_{j = 1}^{k} G.\mathrm{cycleMatroid},
  \]
  the $k$-fold union of the cycle matroid of $G$ (both sides
  matroids on the edge type $\beta$).
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  \uses{lem:k-frame-indep-iff-count, thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse}
  Both matroids have ground set $E(G)$
  (\cref{def:k-frame-matroid}), so by matroid extensionality
  (\texttt{Matroid.ext\_indep}) it suffices to match their independent
  sets. A bar set $E' \subseteq E(G)$ is independent in $F(G, X)$ iff
  $G \restriction E'$ is $(k,k)$-sparse
  (\cref{lem:k-frame-indep-iff-count}), and independent in the cycle-matroid
  union under the same condition
  (\cref{thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse}); the two characterizations
  coincide.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Tree-packing as a corollary}
\label{sec:body-bar-tree-packing}

Specializing the matroid-partition theorem to $k$ copies of the
cycle matroid turns \cref{thm:k-frame-union-cycle} into a count
characterization, recovering the Tutte--Nash-Williams tree-packing
theorem~\cite{tutte1961,nashWilliams1961}. We introduce a
$\mathtt{Graph}$-native sparsity predicate for the statement; it is
fresh to Phase~13 and is \emph{not} migrated from the
$\mathtt{SimpleGraph}$ sparsity of \cref{def:isSparse}.

\begin{definition}[Graph $(k, \ell)$-sparsity and tightness]
  \label{def:graph-sparse}
  \lean{Graph.IsSparse, Graph.IsTight}
  \leanok
  A multigraph $G$ is \emph{$(k, \ell)$-sparse} if every non-empty
  set of bars $E'$ spanning vertex set $V'$ satisfies
  $|E'| \le k|V'| - \ell$, and \emph{$(k, \ell)$-tight} if it is
  sparse with global equality $|E| = k|V| - \ell$. Body-bar Tay is
  the $\ell = k = d$ case.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Union independence by the count;
  Whiteley Corollary~3]
  \label{thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse}
  \lean{Graph.unionPow_cycleMatroid_indep_iff_isSparse_restrict}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:graph-sparse, thm:matroid-partition-rank,
    lem:union-indep-iff}
  A set of bars $E' \subseteq E(G)$ is independent in the $k$-fold
  cycle-matroid union $\bigcup_{j=1}^{k} G.\mathrm{cycleMatroid}$ if
  and only if $G$ restricted to that set is $(k, k)$-sparse.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{thm:matroid-partition-rank, lem:union-indep-iff}
  The partition rank formula (\cref{thm:matroid-partition-rank}),
  specialized to the constant family, characterizes independence in
  the union by the count $|E''| \le k \cdot r(E'')$ for every
  $E'' \subseteq E'$ (\cref{lem:union-indep-iff}). For the forward
  direction this count combined with the cycle-matroid rank formula
  $r(E'') = |V''| - c(E'')$ and $c(E'') \ge 1$ on a non-empty $E''$
  gives $(k, k)$-sparsity. For the reverse, sparsity alone yields
  only $|E''| \le k(|V''| - 1)$, weaker than the count when $E''$ has
  several non-trivial components; decomposing $E''$ along the
  connected components of $G \restriction E''$ (deleting isolated
  vertices) and summing the per-component sparsity bounds, with rank
  additive over the skew component family, recovers
  $|E''| \le k \cdot r(E'')$.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Tutte--Nash-Williams tree-packing]
  \label{thm:tutte-nash-williams}
  \lean{Graph.tutte_nash_williams}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:graph-sparse, thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse}
  A multigraph $G$ is the edge-disjoint union of $k$ forests if and
  only if $G$ is $(k, k)$-sparse.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse, lem:union-indep-iff}
  Edge-disjoint $k$-forest decomposition is exactly independence of
  the whole edge set in the cycle-matroid union
  (\cref{lem:union-indep-iff}, with the forests as the per-copy
  independent sets), which \cref{thm:unionPow-cycle-indep-iff-sparse}
  identifies with $(k, k)$-sparsity. The disjointness is free: an
  acyclic cover disjointifies (subsets of forests are forests), and a
  disjoint cover is a cover.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Spanning-tree refinement]
  \label{cor:k-spanning-trees}
  \lean{Graph.isSpanningTreePacking_of_isTight}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:graph-sparse, thm:tutte-nash-williams}
  If $G$ is connected and $(k, k)$-tight then the $k$ edge-disjoint
  forests of \cref{thm:tutte-nash-williams} are spanning trees. This
  is the ``union of $d$ spanning trees'' form of Tay's theorem
  (Whiteley Theorem~13).
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{thm:tutte-nash-williams}
  Connectivity gives $r(G.\mathrm{cycleMatroid}) = |V| - 1$, so the
  tight global equality $|E| = k|V| - k$ reads as $|E| = k \cdot r$.
  The $k$ forests cover $E$ disjointly, so $\sum_{i} |F_i| = |E| = k
  \cdot r$, while each forest is independent and hence $|F_i| \le r$;
  equality of the sum with $k \cdot r$ forces $|F_i| = r$ for every
  $i$, so each $F_i$ is a maximal acyclic set, i.e.\ a spanning tree.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Body-bar frameworks and the rigidity matrix}
\label{sec:body-bar-framework}

Following Whiteley~\cite{whiteley1988} \S3 and Tay~\cite{tay1984}
\S5, a body-bar framework assigns each bar a two-extensor (Pl\"ucker)
coordinate in $\mathbb{R}^{d}$, $d = n(n+1)/2$; the rigidity map is
the bar-constraint linear map analogous to the bar-joint rigidity
map of \cref{sec:frameworks}.

\begin{definition}[Body-bar framework]
  \label{def:body-bar-framework}
  A \emph{body-bar framework} in $\mathbb{R}^{n}$ on a multigraph
  $G$ pairs $G$ with a placement assigning each bar
  $e \in G.\mathrm{edgeSet}$ a two-extensor coordinate in
  $\mathbb{R}^{d}$, $d = n(n+1)/2$ (a point of the Pl\"ucker
  embedding of $\mathrm{Gr}(2, n+1)$, with degenerate coordinates
  permitted).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Body-bar rigidity map]
  \label{def:rigidity-map-body-bar}
  \uses{def:body-bar-framework}
  The \emph{rigidity map} of a body-bar framework is the linear map
  from body motions ($G.\mathrm{vertexSet} \to \mathbb{R}^{d}$) to
  bar constraints ($G.\mathrm{edgeSet} \to \mathbb{R}$) with one row
  per bar, the constraint row of Whiteley~\cite{whiteley1988} \S3.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Infinitesimal rigidity of a body-bar framework]
  \label{def:infinitesimally-rigid-body-bar}
  \uses{def:rigidity-map-body-bar}
  A body-bar framework on $b$ bodies is \emph{infinitesimally rigid}
  when its rigidity map has rank $d \cdot b - d$, i.e.\ its kernel is
  exactly the $d$-dimensional space of trivial screw motions ---
  the body-bar analogue of \cref{def:isInfinitesimallyRigid}.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Tay's theorem (existence-of-realization form)}
\label{sec:body-bar-tay}

The chapter's target, in Whiteley's witness form~\cite{whiteley1988}
(Theorem~8). We ship only the \emph{existence-of-realization} form:
there exists an independent (resp.\ isostatic) body-bar framework iff
the count holds, witnessed by the standard-basis specialization.
Whiteley's stronger ``almost all realizations are rigid'' phrasing,
via the irreducible-variety lift of Proposition~6
in~\cite{whiteley1988}, is \emph{deferred} out of Phase~15
(re-assessed when the body-hinge phase opens; see \texttt{ROADMAP.md}
\S15).

\begin{theorem}[Tay's theorem, independent form; Whiteley Theorem~8]
  \label{thm:tay-witness}
  \uses{def:body-bar-framework, def:infinitesimally-rigid-body-bar,
    def:graph-sparse, thm:tutte-nash-williams, cor:k-spanning-trees}
  Let $n \ge 2$ and $d = n(n+1)/2$. A multigraph $G$ carries an
  independent body-bar framework in $\mathbb{R}^{n}$ iff $G$ is
  $(d, d)$-sparse (equivalently, by \cref{thm:tutte-nash-williams},
  $G$ is the union of $d$ edge-disjoint forests). It carries an
  \emph{isostatic} (independent and infinitesimally rigid) body-bar
  framework iff $G$ is $(d, d)$-tight (equivalently, by
  \cref{cor:k-spanning-trees}, the union of $d$ spanning trees) ---
  the count $|E| = d(b - 1)$ of Tay~\cite{tay1984}.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
  \uses{thm:k-frame-union-cycle, thm:tutte-nash-williams,
    cor:k-spanning-trees}
  Specialize each bar's two-extensor coordinate to the standard
  basis vector $b_{e} = e_{j(e)} \in \mathbb{R}^{d}$, where $j(e)$
  is the index of $e$'s forest in a chosen tree-packing partition
  (\cref{thm:tutte-nash-williams}). This is the reverse direction of
  \cref{thm:k-frame-union-cycle} lifted from indeterminate to real
  coefficients: the rigidity matrix becomes block-diagonal with
  $d$ full-rank forest-incidence blocks, so the framework is
  independent, and isostatic exactly when each block is a spanning
  tree (\cref{cor:k-spanning-trees}). The degenerate $b_{e}$ lie on
  the Pl\"ucker variety vacuously, so this is a valid body-bar
  realization.
\end{proof}
