\subsection{The genericity device (KT Claims 6.4/6.9)}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-genericity}

KT Claims~6.4 and~6.9 supply the analytic fact each case of the induction
relies on: a rank attained at \emph{one} panel-hinge realization is attained
generically, since every entry of the rigidity matrix is polynomial in the
panel normals and each rank-$r$ locus is Zariski-open.
\cref{lem:genericity-device} states this fact; it is what discharges the
genericity hypothesis carried by \cref{lem:case-I}, \cref{lem:case-II}, and
the lower bound of \cref{prop:rigidity-matrix-prop11}, and it underlies the
projective assembly of \cref{lem:cycle-realization}. The device does not
itself construct a realization: each case still supplies its own \emph{seed}
realization, whose rank the device lifts to a generic point, and --- since
the seed's rank is counted over the whole ambient body set while the
induction wants rigidity only on $V(G)$ --- \cref{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-relative}
below converts that absolute count into the relative one the induction
needs. For Case~I the seed is KT eq.~(6.3)'s block-triangular construction
over one common framework (\cref{lem:case-I-realization}), using KT
Claim~6.4 (\cref{lem:claim-6-4}) for the surviving block; for Case~II it is
the $1$-extension normal of \cref{lem:case-II-realization-placement}. The
upper bound of \cref{prop:rigidity-matrix-prop11} is unrelated to
genericity --- it comes from the partition argument of
\cref{lem:trivial-motions-rank-bound}.

\begin{lemma}[Genericity device, codimension form; KT Claim 6.4, Claim 6.9]
  \label{lem:genericity-device}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.exists_good_realization,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.exists_good_realization_const}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:rigidity-matrix, def:panel-hinge-framework}
  Fix a multigraph $G$ and regard a panel-hinge realization as a point
  $p \in \R^N$ of the panel-coordinate space (the per-vertex panel normals).
  Each entry of the panel-hinge rigidity matrix $R(G,p)$ is a polynomial in
  $p$ --- of degree two, bilinear in the normals (\cref{def:rigidity-matrix},
  \cref{def:panel-hinge-framework}). Hence for each $r$ the locus
  $\{p : \operatorname{rank} R(G,p) \ge r\}$, cut out by the non-vanishing of
  some $r \times r$ minor, is Zariski-open, so $\operatorname{rank} R(G,p)$
  is lower semicontinuous and attains its maximum on a dense generic set; a
  rank attained at one realization is attained generically. Dually, in the
  codimension convention of \cref{sec:molecular-rigidity-matrix}
  ($\operatorname{rank} R(G,p) = D|V| - \dim Z(G,p)$,
  \cref{def:dof-generic}), $\dim Z(G,p)$ is upper semicontinuous and
  minimized generically.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  A maximal non-vanishing minor of $R(G,p_0)$ is a polynomial in $p$, nonzero
  at $p_0$; over the infinite field $\R$ a nonzero polynomial is not the
  zero function, so its non-vanishing locus is dense and nonempty, and there
  $\operatorname{rank} R(G,p)$ is at least the size of the minor. Finitely
  many such generic conditions intersect, each the complement of a proper
  algebraic subset. See \cite[\S6.1, \S6.3]{katohTanigawa2011} (Claim~6.4 and
  its Case-II twin, Claim~6.9).

  The parameter here ranges over the whole panel-coordinate space $\R^N$,
  not a single line, so the argument is genuinely multivariate: the minor's
  Gram determinant is one polynomial in $N$ variables, nonzero at the
  witness realization, hence nonzero at some point, where the rank is at
  least the witnessed corank --- in the codimension form used throughout,
  $\#s + \dim Z(F\,p) \le D|V|$ at the produced realization. The same fact
  applied to a single, already-constructed realization rather than a
  varying family is the form Case~I's block-triangular construction uses.
\end{proof}

\noindent The device supplies the genericity step inside
\cref{lem:case-I-realization}, \cref{lem:case-II-realization}, and
\cref{lem:case-III}, each of which constructs its own seed realization and
lifts its rank through the device; it recurs later in Case~III's candidate
framework, in the genericity argument for the $D$-candidate disjunction
(Claim~6.12).

\subsection{The \texorpdfstring{$V(G)$}{V(G)}-relative count}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-relative}

The device (\cref{lem:genericity-device}) bounds the corank
\emph{absolutely}, over the whole screw-assignment space $\R^{D|V_\alpha|}$
of the ambient body set: a produced realization satisfies
$\#s + \dim Z(G,p) \le D|V_\alpha|$. Rigidity on $V(G)$
(\cref{def:rank-hypothesis}) is instead $V(G)$-\emph{relative}. For a
non-spanning inductive subgraph the two differ by the
$D(|V_\alpha| - |V(G)|)$ free dimensions of the bodies outside $V(G)$ ---
each carries no hinge constraint, hence is a free motion of every
realization --- so the absolute count alone never forces rigidity on
$V(G)$. The three lemmas below convert one into the other;
\cref{lem:case-I-realization} and \cref{lem:case-II-realization} both
compose with them and the device to reach rigidity on $V(G)$.

\begin{lemma}[Isolated bodies carry no rigidity: the relative split]
  \label{lem:relative-screw-split}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.finrank_pinnedMotionsOn_vertexSet}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:rigidity-matrix, def:rank-hypothesis, def:pinned-motions-on}
  The screw-assignment space $\R^{D|V_\alpha|}$ splits as a product along
  $V(G)$ and its complement, and a body $w \in \alpha \setminus V(G)$
  carries no hinge constraint, so its $D$ screw coordinates are free in
  every infinitesimal motion. The block pin on the whole vertex set $V(G)$
  therefore captures exactly the free isolated bodies:
  \[
    \dim Z_{V(G)}(G,p) \;=\; D\,(|V_\alpha| - |V(G)|).
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  An assignment vanishing on $V(G)$ automatically satisfies every hinge
  constraint $S(u) - S(v) = 0$ (both endpoints lie in $V(G)$), so
  $Z_{V(G)}(G,p)$ is the kernel of the projection onto the $V(G)$
  coordinates, the intersection of the single-body kernels over $V(G)$.
  Identifying that kernel with the product over the complement
  $\alpha \setminus V(G)$ gives its dimension, $D(|V_\alpha| - |V(G)|)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[The genericity device, $V(G)$-relative form]
  \label{lem:relative-device-count}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.exists_relative_full_count_ofParam}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:genericity-device, lem:relative-screw-split,
        lem:rows-polynomial-in-normals}
  Let $G$ be a multigraph with endpoint selector $\mathrm{ends}$ and
  nonempty body set $V(G)$. If at one free normal assignment $q_0$ the
  rigidity rows of the free-normal panel framework on $G$ indexed by $s$
  are linearly independent and $\#s \ge D(|V(G)|-1)$, then there is a
  normal assignment $q$ at which
  \[
    \dim Z(G,q) \;\le\; D\,\bigl(|\alpha \setminus V(G)| + 1\bigr).
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Apply the device \cref{lem:rows-polynomial-in-normals} to the witness
  $(q_0, s)$ to get a normal assignment $q$ with $\#s + \dim Z(G,q) \le
  D|\alpha|$; substitute $|\alpha| = |V(G)| + |\alpha \setminus V(G)|$ and
  $\#s \ge D(|V(G)|-1)$, then cancel.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[The relative count implies rigidity on $V(G)$]
  \label{lem:isInfRigidOn-of-relative-count}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.isInfinitesimallyRigidOn_vertexSet_of_finrank_le}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:relative-screw-split, lem:rank-delete-vertex, lem:case-I}
  Let $F$ be a body-hinge framework with nonempty body set $V(G)$. If
  \[
    \dim Z(G,p) \;\le\; D\,\bigl(|\alpha \setminus V(G)| + 1\bigr),
  \]
  then $F$ is infinitesimally rigid on $V(G)$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Pick any $v_0 \in V(G)$ and read rigidity on $V(G)$ off the Case~I bridge
  \cref{lem:case-I} at the trivially-rigid singleton block $\{v_0\}$: it
  reduces to $\operatorname{pinned}(v_0) \le Z_{V(G)}(G,p)$. The reverse
  containment is automatic, so it suffices to match dimensions. The
  single-body pin has $\dim \operatorname{pinned}(v_0) = \dim Z(G,p) - D$
  (\cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex}), capped at $D|\alpha \setminus V(G)|$ by
  the hypothesis; the block pin on $V(G)$ has exactly that dimension
  (\cref{lem:relative-screw-split}). Equal dimensions on a containment
  force equality.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Realization glue from a witnessed independent row family]
  \label{lem:realization-of-independent-rows}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.hasFullRankRealization_of_independent_panelRow}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:rank-hypothesis, lem:relative-device-count,
        lem:isInfRigidOn-of-relative-count, lem:rows-polynomial-in-normals}
  Fix a multigraph $G$ on a nonempty body set $V(G)$ and an endpoint
  selector $\mathrm{ends}$ recording each edge's link. If at \emph{one}
  free normal assignment $q_0$ the rigidity rows of the free-normal panel
  framework on $G$ indexed by $s$ are linearly independent and
  $\#s \ge D(|V(G)|-1)$, then $G$ has a full-rank panel realization
  (\cref{def:rank-hypothesis}, the $V(G)$-relative rank).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Apply \cref{lem:relative-device-count} to the witness $(q_0, s)$ to
  obtain a normal assignment $q$ with $\dim Z(G,q) \le
  D(|\alpha \setminus V(G)| + 1)$, then
  \cref{lem:isInfRigidOn-of-relative-count} on the resulting realization,
  whose body set is $V(G)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Rank polynomial from a rank lower bound]
  \label{lem:rank-polynomial-of-le-finrank}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.exists_rankPolynomial_of_le_finrank_linking}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:rows-polynomial-in-normals}
  The deficiency-aware sibling of \cref{lem:rows-polynomial-in-normals}: for
  a free-normal panel framework on $G$ at a seed $q_0$ with a rank lower
  bound $N \le \operatorname{rank} R(G,q_0)$ (no rigidity assumed), there is
  a nonzero rational polynomial $Q$, nonzero at $q_0$, such that $Q(q) \ne
  0$ forces the same rank bound $N \le \operatorname{rank} R(G,q)$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:rows-polynomial-in-normals}
  Where \cref{lem:rows-polynomial-in-normals} extracts $D(|V(G)|-1)$
  independent linking panel rows from a rigid seed, here the rank bound $N$
  extracts exactly $N$ such rows directly, by the same argument. The same
  Gram-determinant coordinatization and polynomial non-vanishing device as
  in \cref{lem:genericity-device}'s proof then produce $Q$; at a non-root
  $q$ the extracted rows stay independent, giving the rank bound at $q$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Case II placement: the $1$-extension normal, one row short; KT eq.~(6.12)]
  \label{lem:case-II-realization-placement}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.case_II_placement_eq612}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:panel-hinge-framework, lem:case-II, lem:genericity-device,
        lem:exists-independent-panel-extensor,
        lem:case-II-placement-new-rows, lem:case-II-placement-old-rows,
        lem:case-II-placement-old-rows-extract,
        lem:case-II-placement-block-independent}
  Let $G_v^{ab}$ be the splitting-off of $G$ at the reducible degree-2 body
  $v$ with neighbours $a$, $b$, realized at a seed $q$: rigid on
  $V(G_v^{ab})$, with transversal hinges. Placing $v$'s panel normal at the
  \emph{shear} $n_a + t\,n_b$ ($t \ne 0$, $n_a = q(a,\cdot)$, $n_b =
  q(b,\cdot)$) extends $q$ to a normal assignment $q_0$ for $G$ at which the
  rigidity rows of the free-normal panel framework contain a linearly
  independent family of size $D(|V(G)|-1) - 1$ --- one row short of the
  full target $D(|V(G)|-1)$; the missing row is the Case~III redundant-row
  content of \cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11} and
  \cref{lem:case-III-candidate-row}.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-II, lem:exists-independent-panel-extensor,
        lem:case-II-placement-new-rows, lem:case-II-placement-old-rows,
        lem:case-II-placement-old-rows-extract,
        lem:case-II-placement-block-independent}
  This is KT's degenerate eq.~(6.12) placement: take $q_0$ to agree with
  $q$ on $V(G) \setminus \{v\} = V(G_v^{ab})$ and assign $v$ the sheared
  normal $n_a + t\,n_b$; overriding only the fresh body $v$ leaves the
  inductive block untouched, so the rigidity of $G_v^{ab}$ transports
  unchanged to $q_0$. The shear identity makes $v$'s $b$-hinge
  \emph{reproduce} the $e_0 = ab$ hinge of the inductive realization (the
  $vb$-row equals the $e_0$-row of $R(G_v^{ab},q)$), while $t \ne 0$ keeps
  $v$'s $a$-hinge a nondegenerate line $L \subset \Pi_q(a)$. So the deleted
  edge $e_0$'s constraint is recovered by this reproduction, not by an
  $e_0$-free block of $G_v^{ab}$'s own rows (see the remark below). The
  independent family is the union of a \emph{new} block --- the $D-1$
  panel rows of $v$'s $b$-edge, independent through $v$'s screw column
  (\cref{lem:case-II-placement-new-rows}) --- and an \emph{old} block ---
  $D(|V(G)|-2)$ independent panel rows of the inductive realization
  (\cref{lem:case-II-placement-old-rows-extract}), transported onto $G$
  through the common subgraph $G - v$
  (\cref{lem:case-II-placement-old-rows}). The pin-a-body column split of
  KT's eq.~(6.16) (\cref{lem:case-II-placement-block-independent}) makes
  the union independent: the old rows vanish on $v$'s column, the new
  rows read it. The count is $(D-1) + D(|V(G)|-2) = D(|V(G)|-1) - 1$. See
  \cite[\S6.3, \S6.4.1]{katohTanigawa2011} (Lemma~6.8, eq.~(6.12)).
\end{proof}

\medskip\noindent\emph{Why the construction reproduces $e_0$ rather than
deleting it.} An $e_0$-free family of $D(|V(G)|-2)$ independent rows of
$G_v^{ab}$ does not exist: $G-v$ is missing both $e_0$ and $v$'s two edges,
so it is not itself rigid on $V(G)\setminus\{v\}$, and $e_0$'s row is
genuinely needed to reach that count in $G_v^{ab}$. The missing row
appears only once $v$'s two hinges are present, via the shear reproduction
above --- the shallower parallel of Case~I's block-triangular construction
(one re-inserted body, $+(D-1)$ rows, in place of a whole rigid block). An
earlier attempt to pin $v$ through a motion-space argument instead of this
row reproduction was found unsound (it assumes a motion of $G$ constant
off $v$ that the non-rigid $G-v$ does not force) and is abandoned; see the
git history for the discarded argument.

\begin{lemma}[A re-inserted body's transversal hinge gives $D-1$ independent panel rows]
  \label{lem:case-II-placement-new-rows}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.exists_independent_panelRow_of_edge,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.exists_independent_panelRow_subfamily_of_edge}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:panel-hinge-framework, def:hinge-row-block, def:rigidity-matrix, lem:rank-delete-vertex}
  Let $e = uv$ be a transversal hinge of the free-normal panel framework on
  $G$: distinct endpoints and a nonzero supporting extensor $C(p(e))$
  (achieved by choosing $v$'s normal off the support-extensor locus,
  \cref{lem:exists-independent-panel-extensor}). Then there is an index set
  $s$ of size $D-1$, each index using the edge $e$, whose panel rows form a
  linearly independent family lying in the single edge's panel-row span
  $\langle \mathrm{panelRow}(e,\cdot,\cdot)\rangle$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:hinge-row-block, lem:rank-delete-vertex}
  The transversal hinge's row block $r(p(e)) = (\operatorname{span}
  C(p(e)))^\perp$ has dimension $D-1$ (\cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex}); pick
  a basis of it. Its image under the relative-screw evaluation
  $S \mapsto S(u)-S(v)$ stays independent (the map is injective at
  distinct endpoints) and lies in the single edge's panel-row span.
  Extracting an independent subfamily of \emph{actual} panel rows at the
  span's dimension and re-indexing each by its $\Lambda^k$-pair gives the
  index set $s$. See \cite[\S6.3]{katohTanigawa2011}.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[The inductive rows transport through the common subgraph $G-v$]
  \label{lem:case-II-placement-old-rows}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.exists_independent_panelRow_transport}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:panel-hinge-framework, lem:case-II, lem:rows-polynomial-in-normals,
        lem:case-II-placement-old-rows-extract}
  Let $s_1$ index a linearly independent family of panel rows of the
  free-normal panel framework $(G_1, \mathrm{ends}_1, q_1)$, and let
  $f : s_2 \to s_1$ be injective with each selected row matching across
  the graph swap: the panel row of $(G_2,\mathrm{ends}_2,q_2)$ at $i$
  equals the panel row of $(G_1,\mathrm{ends}_1,q_1)$ at $f(i)$. Then the
  $s_2$-indexed family is again linearly independent, as panel rows of
  $(G_2,\mathrm{ends}_2,q_2)$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-II, lem:rows-polynomial-in-normals}
  Independence is inherited by a subfamily of an independent family along
  the injective reindex $f$, using the per-row match to identify the two
  sides. Applied here: the inductive realization's $D(|V(G)|-2)$
  independent rows of $G_v^{ab}$
  (\cref{lem:case-II-placement-old-rows-extract}) restrict to the rows
  indexed by edges of $G-v$ (dropping the $e_0$-row), and these survive
  unchanged into $G$ --- neither $G_v^{ab}$ nor $G$ is a subgraph of the
  other, but both contain the common subgraph $G-v$, and a panel row
  depends only on the endpoint selector and the normal assignment, not on
  the ambient graph, so the match is definitional once $q_0$ extends the
  inductive normals and the two endpoint selectors agree on $G-v$'s
  edges. See \cite[\S6.3]{katohTanigawa2011} (Lemma~6.8).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[A rigid realization carries a full-rank independent panel-row family]
  \label{lem:case-II-placement-old-rows-extract}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.exists_independent_panelRow_subfamily_of_rigidOn,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.finrank_infinitesimalMotions_of_isInfinitesimallyRigidOn_vertexSet}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:panel-hinge-framework, def:rigidity-matrix, def:rank-hypothesis,
        lem:rank-delete-vertex, lem:relative-screw-split}
  Let $F$ be a free-normal panel framework on $G_v^{ab}$, infinitesimally
  rigid on its own body set $V(G_v^{ab})$, with all hinges transversal.
  Then there is a linearly independent family of $D(|V(G_v^{ab})|-1)$
  \emph{actual} panel rows of $F$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:rank-delete-vertex, lem:relative-screw-split}
  Rigidity on $V(G_v^{ab})$ pins all residual freedom: the block pin
  $Z_{V(G_v^{ab})}(G_v^{ab},q)$ has dimension
  $D(|V_\alpha| - |V(G_v^{ab})|)$ (\cref{lem:relative-screw-split}), so by
  KT's pin-a-body Lemma~5.1 (\cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex})
  $\dim Z = D + D(|V_\alpha| - |V(G_v^{ab})|)$, whence
  $\operatorname{rank} R = D|V_\alpha| - \dim Z = D(|V(G_v^{ab})|-1)$.
  Since the hinges are transversal the panel rows span the whole
  rigidity-row space, which has exactly that dimension, so an independent
  subfamily of that many actual rows can be extracted and re-indexed by
  edge and $\Lambda^k$-pair. See \cite[\S6.3]{katohTanigawa2011}
  (Lemma~6.8).
\end{proof}

\medskip\noindent This is the genuine content standing between ``rigid on
a set'' and ``carries that many independent rows'': it is not discharged
by the transport \cref{lem:case-II-placement-old-rows} (which only
carries an \emph{already-witnessed} independent family across a graph
swap), nor by a bare spanning-forest count (which gives rows only up to
the forest's size, short of the full $D(|V(G_v^{ab})|-1)$ unless the
block is itself rigid).

\begin{lemma}[The new-edge and old blocks are jointly independent: the pin-a-body column split]
  \label{lem:case-II-placement-block-independent}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.linearIndependent_sum_pinned_block}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:panel-hinge-framework, lem:rank-delete-vertex}
  Let the \emph{new} block be a family of panel rows carried by the
  re-inserted body $v$'s incident hinges, independent as functionals of
  $v$'s screw column, and let the \emph{old} block be a family of panel
  rows of the common subgraph $G-v$ (so each vanishes when the screw
  assignment is pinned to $v$ alone), independent as rows. Then the union
  of the two families is linearly independent.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:rank-delete-vertex}
  Evaluate a vanishing combination of the union at a screw assignment
  pinned to $v$ alone (KT's pin-a-body Lemma~5.1,
  \cref{lem:rank-delete-vertex}): the old terms vanish (their edges avoid
  $v$), leaving a vanishing combination of the new block, which forces its
  coefficients to vanish by hypothesis; the residual is then a vanishing
  combination of the old block, forcing its coefficients by the old
  block's independence. See \cite[\S6.3]{katohTanigawa2011}.
\end{proof}

\medskip\noindent This is the panel-row form of the $1$-extension's exact
$+D$ rank lift that the Case~II accounting \cref{lem:case-II} pins; the
placement \cref{lem:case-II-realization-placement} supplies the two
blocks (giving the count $D(|V(G)|-1)$) and discharges the column-split
hypotheses by construction.
