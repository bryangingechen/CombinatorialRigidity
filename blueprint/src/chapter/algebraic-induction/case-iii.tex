\subsection{Case III deferred (the crux)}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-caseIII}

The crux of Case III is the missing $+1$ row of KT Claim~6.11 (the $D$-th
$ab$-row of $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ that lifts the stratum-1 brick
$D(|V|-1) - 1$ of \cref{lem:case-II-realization-placement} to full rank
$D(|V|-1)$). Claim~6.11's discharge factors, in dependency order, through three
combinatorial$\to$linear gaps; the leaf-most --- pure $M(\tilde G)$ matroid
theory, all inputs green --- is the matroid-base form of KT Lemma~4.3(ii) at
$k = 0$, built here.

\begin{lemma}[Matroid-base 4.3(ii) at $k = 0$; KT Lemma~4.3(ii), Claim~6.11 prerequisite]
  \label{lem:case-III-claim-6-11-base}
  \lean{Graph.splitOff_exists_base_inter_fiber_lt}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-dof, def:matroid-MG, thm:def-eq-corank, lem:forest-surgery-count, lem:splitoff-deficiency}
  Let $G$ be a $0$-dof-graph ($\operatorname{def}(\tilde G) = 0$) with a degree-2
  vertex $v$ (distinct neighbours $a, b$, incident edges exactly $e_a \ne e_b$,
  $e_0 \notin E(G)$ fresh). Then $M(\widetilde{G_v^{ab}})$ has a base $B'$ with
  \[
    \bigl|\widetilde{ab} \cap B'\bigr| \;<\; D - 1,
  \]
  i.e.\ $\widetilde{ab} \not\subseteq B'$: a redundant $\widetilde{ab}$-copy exists.
  This is KT's step~1 toward Claim~6.11 (the redundant $ab$-row of
  $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$); the remaining gaps --- the nested IH at the restricted
  realization and the $M(\tilde G)\leftrightarrow$row-dependence bridge --- stay
  deferred.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Reroute a balanced $D$-forest packing of a base of $M(\tilde G)$ across $v$
  (\cref{lem:forest-surgery-count}): this yields an $M(\widetilde{G_v^{ab}})$-%
  independent set $I'$ with $|I'| = |\text{base}| - D$ and $|\widetilde{ab} \cap I'|
  < D - 1$ (KT Lemma~4.1's two conclusions). At $k = 0$ splitting off keeps the
  graph $0$-dof (\cref{lem:splitoff-deficiency}, with
  $\operatorname{def} \ge 0$), so $\operatorname{rank} M(\widetilde{G_v^{ab}}) =
  D(|V \setminus \{v\}| - 1) = |\text{base}| - D = |I'|$ by the def\,$=$\,corank
  identity (\cref{thm:def-eq-corank}); an independent set of full rank is a base.
  That base $I'$ carries the fiber bound.
\end{proof}

The second factor of Claim~6.11 is KT's nested-induction step: the
vertex-removal $G_v := G - v = G_v^{ab} - ab$ is itself a \emph{minimal}
$k'$-dof-graph for $k' := \operatorname{def}(\tilde G_v)$, and that deficiency is
small ($k' \le D - 2$). The combinatorial half --- minimality and the deficiency
bound, both pure $M(\tilde G)$ matroid theory --- is green here; the
\emph{analytic} half it feeds (applying the geometric IH to $G_v$ at the
inductively-fixed seed, KT eq.\ (6.22)) is the deferred crux.

\begin{lemma}[$G - v$ is minimal $k'$-dof with $k' \le D - 2$; KT Lemma~4.3(ii)/nested IH, Claim~6.11 prerequisite]
  \label{lem:case-III-gap3-minimalKDof}
  \lean{Graph.splitOff_removeVertex_minimalKDof}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-dof, def:graph-operations, lem:subgraph-minimality, thm:def-eq-corank,
        lem:splitoff-deficiency, lem:case-III-claim-6-11-base}
  Let $G$ be a minimal $0$-dof-graph with a degree-2 vertex $v$ (distinct
  neighbours $a, b$, incident edges exactly $e_a \ne e_b$, $e_0 \notin E(G)$
  fresh). Then the vertex-removal $G_v := G - v$ is a minimal
  $k'$-dof-graph for $k' = \operatorname{def}(\tilde G_v)$, with
  \[
    0 \;\le\; k' \;\le\; D - 2.
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Minimality transports along $G_v \le G$ by \cref{lem:subgraph-minimality}: a
  subgraph of a minimal $k$-dof-graph is minimal at its own deficiency. For the
  bound, take the Gap-2 base $B'$ of $M(\widetilde{G_v^{ab}})$ with
  $h := |\widetilde{ab} \cap B'| < D - 1$
  (\cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11-base}). Its surviving part $B' \setminus
  \widetilde{ab}$ lands in $E(\tilde G_v)$ and is independent in $M(\tilde G_v) =
  M(\widetilde{G_v^{ab}}) \restriction E(\tilde G_v)$ (matroid restriction along
  $\tilde G_v \le \widetilde{G_v^{ab}}$), so $\operatorname{rank} M(\tilde G_v)
  \ge |B'| - h$. At $k = 0$ the splitting off is $0$-dof
  (\cref{lem:splitoff-deficiency}), so $|B'| = D(|V \setminus \{v\}| - 1)$; the
  def\,$=$\,corank identity (\cref{thm:def-eq-corank}, same vertex set $V(G)
  \setminus \{v\}$) then gives $\operatorname{def}(\tilde G_v) = D(|V \setminus
  \{v\}| - 1) - \operatorname{rank} M(\tilde G_v) \le h < D - 1$, i.e.\
  $k' \le D - 2$. The lower bound $0 \le k'$ is the nonnegativity of deficiency.
\end{proof}

The third factor is the \emph{analytic} kernel KT's footnote~6 supplies: the nested
induction fixes the realizing seed with algebraically-independent coordinates, so the
\emph{inductively-fixed} seed --- not merely \emph{some} generic seed --- attains the
maximal (matroid-predicted) rank of each subgraph. At the $0$-dof level this is KT
eq.~(6.18): the split-off $G_v^{ab}$ is $0$-dof, so its generic realization is rigid,
and the seed transfer below certifies the fixed seed is rigid for it too.

\begin{lemma}[Seed-rank bridge: rigidity transfers to every algebraically-independent seed; KT footnote 6, eqs.\ (6.18)/(6.22)]
  \label{lem:case-III-seed-rank-bridge}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.isInfinitesimallyRigidOn_ofNormals_of_algebraicIndependent}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:genericity-device, def:rank-hypothesis}
  Let $\mathrm{ofNormals}\,G\,\mathit{ends}\,q_0$ be a free-normal panel framework with a
  link-recording selector and transversal hinges. If it is infinitesimally rigid on
  $V(G)$ at \emph{some} seed $q_0$, then it is infinitesimally rigid on $V(G)$ at
  \emph{any} seed $q$ that is algebraically independent over $\mathbb{Q}$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  The rigid leg at $q_0$ carries a nonzero rank polynomial
  $Q : \mathrm{MvPolynomial}\,\sigma\,\mathbb{R}$ whose non-roots witness the full-size
  independent panel-row subfamily (the genericity device's coordinatization,
  \cref{lem:genericity-device}). $Q$'s coefficients are the structural change-of-basis
  constants of the complement isomorphism, hence rational
  ($Q.\mathrm{coeffs} \subseteq \mathrm{range}(\mathbb{Q} \to \mathbb{R})$). An
  algebraically-independent-over-$\mathbb{Q}$ seed $q$ lies off the zero locus of every
  nonzero rational polynomial (KT footnote~6), so $\mathrm{eval}\,q\,Q \ne 0$; the device
  consumer then reads infinitesimal rigidity at $q$ off that non-root.
\end{proof}

The seed-rank bridge above transfers \emph{full} rigidity (the $0$-dof case, eq.~(6.18)). The
nested IH step (eq.~(6.22)) needs the $\operatorname{def} > 0$ counterpart: at the same
inductively-fixed seed, the nested subgraph $G_v$ (minimal $k'$-dof, $k' = \operatorname{def}(\tilde
G_v)$) attains rank $D(|V_v|-1) - k'$, equivalently $\dim Z(G_v, q) = D + k'$. Pairing the
genericity-free \emph{lower} bound $D + \operatorname{def} \le \dim Z$ (the $\mathit{hub}$ input of
the Proposition-1.1 reconciliation, \cref{prop:rigidity-matrix-prop11}) with the matching
\emph{upper} bound below pins the equality. The upper bound is the brick green here.

\begin{lemma}[Eq.~(6.22) rank upper bound at every algebraically-independent seed; KT footnote 6, eq.\ (6.22)]
  \label{lem:case-III-seed-rank-upper}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.finrank_infinitesimalMotions_le_of_rankPolynomial_algebraicIndependent}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-III-seed-rank-bridge, thm:def-eq-corank}
  Let $\mathrm{ofNormals}\,G\,\mathit{ends}\,\cdot$ be the free-normal panel framework, and let
  $Q$ be a nonzero \emph{rational} polynomial (coefficients in the image of $\mathbb{Q} \to
  \mathbb{R}$) whose non-roots witness a linearly independent $\mathrm{panelRow}$-subfamily $s$
  whose edges link in $G$. Then at \emph{any} seed $q$ algebraically independent over $\mathbb{Q}$,
  \[
    \dim Z(G, q) \;\le\; D\,|\alpha| - |s|.
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  An algebraically-independent-over-$\mathbb{Q}$ seed is a non-root of the nonzero rational $Q$
  (KT footnote~6, as in \cref{lem:case-III-seed-rank-bridge}), so the witnessed subfamily $s$ is
  linearly independent at $q$ itself. Each panel row of $s$ lies in the rigidity rows (its edge
  links in $G$), so $|s| \le \dim \operatorname{span}(\text{rigidity rows}) = D\,|\alpha| - \dim
  Z(G, q)$ by rank--nullity (the rigidity-row span and the null space are dual-annihilator
  complements). Rearranging gives the bound.
\end{proof}

The upper bound above is the last \emph{leaf}; the two analytic halves now assemble. The nested
subgraph $G_v$ attains its predicted rank at the inductively-fixed seed by pinning the equality
between the genericity-free lower bound and the matroid-predicted upper bound.

\begin{lemma}[Eq.~(6.22) rank attainment at the inductively-fixed seed; KT footnote 6, eq.\ (6.22)]
  \label{lem:case-III-rank-attainment}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.rankHypothesis_ofNormals_of_rankPolynomial_algebraicIndependent}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-III-seed-rank-upper, lem:trivial-motions-rank-bound, thm:def-eq-corank}
  Let $\mathrm{ofNormals}\,G\,\mathit{ends}\,q$ be the free-normal panel framework on the
  spanning graph $G$ ($V(G) = \alpha$) with every hinge genuine ($C(e) \neq 0$), and fix the
  inductively-fixed seed $q$, algebraically independent over $\mathbb{Q}$. Suppose a nonzero
  \emph{rational} polynomial $Q$ witnesses a linearly independent $\mathrm{panelRow}$-subfamily $s$
  (edges linking in $G$) of the matroid-predicted full size $|s| \ge D(|V|-1) -
  \operatorname{def}(\tilde G)$. Then $G$ attains its predicted rank at $q$:
  \[
    \dim Z(G, q) \;=\; D + \operatorname{def}(\tilde G),
    \qquad\text{i.e.}\qquad F.\,\mathrm{RankHypothesis}\bigl(\operatorname{def}(\tilde G)\bigr).
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  The eq.~(6.22) upper bound (\cref{lem:case-III-seed-rank-upper}) gives $\dim Z(G, q) \le D|V| -
  |s|$ at $q$. Substituting the matroid-predicted size $|s| \ge D(|V|-1) - \operatorname{def}$
  (the matroid rank $\operatorname{rank} M(\tilde G) = D(|V|-1) - \operatorname{def}$ of the
  def\,$=$\,corank identity \cref{thm:def-eq-corank}) and the spanning split $D|V| = D(|V|-1) + D$
  collapses the upper bound to $\dim Z(G, q) \le D + \operatorname{def}$. The matching
  genericity-free lower bound $D + \operatorname{def} \le \dim Z(G, q)$ --- the $\mathit{hub}$ bound
  of \cref{lem:trivial-motions-rank-bound}, available since every hinge is genuine --- pins the
  equality. Deficiency nonnegativity rules out the degenerate over-count.
\end{proof}

The two rank attainments above (eq.~(6.18) for $G_v^{ab}$, eq.~(6.22) for $G_v = G_v^{ab} - ab$)
combine into KT's eq.~(6.23): the $D-1$ many $ab$-rows of $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ raise the rank of
$R(G_v, q)$ by only $k' \le D - 2 < D - 1$ (the smaller graph $G_v$ already realizes all but $k'$ of
the full rank), so one of those $ab$-rows is a linear combination of the others together with the
$R(G_v, q)$ rows. The pure linear-algebra core of that pigeonhole
(\cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11-redundant-row}) is instantiated at KT's two specific graphs by the
\emph{row-set identity} $\operatorname{span}(R(G_v^{ab})\text{-rows}) =
\operatorname{span}(R(G_v)\text{-rows}) + \operatorname{span}(ab\text{-block})$: since
$G_v = G_v^{ab} - ab$ and both frameworks share the same seed $q$ and selector $\mathrm{ends}$ (so
the same supporting extensors), the only rigidity rows of $R(G_v^{ab})$ not already in $R(G_v)$ are
the $ab$-rows, which span exactly the $ab$-block. Feeding the two rank attainments through it
discharges the corank gap and produces KT's redundant $ab$-row
(\cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11}).

\begin{lemma}[Redundant $ab$-row from a small corank over the $ab$-block; KT Claim 6.11, eq.\ (6.23)]
  \label{lem:case-III-claim-6-11-redundant-row}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.exists_redundant_panelRow_of_edge_of_finrank_lt}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-III-seed-rank-bridge, lem:case-III-rank-attainment,
        lem:case-III-gap3-minimalKDof, lem:case-II-placement-new-rows}
  Let $F$ be a body-hinge framework with a transversal hinge $e$ (distinct endpoints, nonzero
  supporting extensor $C(e) \ne 0$), and let $W$ be any subspace of the dual row space (intended:
  $W = \operatorname{span}(R(G_v, q)\text{-rows})$ for the smaller graph $G_v = G_v^{ab} - ab$, with
  $e = ab$). If the $e$-block --- the per-edge panel-row span
  $\operatorname{span}\{\mathrm{panelRow}\,(e, \cdot, \cdot)\}$, of dimension $D - 1$ --- raises the
  finrank of $W$ by strictly fewer than $D - 1$,
  \[
    \dim\bigl(W + \operatorname{span}(e\text{-block})\bigr) \;<\; \dim W + (D - 1),
  \]
  then there is a $\mathrm{Fin}(D-1)$-indexed independent family $r$ of the $e$-block (its span is
  the whole block) with one redundant member: $r\,i_0 \in W + \operatorname{span}(r \restriction
  \{j \ne i_0\})$. This is KT's eq.~(6.23): with the corank gap fed by eq.~(6.18)
  (\cref{lem:case-III-seed-rank-bridge}, full rank of $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ at the $0$-dof $G_v^{ab}$) and
  eq.~(6.22) (\cref{lem:case-III-rank-attainment}, with $k' = \operatorname{def}(\tilde G_v) \le
  D - 2$ from \cref{lem:case-III-gap3-minimalKDof}), one of the $ab$-rows is redundant, so dropping
  it does not lower the rank.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  The $e$-block is the $\mathrm{hingeRow}$ image of the $(D-1)$-dimensional hinge-row block
  $r(p(e))$, so it is $(D-1)$-dimensional and is spanned by the $D - 1$ independent panel rows the
  transversal hinge contributes (\cref{lem:case-II-placement-new-rows}). The finrank hypothesis is
  exactly the abstract pigeonhole's input at this $\mathrm{Fin}(D-1)$-indexed family: a family
  raising $\dim W$ by fewer than its cardinality has a member lying in $W$ plus the span of the rest.
  That member is the redundant $ab$-row.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[KT Claim 6.11, eq.\ (6.23): the deleted $ab$-edge has a redundant row]
  \label{lem:case-III-claim-6-11}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.exists_redundant_panelRow_ab_of_finrank_eq}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-III-claim-6-11-redundant-row, lem:case-III-seed-rank-bridge,
        lem:case-III-rank-attainment, lem:case-III-gap3-minimalKDof}
  Let $G_{ab} = G_v^{ab}$ be the split-off graph and $G_v = G_v^{ab} - ab$ its single-edge deletion,
  realized as $\mathrm{ofNormals}\,(\cdot)\,\mathrm{ends}\,q$ at the same inductively-fixed seed $q$
  and selector $\mathrm{ends}$, with $e_0 = ab$ the deleted transversal hinge ($C(e_0) \ne 0$). Given
  the two rank attainments --- eq.~(6.18) $\dim \operatorname{span}(R(G_{ab}, q)\text{-rows}) =
  D(m-1)$ (the $0$-dof full rank, $m = |V(G_{ab})| = |V(G_v)|$) and eq.~(6.22)
  $\dim \operatorname{span}(R(G_v, q)\text{-rows}) = D(m-1) - k'$ with $k' \le D - 2$ --- one of the
  $D-1$ many $ab$-rows of $R(G_{ab}, q)$ is redundant modulo $\operatorname{span}(R(G_v, q)\text{-rows})$
  and the other $ab$-rows: KT's eq.~(6.23). This is the $+1$ that the deferred candidate-completion
  assembly converts into the missing full-rank row.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  The two frameworks differ only by the $ab$-edge $e_0$ (their links agree off $e_0$, and they share
  the same supporting extensors), so the row-set identity $\operatorname{span}(R(G_{ab})\text{-rows})
  = \operatorname{span}(R(G_v)\text{-rows}) + \operatorname{span}(ab\text{-block})$ holds. Eq.~(6.18)
  then reads $\dim\bigl(W + \operatorname{span}(ab\text{-block})\bigr) = D(m-1)$ with
  $W = \operatorname{span}(R(G_v)\text{-rows})$, while eq.~(6.22) gives $\dim W = D(m-1) - k'$; so the
  $ab$-block raises $\dim W$ by only $k' \le D - 2 < D - 1$, the corank gap the redundant-row
  pigeonhole (\cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11-redundant-row}) consumes to yield the redundant
  $ab$-row.
\end{proof}

\subsubsection{The candidate-completion: converting the redundant row into the
missing \texorpdfstring{$+1$}{+1} (KT eqs.\ (6.24)--(6.29))}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-candidate-completion}

Claim~6.11 (\cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11}) gives a redundant $ab$-row of
$R(G_v^{ab}, q)$; the candidate-completion turns it into the full-rank $D$-th row
of the stratum-1 brick. KT's mechanism (eqs.~(6.24)--(6.29)) is a single
fundamental row operation: the redundant row's linear combination, transported
from $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ up to $R(G, p_1)$ across the seam
$R(G, p_1; E \setminus \{vb\}, V \setminus \{v\}) = R(G_v^{ab}, q)$, produces a row
$w$ of $R(G, p_1)$ whose $V \setminus \{v\}$ part vanishes (eqs.~(6.27)--(6.28)).
The eqs.~(6.24)--(6.25) decomposition of the redundant row
(\cref{lem:case-III-redundant-decomposition}, green), its eq.~(6.43) $a$-column-block vanishing
(\cref{lem:case-III-acolumn-zero}, green), the per-edge seam identity
(\cref{lem:case-III-seam}, green), and the pure-$v$-column characterization of such a row
(\cref{lem:case-III-vanish-off-column}, green) are all in place; the eq.~(6.27) transport of the
combination across the seam --- assembling these into the row $w$ and reconciling the
$(vb)j \leftrightarrow (ab)j$ correspondence via the eq.~(6.43) $a$-block-vanishing (see the caveat
in \cref{lem:case-III-candidate-row}'s proof) --- remains the open crux.

\begin{lemma}[A rigidity row vanishing off $v$'s column is a pure $v$-column row; KT eq.\ (6.28)]
  \label{lem:case-III-vanish-off-column}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.dualMap_eq_comp_single_proj_of_vanish_off}
  \leanok
  Let $f$ be a functional on the screw assignments $\alpha \to \mathrm{ScrewSpace}\,k$
  that vanishes on every assignment supported off a fixed body $v$ (i.e.\
  $S v = 0 \Rightarrow f S = 0$). Then $f$ factors through body $v$'s screw column:
  $f = (f \circ \mathrm{single}_v) \circ \mathrm{proj}_v$. This is KT eq.~(6.28)'s
  formal content: the candidate-completion's row operation (eqs.~(6.24)--(6.27))
  reduces the $(vb)i^*$-row of $R(G, p_1)$ to a row whose $V \setminus \{v\}$ part is
  all zero, and such a row is determined by its $v$-column block --- written
  $S = \mathrm{single}_v(S v) + (S - \mathrm{single}_v(S v))$, the second summand is
  supported off $v$, so $f$ kills it.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Split $S = \mathrm{single}_v(S v) + (S - \mathrm{single}_v(S v))$. The second
  summand reads $0$ at $v$, so $f$ kills it by hypothesis, leaving
  $f S = f(\mathrm{single}_v(S v)) = (f \circ \mathrm{single}_v)(S v)$, which is
  exactly $((f \circ \mathrm{single}_v) \circ \mathrm{proj}_v)\,S$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[The seam identity: an edge's row depends only on the seed at its endpoints; KT eq.\ (6.26)]
  \label{lem:case-III-seam}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.ofNormals_panelRow_eq_of_ends_seed_eq}
  \leanok
  Two $\mathrm{ofNormals}$ realizations at seeds $q_0$, $q$ that \emph{agree at the
  two endpoints} of an edge $e$ --- $q_0(\mathrm{ends}\,e)_1 = q(\mathrm{ends}\,e)_1$
  and $q_0(\mathrm{ends}\,e)_2 = q(\mathrm{ends}\,e)_2$ as panel normals --- produce
  identical $e$-rows of their rigidity matrices: for every basis pair $(t_1, t_2)$,
  \[ R(G, q_0; e, t_1, t_2) = R(G_v^{ab}, q; e, t_1, t_2), \]
  regardless of the two underlying graphs. This is KT's seam identity
  $R(G, p_1; E \setminus \{vb\}, V \setminus \{v\}) = R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ in per-row form:
  a panel row $\mathrm{hingeRow}\,(\mathrm{ends}\,e)_1\,(\mathrm{ends}\,e)_2\,
  (\mathrm{annihRow}\,C(p(e))\,t_1\,t_2)$ reads only the endpoint selector, the index,
  and the supporting extensor $C(p(e)) = \mathrm{panelSupportExtensor}\,(\mathrm{normal}\,
  (\mathrm{ends}\,e)_1)\,(\mathrm{normal}\,(\mathrm{ends}\,e)_2)$, which depends on the
  seed only at the two endpoints (the underlying graph never enters a panel row).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Both rows unfold to $\mathrm{hingeRow}\,(\mathrm{ends}\,e)_1\,(\mathrm{ends}\,e)_2\,
  (\mathrm{annihRow}\,C\,t_1\,t_2)$ with $C = \mathrm{panelSupportExtensor}$ of the two
  endpoint normals; the hypotheses $q_0 = q$ at $(\mathrm{ends}\,e)_1$, $(\mathrm{ends}
  \,e)_2$ make the two normals equal, hence the extensors and the rows.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[The redundant $ab$-row as an explicit vanishing combination; KT eqs.\ (6.24)--(6.25)]
  \label{lem:case-III-redundant-decomposition}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.exists_redundant_panelRow_ab_decomposition}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-III-claim-6-11}
  The functional-identity form of Claim~6.11 (\cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11}, eq.~(6.23)) that the
  candidate-completion row operation consumes. Where \cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11} delivers the
  \emph{membership} $r\,i^* \in \operatorname{span}(R(G_v, q)\text{-rows}) \sqcup
  \operatorname{span}(r \mathbin{''} \{j \neq i^*\})$ --- the $D - 1$ independent $ab$-rows $r$
  spanning the $ab$-block, one of them ($i^*$) redundant modulo the $G_v$-rows and the rest --- this
  lemma unwinds it (submodule $\sqcup$-membership) into KT's eq.~(6.24): the redundant $ab$-row
  $r\,i^*$ \emph{equals} a $G_v$-row element $w_{\mathrm{Gv}} \in \operatorname{span}(R(G_v,
  q)\text{-rows})$ plus a combination $w_{\mathrm{Other}} \in \operatorname{span}(r \mathbin{''} \{j
  \neq i^*\})$ of the other $ab$-rows. Rearranged this is the vanishing combination
  $r\,i^* - w_{\mathrm{Other}} - w_{\mathrm{Gv}} = 0$ of eq.~(6.24) with the $(ab)i^*$-coefficient
  $\lambda_{(ab)i^*} = 1$ (eq.~(6.25)): $r\,i^*$ carries coefficient $1$, $w_{\mathrm{Other}}$ the
  other $\lambda_{(ab)j}$ ($j \neq i^*$), $w_{\mathrm{Gv}}$ the $\lambda_{ej}$ over $E_v$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Immediate from \cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11} by splitting the $\sqcup$-membership of the redundant
  row $r\,i^*$ into its $\operatorname{span}(R(G_v, q)\text{-rows})$ summand $w_{\mathrm{Gv}}$ and its
  $\operatorname{span}(r \mathbin{''} \{j \neq i^*\})$ summand $w_{\mathrm{Other}}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[The $a$-column block of the eq.\ (6.24) vanishing combination is $0$; KT eq.\ (6.43)]
  \label{lem:case-III-acolumn-zero}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.exists_redundant_panelRow_ab_decomposition_acolumn_zero}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-III-redundant-decomposition}
  The eqs.~(6.24)--(6.25) decomposition (\cref{lem:case-III-redundant-decomposition}) writes the
  redundant $ab$-row as $r\,i^* = w_{\mathrm{Gv}} + w_{\mathrm{Other}}$, i.e.\ the vanishing
  combination $g := w_{\mathrm{Gv}} + w_{\mathrm{Other}} - r\,i^* = 0$ --- KT's eq.~(6.24)
  $\sum_j \lambda_{(ab)j} R(G_v^{ab}, q; (ab)j) + \sum_{e \in E_v, j} \lambda_{ej} R(G_v^{ab}, q;
  e_j) = 0$ as a functional on the screw assignments $\alpha \to \mathrm{ScrewSpace}\,k$. KT
  eq.~(6.43) is its restriction to any single body $a$'s screw column: precomposing $g$ with the
  column injection $\mathrm{single}_a \colon \mathrm{ScrewSpace}\,k \to (\alpha \to
  \mathrm{ScrewSpace}\,k)$ (a screw on $a$, $0$ elsewhere) is again $0$,
  \[ g \circ \mathrm{single}_a = 0, \qquad\text{i.e.}\qquad
     \sum_{e \in E_v \cup \{ab\},\, j} \lambda_{ej}\, R(G_v^{ab}, q; e_j, a) = 0. \]
  This is the residual that the candidate-completion transport
  (\cref{lem:case-III-candidate-row}) must kill to certify that the transported row $w$'s
  $V \setminus \{v\}$ part vanishes: over $V \setminus \{v\}$ the $(vb)j$-row of $R(G, p_1)$ and the
  $(ab)j$-row of $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ differ by exactly the $a$-column block $r_j(\cdot\,a)$, so $w$'s
  $V \setminus \{v\}$ part is the eq.~(6.24) sum ($= g(S) = 0$) minus that $a$-block.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Since $r\,i^* = w_{\mathrm{Gv}} + w_{\mathrm{Other}}$
  (\cref{lem:case-III-redundant-decomposition}), the combination $g = w_{\mathrm{Gv}} +
  w_{\mathrm{Other}} - r\,i^*$ is the zero functional; precomposing it with $\mathrm{single}_a$ is
  therefore $0$ for every body $a$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[The candidate-completion row operation: the missing $+1$ row $w$; KT eqs.\ (6.24)--(6.28)]
  \label{lem:case-III-candidate-row}
  \uses{lem:case-III-claim-6-11, lem:case-III-redundant-decomposition,
        lem:case-III-acolumn-zero, lem:case-III-vanish-off-column, lem:case-III-seam,
        lem:case-II-realization-placement}
  Realize $G$ at the degenerate eq.~(6.12) placement $p_1$ with $p_1(vb) = q(ab)$
  (\cref{lem:case-II-realization-placement}). The redundant-$ab$-row of $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$,
  decomposed by \cref{lem:case-III-redundant-decomposition} (eqs.~(6.24)--(6.25)) into a $G_v$-row
  part and an other-$ab$-row part with $\lambda_{(ab)i^*} = 1$, is transported up to $R(G, p_1)$
  by the eq.~(6.26) row correspondence: the $(ab)j$-rows of $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ map to the $(vb)j$-rows
  of $R(G, p_1)$ (KT eq.~(6.16)) and the $E_v$-rows correspond directly. The resulting row
  $w = \sum_{1 \le j \le 5} \lambda_{(ab)j} R(G, p_1; (vb)j) + \sum_{e \in E_v, j}
  \lambda_{e_j} R(G, p_1; e_j)$ (eq.~(6.27)) lies in $\operatorname{span}(R(G, p_1)\text{-rows})$ and
  has its $V \setminus \{v\}$ part all zero. By \cref{lem:case-III-vanish-off-column}, $w$ is the pure
  $v$-column row $w = \bigl(\sum_j \lambda_{(ab)j}\, r_j(q(ab)),\ 0\bigr)$ of
  eq.~(6.28) (using $p_1(vb) = q(ab)$ and $R(G, p_1; E_v, v) = 0$). It is the new
  $(vb)i^*$-row that, joining the $va$-block, lifts $v$'s column contribution from
  $D - 1$ to $D$: the matrix $R(G, p_1)$ becomes block-triangular as in eq.~(6.29),
  with bottom-right $R(G_v^{ab} \setminus (ab)i^*, q)$ of rank $D(|V \setminus \{v\}| - 1)$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \uses{lem:case-III-claim-6-11, lem:case-III-redundant-decomposition,
        lem:case-III-acolumn-zero, lem:case-III-vanish-off-column, lem:case-III-seam}
  \emph{The open crux, deferred within Phase~22.} The eqs.~(6.24)--(6.25) decomposition
  (\cref{lem:case-III-redundant-decomposition}), its eq.~(6.43) $a$-column-block vanishing
  (\cref{lem:case-III-acolumn-zero}), and the per-edge seam identity (\cref{lem:case-III-seam}) are
  green. What stays open is the eq.~(6.27) transport of the combination from $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ to
  $R(G, p_1)$ and the proof that its $V \setminus \{v\}$ part vanishes. \textbf{Structural caveat
  (Phase 22e finding).} The vanishing is \emph{not} immediate from the per-edge seam alone: the
  $E_v$-rows transport as literal functional equalities (their endpoints avoid $v$, so $q_0 = q$
  there, \cref{lem:case-III-seam}), but the $(vb)j$-row of $R(G, p_1)$ ($hingeRow\ v\ b\ r$) and the
  $(ab)j$-row of $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ ($hingeRow\ a\ b\ r$), restricted to $V \setminus \{v\}$, differ by
  the $a$-column block $r_j(\cdot\,a)$ (the $(vb)$-hinge is $0$ in column $a$, the $(ab)$-hinge is
  $r_j$ there). The transported combination's $V \setminus \{v\}$ part is therefore the eq.~(6.24)
  sum (which is $0$) \emph{minus} $\sum_j \lambda_{(ab)j} r_j(\cdot\,a)$, the $a$-column block of
  eq.~(6.24), killed by KT eq.~(6.43) (\cref{lem:case-III-acolumn-zero}, green) --- so the full
  $V \setminus \{v\}$ part is $0$ after all. The remaining build is to read off the $v$-column part
  from $R(G, p_1; E_v, v) = 0$ and $R(G, p_1; (vb)j, v) = r_j(p_1(vb)) = r_j(q(ab))$, and apply
  \cref{lem:case-III-vanish-off-column} to certify $w$ is a pure $v$-column row.
\end{proof}
