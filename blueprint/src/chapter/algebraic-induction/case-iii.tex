\subsection{Case III deferred (the crux)}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-caseIII}

The crux of Case III is the missing $+1$ row of KT Claim~6.11 (the $D$-th
$ab$-row of $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ that lifts the stratum-1 brick
$D(|V|-1) - 1$ of \cref{lem:case-II-realization-placement} to full rank
$D(|V|-1)$). Claim~6.11's discharge factors, in dependency order, through three
combinatorial$\to$linear gaps; the leaf-most --- pure $M(\tilde G)$ matroid
theory, all inputs green --- is the matroid-base form of KT Lemma~4.3(ii) at
$k = 0$, built here.

\begin{lemma}[Matroid-base 4.3(ii) at $k = 0$; KT Lemma~4.3(ii), Claim~6.11 prerequisite]
  \label{lem:case-III-claim-6-11-base}
  \lean{Graph.splitOff_exists_base_inter_fiber_lt}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-dof, def:matroid-MG, thm:def-eq-corank, lem:forest-surgery-count, lem:splitoff-deficiency}
  Let $G$ be a $0$-dof-graph ($\operatorname{def}(\tilde G) = 0$) with a degree-2
  vertex $v$ (distinct neighbours $a, b$, incident edges exactly $e_a \ne e_b$,
  $e_0 \notin E(G)$ fresh). Then $M(\widetilde{G_v^{ab}})$ has a base $B'$ with
  \[
    \bigl|\widetilde{ab} \cap B'\bigr| \;<\; D - 1,
  \]
  i.e.\ $\widetilde{ab} \not\subseteq B'$: a redundant $\widetilde{ab}$-copy exists.
  This is KT's step~1 toward Claim~6.11 (the redundant $ab$-row of
  $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$); the remaining gaps --- the nested IH at the restricted
  realization and the $M(\tilde G)\leftrightarrow$row-dependence bridge --- stay
  deferred.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Reroute a balanced $D$-forest packing of a base of $M(\tilde G)$ across $v$
  (\cref{lem:forest-surgery-count}): this yields an $M(\widetilde{G_v^{ab}})$-%
  independent set $I'$ with $|I'| = |\text{base}| - D$ and $|\widetilde{ab} \cap I'|
  < D - 1$ (KT Lemma~4.1's two conclusions). At $k = 0$ splitting off keeps the
  graph $0$-dof (\cref{lem:splitoff-deficiency}, with
  $\operatorname{def} \ge 0$), so $\operatorname{rank} M(\widetilde{G_v^{ab}}) =
  D(|V \setminus \{v\}| - 1) = |\text{base}| - D = |I'|$ by the def\,$=$\,corank
  identity (\cref{thm:def-eq-corank}); an independent set of full rank is a base.
  That base $I'$ carries the fiber bound.
\end{proof}

The second factor of Claim~6.11 is KT's nested-induction step: the
vertex-removal $G_v := G - v = G_v^{ab} - ab$ is itself a \emph{minimal}
$k'$-dof-graph for $k' := \operatorname{def}(\tilde G_v)$, and that deficiency is
small ($k' \le D - 2$). The combinatorial half --- minimality and the deficiency
bound, both pure $M(\tilde G)$ matroid theory --- is green here; the
\emph{analytic} half it feeds (applying the geometric IH to $G_v$ at the
inductively-fixed seed, KT eq.\ (6.22)) is the deferred crux.

\begin{lemma}[$G - v$ is minimal $k'$-dof with $k' \le D - 2$; KT Lemma~4.3(ii)/nested IH, Claim~6.11 prerequisite]
  \label{lem:case-III-gap3-minimalKDof}
  \lean{Graph.splitOff_removeVertex_minimalKDof}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:k-dof, def:graph-operations, lem:subgraph-minimality, thm:def-eq-corank,
        lem:splitoff-deficiency, lem:case-III-claim-6-11-base}
  Let $G$ be a minimal $0$-dof-graph with a degree-2 vertex $v$ (distinct
  neighbours $a, b$, incident edges exactly $e_a \ne e_b$, $e_0 \notin E(G)$
  fresh). Then the vertex-removal $G_v := G - v$ is a minimal
  $k'$-dof-graph for $k' = \operatorname{def}(\tilde G_v)$, with
  \[
    0 \;\le\; k' \;\le\; D - 2.
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Minimality transports along $G_v \le G$ by \cref{lem:subgraph-minimality}: a
  subgraph of a minimal $k$-dof-graph is minimal at its own deficiency. For the
  bound, take the Gap-2 base $B'$ of $M(\widetilde{G_v^{ab}})$ with
  $h := |\widetilde{ab} \cap B'| < D - 1$
  (\cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11-base}). Its surviving part $B' \setminus
  \widetilde{ab}$ lands in $E(\tilde G_v)$ and is independent in $M(\tilde G_v) =
  M(\widetilde{G_v^{ab}}) \restriction E(\tilde G_v)$ (matroid restriction along
  $\tilde G_v \le \widetilde{G_v^{ab}}$), so $\operatorname{rank} M(\tilde G_v)
  \ge |B'| - h$. At $k = 0$ the splitting off is $0$-dof
  (\cref{lem:splitoff-deficiency}), so $|B'| = D(|V \setminus \{v\}| - 1)$; the
  def\,$=$\,corank identity (\cref{thm:def-eq-corank}, same vertex set $V(G)
  \setminus \{v\}$) then gives $\operatorname{def}(\tilde G_v) = D(|V \setminus
  \{v\}| - 1) - \operatorname{rank} M(\tilde G_v) \le h < D - 1$, i.e.\
  $k' \le D - 2$. The lower bound $0 \le k'$ is the nonnegativity of deficiency.
\end{proof}

The third factor is the \emph{analytic} kernel KT's footnote~6 supplies: the nested
induction fixes the realizing seed with algebraically-independent coordinates, so the
\emph{inductively-fixed} seed --- not merely \emph{some} generic seed --- attains the
maximal (matroid-predicted) rank of each subgraph. At the $0$-dof level this is KT
eq.~(6.18): the split-off $G_v^{ab}$ is $0$-dof, so its generic realization is rigid,
and the seed transfer below certifies the fixed seed is rigid for it too.

\begin{lemma}[Seed-rank bridge: rigidity transfers to every algebraically-independent seed; KT footnote 6, eqs.\ (6.18)/(6.22)]
  \label{lem:case-III-seed-rank-bridge}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.isInfinitesimallyRigidOn_ofNormals_of_algebraicIndependent}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:genericity-device, def:rank-hypothesis}
  Let $\mathrm{ofNormals}\,G\,\mathit{ends}\,q_0$ be a free-normal panel framework with a
  link-recording selector and transversal hinges. If it is infinitesimally rigid on
  $V(G)$ at \emph{some} seed $q_0$, then it is infinitesimally rigid on $V(G)$ at
  \emph{any} seed $q$ that is algebraically independent over $\mathbb{Q}$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  The rigid leg at $q_0$ carries a nonzero rank polynomial
  $Q : \mathrm{MvPolynomial}\,\sigma\,\mathbb{R}$ whose non-roots witness the full-size
  independent panel-row subfamily (the genericity device's coordinatization,
  \cref{lem:genericity-device}). $Q$'s coefficients are the structural change-of-basis
  constants of the complement isomorphism, hence rational
  ($Q.\mathrm{coeffs} \subseteq \mathrm{range}(\mathbb{Q} \to \mathbb{R})$). An
  algebraically-independent-over-$\mathbb{Q}$ seed $q$ lies off the zero locus of every
  nonzero rational polynomial (KT footnote~6), so $\mathrm{eval}\,q\,Q \ne 0$; the device
  consumer then reads infinitesimal rigidity at $q$ off that non-root.
\end{proof}

The seed-rank bridge above transfers \emph{full} rigidity (the $0$-dof case, eq.~(6.18)). The
nested IH step (eq.~(6.22)) needs the $\operatorname{def} > 0$ counterpart: at the same
inductively-fixed seed, the nested subgraph $G_v$ (minimal $k'$-dof, $k' = \operatorname{def}(\tilde
G_v)$) attains rank $D(|V_v|-1) - k'$, equivalently $\dim Z(G_v, q) = D + k'$. Pairing the
genericity-free \emph{lower} bound $D + \operatorname{def} \le \dim Z$ (the $\mathit{hub}$ input of
the Proposition-1.1 reconciliation, \cref{prop:rigidity-matrix-prop11}) with the matching
\emph{upper} bound below pins the equality. The upper bound is the brick green here.

\begin{lemma}[Eq.~(6.22) rank upper bound at every algebraically-independent seed; KT footnote 6, eq.\ (6.22)]
  \label{lem:case-III-seed-rank-upper}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.finrank_infinitesimalMotions_le_of_rankPolynomial_algebraicIndependent}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-III-seed-rank-bridge, thm:def-eq-corank}
  Let $\mathrm{ofNormals}\,G\,\mathit{ends}\,\cdot$ be the free-normal panel framework, and let
  $Q$ be a nonzero \emph{rational} polynomial (coefficients in the image of $\mathbb{Q} \to
  \mathbb{R}$) whose non-roots witness a linearly independent $\mathrm{panelRow}$-subfamily $s$
  whose edges link in $G$. Then at \emph{any} seed $q$ algebraically independent over $\mathbb{Q}$,
  \[
    \dim Z(G, q) \;\le\; D\,|\alpha| - |s|.
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  An algebraically-independent-over-$\mathbb{Q}$ seed is a non-root of the nonzero rational $Q$
  (KT footnote~6, as in \cref{lem:case-III-seed-rank-bridge}), so the witnessed subfamily $s$ is
  linearly independent at $q$ itself. Each panel row of $s$ lies in the rigidity rows (its edge
  links in $G$), so $|s| \le \dim \operatorname{span}(\text{rigidity rows}) = D\,|\alpha| - \dim
  Z(G, q)$ by rank--nullity (the rigidity-row span and the null space are dual-annihilator
  complements). Rearranging gives the bound.
\end{proof}

The upper bound above is the last \emph{leaf}; the two analytic halves now assemble. The nested
subgraph $G_v$ attains its predicted rank at the inductively-fixed seed by pinning the equality
between the genericity-free lower bound and the matroid-predicted upper bound.

\begin{lemma}[Eq.~(6.22) rank attainment at the inductively-fixed seed; KT footnote 6, eq.\ (6.22)]
  \label{lem:case-III-rank-attainment}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.PanelHingeFramework.rankHypothesis_ofNormals_of_rankPolynomial_algebraicIndependent}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-III-seed-rank-upper, lem:trivial-motions-rank-bound, thm:def-eq-corank}
  Let $\mathrm{ofNormals}\,G\,\mathit{ends}\,q$ be the free-normal panel framework on the
  spanning graph $G$ ($V(G) = \alpha$) with every hinge genuine ($C(e) \neq 0$), and fix the
  inductively-fixed seed $q$, algebraically independent over $\mathbb{Q}$. Suppose a nonzero
  \emph{rational} polynomial $Q$ witnesses a linearly independent $\mathrm{panelRow}$-subfamily $s$
  (edges linking in $G$) of the matroid-predicted full size $|s| \ge D(|V|-1) -
  \operatorname{def}(\tilde G)$. Then $G$ attains its predicted rank at $q$:
  \[
    \dim Z(G, q) \;=\; D + \operatorname{def}(\tilde G),
    \qquad\text{i.e.}\qquad F.\,\mathrm{RankHypothesis}\bigl(\operatorname{def}(\tilde G)\bigr).
  \]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  The eq.~(6.22) upper bound (\cref{lem:case-III-seed-rank-upper}) gives $\dim Z(G, q) \le D|V| -
  |s|$ at $q$. Substituting the matroid-predicted size $|s| \ge D(|V|-1) - \operatorname{def}$
  (the matroid rank $\operatorname{rank} M(\tilde G) = D(|V|-1) - \operatorname{def}$ of the
  def\,$=$\,corank identity \cref{thm:def-eq-corank}) and the spanning split $D|V| = D(|V|-1) + D$
  collapses the upper bound to $\dim Z(G, q) \le D + \operatorname{def}$. The matching
  genericity-free lower bound $D + \operatorname{def} \le \dim Z(G, q)$ --- the $\mathit{hub}$ bound
  of \cref{lem:trivial-motions-rank-bound}, available since every hinge is genuine --- pins the
  equality. Deficiency nonnegativity rules out the degenerate over-count.
\end{proof}

The two rank attainments above (eq.~(6.18) for $G_v^{ab}$, eq.~(6.22) for $G_v = G_v^{ab} - ab$)
combine into KT's eq.~(6.23): the $D-1$ many $ab$-rows of $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ raise the rank of
$R(G_v, q)$ by only $k' \le D - 2 < D - 1$ (the smaller graph $G_v$ already realizes all but $k'$ of
the full rank), so one of those $ab$-rows is a linear combination of the others together with the
$R(G_v, q)$ rows. The pure linear-algebra core of that pigeonhole
(\cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11-redundant-row}) is instantiated at KT's two specific graphs by the
\emph{row-set identity} $\operatorname{span}(R(G_v^{ab})\text{-rows}) =
\operatorname{span}(R(G_v)\text{-rows}) + \operatorname{span}(ab\text{-block})$: since
$G_v = G_v^{ab} - ab$ and both frameworks share the same seed $q$ and selector $\mathrm{ends}$ (so
the same supporting extensors), the only rigidity rows of $R(G_v^{ab})$ not already in $R(G_v)$ are
the $ab$-rows, which span exactly the $ab$-block. Feeding the two rank attainments through it
discharges the corank gap and produces KT's redundant $ab$-row
(\cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11}).

\begin{lemma}[Redundant $ab$-row from a small corank over the $ab$-block; KT Claim 6.11, eq.\ (6.23)]
  \label{lem:case-III-claim-6-11-redundant-row}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.exists_redundant_panelRow_of_edge_of_finrank_lt}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-III-seed-rank-bridge, lem:case-III-rank-attainment,
        lem:case-III-gap3-minimalKDof, lem:case-II-placement-new-rows}
  Let $F$ be a body-hinge framework with a transversal hinge $e$ (distinct endpoints, nonzero
  supporting extensor $C(e) \ne 0$), and let $W$ be any subspace of the dual row space (intended:
  $W = \operatorname{span}(R(G_v, q)\text{-rows})$ for the smaller graph $G_v = G_v^{ab} - ab$, with
  $e = ab$). If the $e$-block --- the per-edge panel-row span
  $\operatorname{span}\{\mathrm{panelRow}\,(e, \cdot, \cdot)\}$, of dimension $D - 1$ --- raises the
  finrank of $W$ by strictly fewer than $D - 1$,
  \[
    \dim\bigl(W + \operatorname{span}(e\text{-block})\bigr) \;<\; \dim W + (D - 1),
  \]
  then there is a $\mathrm{Fin}(D-1)$-indexed independent family $r$ of the $e$-block (its span is
  the whole block) with one redundant member: $r\,i_0 \in W + \operatorname{span}(r \restriction
  \{j \ne i_0\})$. This is KT's eq.~(6.23): with the corank gap fed by eq.~(6.18)
  (\cref{lem:case-III-seed-rank-bridge}, full rank of $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ at the $0$-dof $G_v^{ab}$) and
  eq.~(6.22) (\cref{lem:case-III-rank-attainment}, with $k' = \operatorname{def}(\tilde G_v) \le
  D - 2$ from \cref{lem:case-III-gap3-minimalKDof}), one of the $ab$-rows is redundant, so dropping
  it does not lower the rank.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  The $e$-block is the $\mathrm{hingeRow}$ image of the $(D-1)$-dimensional hinge-row block
  $r(p(e))$, so it is $(D-1)$-dimensional and is spanned by the $D - 1$ independent panel rows the
  transversal hinge contributes (\cref{lem:case-II-placement-new-rows}). The finrank hypothesis is
  exactly the abstract pigeonhole's input at this $\mathrm{Fin}(D-1)$-indexed family: a family
  raising $\dim W$ by fewer than its cardinality has a member lying in $W$ plus the span of the rest.
  That member is the redundant $ab$-row.
\end{proof}

  and selector $\mathrm{ends}$, with $e_0 = ab$ the deleted transversal hinge ($C(e_0) \ne 0$). Given
  the two rank attainments --- eq.~(6.18) $\dim \operatorname{span}(R(G_{ab}, q)\text{-rows}) =
  D(m-1)$ (the $0$-dof full rank, $m = |V(G_{ab})| = |V(G_v)|$) and eq.~(6.22)

\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok

\subsubsection{The candidate-completion: converting the redundant row into the
missing \texorpdfstring{$+1$}{+1} (KT eqs.\ (6.24)--(6.29))}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-candidate-completion}

Claim~6.11 (\cref{lem:case-III-claim-6-11}) gives a redundant $ab$-row of
$R(G_v^{ab}, q)$; the candidate-completion turns it into the full-rank $D$-th row
of the stratum-1 brick. KT's mechanism (eqs.~(6.24)--(6.29)) is a single fundamental
row operation \emph{followed by the eqs.~(6.14)--(6.15) column operation}: the redundant
row's linear combination, transported from $R(G_v^{ab}, q)$ up to $R(G, p_1)$ and then
column-reduced (add column $v$ to column $a$), produces a row $w$ of $R(G, p_1)$ whose
$V \setminus \{v\}$ part vanishes (eqs.~(6.27)--(6.28)). The eqs.~(6.24)--(6.25) decomposition
of the redundant row (\cref{lem:case-III-redundant-decomposition}, green), the per-edge seam
identity (\cref{lem:case-III-seam}, green), and the pure-$v$-column characterization of such a
row (\cref{lem:case-III-vanish-off-column}, green) are in place, as is the column operation $\Phi$
itself: a Phase~22e recon found it load-bearing for the vanishing --- the transported row
$w = \mathrm{hingeRow}\ v\ a\ \rho_g$ is supported on \emph{both} columns $v$ and $a$, so the column
op, not the seam alone, is what makes its $V \setminus \{v\}$ part zero --- and
\cref{lem:case-III-columnop} now supplies $\Phi$ (the $\mathrm{col}_a \mathrel{+}= \mathrm{col}_v$
change-of-variables automorphism) together with the fact that $w \circ \Phi$ is pure $v$-column.
The per-row eq.~(6.27) transport collapse (\cref{lem:case-III-transport-collapse}, green) closes the
core algebra: each transported $(vb)j$-row minus the inductive $(ab)j$-row is the pure $(va)$-hinge
row $\mathrm{hingeRow}\ v\ a\ (\mathrm{annihRow}\ C\ \cdot)$ (same supporting extensor by the shear
identity), and it packages the column-op composition certifying $w \circ \Phi$ pure $v$-column.
The combination-level threading is itself now green
(\cref{lem:case-III-candidate-row-construction}): the redundant row's common $G_v$-row element
$w_{\mathrm{Gv}}$ is an $ab$-block element, so it is $\mathrm{hingeRow}\ a\ b\ \rho$ for some
$\rho \in r(p(e_0))$, its transported $(vb)i^*$-row $\mathrm{hingeRow}\ v\ b\ \rho$ is a genuine
rigidity row of $R(G, p_1)$, and the collapse yields the candidate row $\mathrm{hingeRow}\ v\ a\ \rho$.
The full $D(|V|-1)$-family assembly is now in place (\cref{lem:case-III-candidate-row}, green): the
operated row $w \circ \Phi$ joins the stratum-1 brick's $va$-block and old block in the pin-a-body
augment (\cref{lem:case-III-conditional-block}), and the operated independence transports back
through the automorphism $\Phi$ to the original family of size $((D-1)+1)+D(|V_v|-1) = D(|V|-1)$.
The one \emph{conditional} it passes through --- the eq.~(6.29) top-left $D \times D$
full rank (\cref{lem:case-III-eq629-conditional}) --- is discharged by
Claim~6.12 (\cref{lem:case-III-claim612},
\cref{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-claim612}), so the candidate-completion assembly is
green. The
$a$-column-block vanishing (\cref{lem:case-III-acolumn-zero}, green) is a Claim~6.12 fact (the $M_3$
case), not the candidate-row input.

\begin{lemma}[A rigidity row vanishing off $v$'s column is a pure $v$-column row; KT eq.\ (6.28)]
  \label{lem:case-III-vanish-off-column}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.dualMap_eq_comp_single_proj_of_vanish_off}
  \leanok
  Let $f$ be a functional on the screw assignments $\alpha \to \mathrm{ScrewSpace}\,k$
  that vanishes on every assignment supported off a fixed body $v$ (i.e.\
  $S v = 0 \Rightarrow f S = 0$). Then $f$ factors through body $v$'s screw column:
  $f = (f \circ \mathrm{single}_v) \circ \mathrm{proj}_v$. This is KT eq.~(6.28)'s
  formal content: the candidate-completion's row operation (eqs.~(6.24)--(6.27))
  reduces the $(vb)i^*$-row of $R(G, p_1)$ to a row whose $V \setminus \{v\}$ part is
  all zero, and such a row is determined by its $v$-column block --- written
  $S = \mathrm{single}_v(S v) + (S - \mathrm{single}_v(S v))$, the second summand is
  supported off $v$, so $f$ kills it.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Split $S = \mathrm{single}_v(S v) + (S - \mathrm{single}_v(S v))$. The second
  summand reads $0$ at $v$, so $f$ kills it by hypothesis, leaving
  $f S = f(\mathrm{single}_v(S v)) = (f \circ \mathrm{single}_v)(S v)$, which is
  exactly $((f \circ \mathrm{single}_v) \circ \mathrm{proj}_v)\,S$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[The column operation $\Phi$: the candidate row is pure $v$-column in the operated frame; KT eqs.\ (6.14)--(6.16)]
  \label{lem:case-III-columnop}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.columnOp,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.hingeRow_comp_columnOp_apply,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.hingeRow_comp_columnOp_vanish_off}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-III-vanish-off-column}
  KT's column operation $\mathrm{col}_a \mathrel{+}= \mathrm{col}_v$ (eqs.~(6.14)--(6.15)), the
  device that makes the transported candidate row vanish off $v$. Since the rigidity rows read
  relative screws $S_u - S_w$, the operation is modelled by its dual substitution on the screw
  assignment $\alpha \to \mathrm{ScrewSpace}\,k$:
  $\Phi(S) = \mathrm{update}\ S\ v\ (S_v + S_a)$, a linear automorphism for $v \neq a$ (inverse
  $\Phi^{-1}(S) = \mathrm{update}\ S\ v\ (S_v - S_a)$). It is rank-preserving (precomposition with a
  linear equivalence), so it certifies the candidate row's vanishing without changing any rank. The
  transported candidate row $w = \mathrm{hingeRow}\ v\ a\ \rho$ is supported on \emph{both} columns
  $v$ and $a$, with $w(S) = \rho(S_v - S_a)$; precomposing with $\Phi$ reads $\Phi(S)$ as $S_v + S_a$
  at $v$ and $S_a$ at $a$ (using $v \neq a$), so
  \[ w(\Phi(S)) = \rho\bigl((S_v + S_a) - S_a\bigr) = \rho(S_v), \]
  the $a$-column contribution cancels. In particular $w(\Phi(S)) = 0$ whenever $S_v = 0$, which is
  exactly the off-$v$ vanishing hypothesis \cref{lem:case-III-vanish-off-column} consumes: $w \circ
  \Phi$ is a pure $v$-column row. This --- KT's eqs.~(6.14)--(6.16) column reduction --- is the
  vanishing mechanism, \emph{not} the per-edge seam plus eq.~(6.43).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  $\Phi$ is a linear equivalence by direct verification of additivity, homogeneity, and the two
  inverse identities (each a case split on whether the coordinate is $v$; the off-$v$ coordinates are

\end{proof}

\subsubsection{The \texorpdfstring{$D$}{D}-candidate disjunction (KT eqs.\ (6.30)--(6.45))}
\label{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-claim612}

The candidate-completion (\cref{sec:molecular-algebraic-induction-candidate-completion}) lifts a

q}(u)$). The argument is a clean contrapositive: if all three fail, a single nonzero $r \in \R^D$

the union~(6.45), which Lemma~2.1 forces to span all of $\R^D$, so $r = 0$ --- a contradiction.

\begin{lemma}[Point-join $\leftrightarrow$ panel-meet duality: a line in $\Pi(u)$ is annihilated by $r$ once $r \perp C(L)$; KT eq.\ (6.45) via Grassmann--Cayley]
  \label{lem:case-III-claim612-line-in-panel-union}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.complementIso_smul_eq_extensor_join,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.extensor_join_eq_zero_of_complementIso_eq_zero}
  \leanok
  \uses{def:join, def:meet, def:meet-complement-iso, def:panel-support-extensor,
        lem:extensor-independence, lem:case-III-claim612-extensor-span,
        lem:complement-iso-toDual, lem:complement-iso-decomposable-wedge-perp,
        lem:complement-iso-line-one-dim}
  At $d = 3$ ($D = 6$, $k = 2$, $\mathrm{ScrewSpace}\,2 = \bigwedge^2 \R^4$), let $p_i, p_j$ be two
  of the points of \cref{lem:case-III-claim612-points-affineIndep} whose connecting line
  $L = p_i p_j$ lies in a panel $\Pi(u)$ ($u \in \{a, b, c\}$). Then the point-join
  $\bar{p_i} \vee \bar{p_j} \in \bigwedge^2 \R^4$ and the panel-meet supporting extensor
  $C(L) = \mathrm{panelSupportExtensor}\ n_u\ (\cdot) = \mathrm{complementIso}(n_u \wedge \cdot)
  \in \bigwedge^2 \R^4$ of the same line are \emph{scalar multiples} of one another (both are the
  Pl\"ucker coordinate vector of the line $L$, up to the projective scale). Consequently a screw
  functional $r \in \mathrm{Module.Dual}\ \R\ (\mathrm{ScrewSpace}\,2)$ that annihilates
  \emph{every} panel-meet extensor $C(L)$ of lines $L \subset \Pi(a) \cup \Pi(b) \cup \Pi(c)$
  also annihilates each spanning join: $r(C(L)) = 0 \Rightarrow r(\bar{p_i} \vee \bar{p_j}) = 0$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Both $\bar{p_i} \vee \bar{p_j}$ and $C(L)$ are $2$-extensors in $\bigwedge^2 \R^4$
  representing the same projective line $L$. The join $\bar{p_i} \vee \bar{p_j}$
  (\cref{def:join}) is the line's supporting extensor written from \emph{two points on it}; the
  meet $C(L)$ (\cref{def:meet}, the complement iso \cref{def:meet-complement-iso} of the
  grade-$2$ join of two panel normals, \cref{def:panel-support-extensor}) is the same line's
  supporting extensor written from \emph{two hyperplanes through it}, and the Grassmann--Cayley
  duality of \cref{sec:molecular-meet} makes these two presentations of a $1$-dimensional
  projective subspace agree up to a nonzero scalar.

  We realize that common line concretely as the $1$-dimensional space
  \[
    \Omega := \{\, Z \in \textstyle\bigwedge^2 \R^4 \;:\; \langle Z, \phi\rangle_b = 0
      \text{ for all } \phi \in \widetilde\Phi \,\},
    \qquad
    \widetilde\Phi := (n_u \wedge \R^4) + (n' \wedge \R^4),
  \]
  the $\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle_b$-orthogonal complement of the shared-direction span, where
  $\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle_b$ is the coordinate (perfect) pairing of $\bigwedge^2 \R^4$ induced by
  the standard basis. The span $\widetilde\Phi$ is $5$-dimensional --- $\dim(n_u \wedge \R^4) +
  \dim(n' \wedge \R^4) - \dim(n_u \wedge \R^4 \cap n' \wedge \R^4) = 3 + 3 - 1$ by
  inclusion--exclusion, the intersection being the line $\mathrm{span}\{n_u \wedge n'\}$
  (\cref{lem:complement-iso-line-one-dim} supplies the exterior-square dimensions; the decomposable
  intersection is the genuinely new count). As $\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle_b$ is a perfect pairing on
  the $6$-dimensional $\bigwedge^2 \R^4$, $\dim \Omega = 6 - 5 = 1$.

  Both extensors lie in $\Omega$. The panel-meet $C(L) = \mathrm{complementIso}(n_u \wedge n')$ does
  by step (i) (\cref{lem:complement-iso-decomposable-wedge-perp}): through the dictionary entry
  \cref{lem:complement-iso-toDual}, a wedge with a shared factor has vanishing complement pairing, so
  $\mathrm{complementIso}(n_u \wedge n')$ is annihilated by every $2$-extensor sharing a vector with
  $n_u \wedge n'$ --- in particular by each $n_u \wedge v$ (sharing $n_u$ directly) and each
  $n' \wedge v$ (the appended family repeats $n'$), which together span $\widetilde\Phi$. The
  point-join $\bar{p_i} \vee \bar{p_j}$ does too: under $\langle\cdot,\cdot\rangle_b$ the pairing
  $\langle \bar{p_i} \vee \bar{p_j},\, n_u \wedge v\rangle_b$ is the Gram determinant of the dot
  products $\langle \bar{p}, n_u\rangle$, $\langle \bar{p}, v\rangle$, whose row of incidences
  $\langle \bar{p_i}, n_u\rangle = \langle \bar{p_j}, n_u\rangle = 0$ vanishes (likewise for $n'$).
  Since $C(L) \ne 0$ precisely because $L$ is a genuine line ($\{n_u, n'\}$ independent), the two
  members of the line $\Omega$ are scalar multiples: $\bar{p_i} \vee \bar{p_j} = \lambda\, C(L)$
  (\cref{lem:complement-iso-line-one-dim}). The annihilation transfer is then immediate:
  $r(C(L)) = 0$ gives $r(\bar{p_i} \vee \bar{p_j}) = \lambda\, r(C(L)) = 0$. This is the
  duality KT use implicitly when reading eq.~(6.45): the spanning family of
  \cref{lem:case-III-claim612-extensor-span} (point-joins) and the annihilated family of the
  contrapositive (panel-meets) are the \emph{same} extensors of the lines in the panel union.
\end{proof}

  (\operatorname{span} C)^\perp$ --- the row-space criterion for extending an independent family by

\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Eq.\ (6.44): the $M_3$ row equals $-r$, routing $M_3$ onto the same $r$; KT eq.\ (6.44)]
  \label{lem:case-III-claim612-eq644}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.candidateRow_ac_eq_neg,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.hingeRow_comp_single_tail,
        CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.hingeRow_comp_single_off}
  \leanok
  \uses{lem:case-III-acolumn-zero, lem:case-III-redundant-decomposition}
  In $G_v^{ab}$ only $ab$ and $ac$ are incident to the body $a$, so the $a$-column block of the
  eq.~(6.24) vanishing combination (\cref{lem:case-III-acolumn-zero}, eq.~(6.43)) reads
  \[
    0 = \sum_{1 \le j \le 5} \lambda_{(ab)j}\, r_j(q(ab)) + \sum_{1 \le j \le 5} \lambda_{(ac)j}\,
        r_j(q(ac)),
  \]
  which, with $r = \sum_j \lambda_{(ab)j}\, r_j(q(ab))$, gives KT's eq.~(6.44):
  \[
    r = -\sum_{1 \le j \le 5} \lambda_{(ac)j}\, r_j(q(ac)).
  \]
  Hence the $M_3$ candidate row $\sum_j \lambda_{(ac)j}\, r_j(q(ac))$ equals $-r$, routing the
  third candidate's full-rank criterion onto the \emph{same} common vector $r$ as $M_1, M_2$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  \leanok
  Eq.~(6.43) (\cref{lem:case-III-acolumn-zero}) is the $a$-column restriction of the eq.~(6.24)
  vanishing combination; since only the $ab$- and $ac$-edges are incident to $a$ in $G_v^{ab}$
  (every other $R(G_v^{ab}, q; e_j, a) = 0$ by the rigidity-matrix definition --- the
  column-restriction leaves $\mathrm{hingeRow\_comp\_single\_tail}$ for the $ab$/$ac$-rows and
  $\mathrm{hingeRow\_comp\_single\_off}$ for every other edge), the surviving terms are exactly
  the $ab$- and $ac$-sums. Rearranging $\sum_j \lambda_{(ab)j}\, r_j(q(ab)) + \sum_j
  \lambda_{(ac)j}\, r_j(q(ac)) = 0$ for $r$ is eq.~(6.44).
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Claim 6.12: at least one of $M_1, M_2, M_3$ has full rank; KT Claim 6.12, eqs.\ (6.30)--(6.45)]
  \label{lem:case-III-claim612}
  \lean{CombinatorialRigidity.Molecular.BodyHingeFramework.case_III_claim612}
  \uses{lem:case-III-claim612-extensor-span, lem:case-III-claim612-orthseq-vanish,
        lem:case-III-claim612-points-affineIndep, lem:case-III-claim612-line-in-panel-union,
        lem:case-III-claim612-block-iff-perp,
        lem:case-III-claim612-r-nonzero, lem:case-III-claim612-p2-placement,
        lem:case-III-claim612-p3-placement, lem:case-III-claim612-eq644}
  At $d = 3$, for the three candidate placements $p_1, p_2, p_3$
  (\cref{lem:case-II-realization-placement}, \cref{lem:case-III-claim612-p2-placement},
  \cref{lem:case-III-claim612-p3-placement}), at least one of the top-left $D \times D$ submatrices
  $M_1, M_2, M_3$ (of eqs.~(6.29), (6.30), (6.41)) has full rank, for some choice of
  $(d-2)$-affine lines $L \subset \Pi(a)$, $L' \subset \Pi(b)$, $L'' \subset \Pi(c)$. Equivalently,
  at least one of $R(G, p_1), R(G, p_2), R(G, p_3)$ attains the desired rank $D(|V| - 1)$, discharging
  the candidate-completion conditional (\cref{lem:case-III-eq629-conditional}).
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
  Contrapositive. Suppose all three blocks fail to have full rank for \emph{every} choice of lines.
  By the row-space criterion (\cref{lem:case-III-claim612-block-iff-perp}), $M_1$ failing places the
  common vector $r := \sum_j \lambda_{(ab)j}\, r_j(q(ab))$ in $(\operatorname{span} C(L))^\perp$ for
  all $L \subset \Pi(a)$; likewise $M_2$ failing puts $r \in (\operatorname{span} C(L'))^\perp$ for
  all $L' \subset \Pi(b)$. For $M_3$, eq.~(6.44) (\cref{lem:case-III-claim612-eq644}) makes its
  candidate row $\sum_j \lambda_{(ac)j}\, r_j(q(ac)) = -r$, so $M_3$ failing puts the \emph{same} $r$
  in $(\operatorname{span} C(L''))^\perp$ for all $L'' \subset \Pi(c)$. Hence $r$ is orthogonal to
  every panel-meet supporting extensor $C(L)$ in the union~(6.45),
  $\bigl(\bigcup_{L \subset \Pi(a)} C(L)\bigr) \cup \bigl(\bigcup_{L' \subset \Pi(b)} C(L')\bigr)
  \cup \bigl(\bigcup_{L'' \subset \Pi(c)} C(L'')\bigr)$. Take the four affinely-independent points
  $p_1, p_2, p_3, p_4$ of \cref{lem:case-III-claim612-points-affineIndep}: each connecting line
  $p_i p_j$ lies in $\Pi(a) \cup \Pi(b) \cup \Pi(c)$, so by the point-join $\leftrightarrow$
  panel-meet duality (\cref{lem:case-III-claim612-line-in-panel-union}) the join $p_i \vee p_j$ is a
  scalar multiple of that line's panel-meet $C(L)$, hence $r$ annihilates each $p_i \vee p_j$ too.
  But the six joins span $\R^D$ (\cref{lem:case-III-claim612-extensor-span}, Lemma~2.1 on the four
  affinely-independent points). So $r$ annihilates a spanning set and $r = 0$
  (\cref{lem:case-III-claim612-orthseq-vanish}), contradicting $r \ne 0$
  (\cref{lem:case-III-claim612-r-nonzero}). Therefore at least one block has full rank.

  That the disjunction must range over a free choice of lines, not three fixed supports, is
  essential: three $2$-extensors $C_1, C_2, C_3$ span at most $3$ of the $6$ dimensions of
  $\bigwedge^2 \R^4$, so $r \perp C_1, C_2, C_3$ alone cannot force $r = 0$; KT's contrapositive
  sweeps \emph{every} line $L \subset \Pi(u)$, reaching the full six-join span~(6.45).
\end{proof}
